## 📌 Laryngeal Cancer Overview in NCCN Guidelines (v2.2023)

### 1. Anatomical Subsites Covered
- **Glottic Larynx**
- **Supraglottic Larynx**

### 2. Workup (Common to Both)
- **History & Physical (H&P)** including complete head and neck exam, mirror/fiberoptic exam.
- **Biopsy** of primary site or **FNA** of neck nodes.
- **Imaging**: CT with contrast ± MRI of primary and neck; chest CT; consider FDG-PET/CT.
- **Endoscopy under anesthesia (EUA)**.
- **Preanesthesia studies**, pulmonary function tests (for conservation surgery candidates).
- **Multidisciplinary consultation**.
- **Supportive evaluations**: dental, nutrition, speech/swallowing, audiogram, smoking cessation, fertility counseling.
### 3. Clinical Staging and Treatment Pathways

#### A. Glottic Larynx (GLOT)
- **Carcinoma in situ**: Endoscopic resection (preferred) or RT.
- **T1–T2, N0**: RT or partial laryngectomy ± neck dissection.
- **T3, N0–1**: Options include:
  - Concurrent systemic therapy/RT
  - Surgery (laryngectomy + neck dissection ± thyroidectomy)
  - Induction chemotherapy (category 3)
- **T4a, N0–3**: Surgery (laryngectomy + neck dissection + thyroidectomy) or concurrent systemic therapy/RT.
- **Selected T4a patients who decline surgery**: Consider concurrent systemic therapy/RT or clinical trial.
#### B. Supraglottic Larynx (SUPRA)
- **T1–T2, N0**: RT or partial supraglottic laryngectomy + neck dissection.
- **T3, N0**: Concurrent systemic therapy/RT or laryngectomy + neck dissection.
- **T3, N1–N3**: Concurrent systemic therapy/RT or surgery ± adjuvant therapy.
- **T4a, N0–N3**: Laryngectomy + neck dissection or concurrent systemic therapy/RT.
- **T4b or unresectable**: See Very Advanced Head and Neck Cancer (ADV-1).
### 4. Adjuvant Treatment Based on Pathology
- **No adverse features**: Observation.
- **Positive margins**: Re-resection or RT.
- **Extranodal extension (ENE)**: Systemic therapy/RT (category 1).
- **Other risk features**: RT ± systemic therapy.

**Adverse pathologic features** include:
- ENE
- Positive/close margins
- pT3/T4 primary
- pN2/N3 nodal disease
- Perineural, vascular, or lymphatic invasion
- Subglottic extension (for glottic cancer)
### 5. Principles of Radiation Therapy (RT)

#### A. Definitive RT 
- **Dose**: 66–70 Gy (2.0–2.2 Gy/fx) for high-risk volumes.
- **Techniques**: IMRT, 3D-CRT, or proton therapy in selected cases.
- **Altered fractionation** options: concomitant boost, hyperfractionation.
#### B. Postoperative RT
- **Dose**: 60–66 Gy for high-risk features.
- **Interval**: ≤6 weeks post-surgery.
#### C. Concurrent Systemic Therapy/RT
- Preferred agent: **cisplatin** (category 1).
- Alternative agents for cisplatin-ineligible patients: carboplatin, cetuximab.
### 6. Systemic Therapy Principles (SYST-A)
- **Concurrent with RT**: Cisplatin preferred.
- **Induction chemotherapy**: Category 3 recommendation (docetaxel/cisplatin/5-FU or similar).
- **Recurrent/metastatic disease**:
  - Pembrolizumab (if PD-L1 CPS ≥1)
  - Cetuximab ± chemotherapy
  - Nivolumab, chemotherapy combinations
### 7. Special Considerations
- **Larynx preservation** is emphasized where possible.
- **Thyroidectomy** is recommended for T4 or N2–3 diseases to clear central compartment nodes.
- **Avoid triple modality treatment** when possible to reduce toxicity.
- **HPV testing** is not routinely recommended for laryngeal cancers.
### 8. Follow-Up and Surveillance (FOLL-A)
- **Clinical exam** every 1–3 months for Year 1, every 2–6 months for Year 2, etc.
- **Imaging**: CT/MRI at 3–6 months post-treatment, then as clinically indicated.
- **FDG-PET/CT** at 12 weeks post-treatment for response assessment.
### 9. Recurrent/Persistent Disease
- **Resectable**: Surgery ± reirradiation.
- **Unresectable**: Reirradiation ± systemic therapy, clinical trial, or palliative care.
- **Distant metastases**: Systemic therapy per recurrent/metastatic protocols.
### 10. Key Updates in v2.2023 Relevant to Larynx
- Added **pN1 without other risk features** as a category where **consider RT** is an option.
- Clarified **thyroidectomy and central neck dissection** indications for advanced disease.
- Emphasis on **minimizing treatment modalities** to reduce toxicity.
