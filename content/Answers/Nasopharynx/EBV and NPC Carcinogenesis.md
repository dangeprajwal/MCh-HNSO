## EBV: Virology and Biology

### Taxonomy and Structure
- **Full name:** Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV); also designated **Human Herpesvirus 4 (HHV-4)**
- **Family:** Herpesviridae, subfamily Gammaherpesvirinae, genus Lymphocryptovirus
- **Genome:** Double-stranded DNA (dsDNA), ~172 kb; enclosed in icosahedral capsid; tegument layer; lipid envelope with glycoproteins (gp350/220 — binds CD21 on B cells)
- **Replication:** Lytic cycle (virion production) vs latent cycle (episomal persistence); NPC cells maintain exclusively latent infection

### Epidemiology
- **Ubiquitous:** >90% seroprevalence worldwide (primary infection in childhood, usually subclinical)
- In adolescents and adults: primary EBV infection → infectious mononucleosis (fever, pharyngitis, lymphadenopathy)
- Establishes **lifelong latency** in memory B lymphocytes
- Normal immune system suppresses viral reactivation; immunosuppression → viral reactivation, EBV-associated malignancies

## EBV Latency Programmes

### Three Forms of Latency

| Latency Type | Gene Expression Pattern | Associated Malignancy |
|---|---|---|
| **Type I (Latency I)** | EBER, EBNA1, BART miRNAs | Burkitt lymphoma |
| **Type II (Latency II)** | EBER, EBNA1, LMP1, LMP2A/B, BART miRNAs | **NPC**, Hodgkin lymphoma, T/NK-cell lymphoma |
| **Type III (Latency III)** | All EBNAs (1–6), LMPs, EBERs | Post-transplant lymphoproliferative disease (PTLD), EBV+ DLBCL |

> **NPC uses Type II latency** — a critically examined fact. Key gene products expressed:

### Key EBV Gene Products in NPC

#### EBERs (EBV-encoded small RNAs: EBER1 and EBER2)
- Non-polyadenylated, non-translated small nuclear RNAs
- Present in **virtually all EBV-infected cells** regardless of latency type
- Functions: block apoptosis (via PKR inhibition), stimulate IL-10 production, promote growth
- **EBER in-situ hybridisation (EBER-ISH)** is the gold standard diagnostic marker for EBV in tissue — more sensitive and specific than LMP1 IHC

#### EBNA1 (EBV Nuclear Antigen 1)
- Only EBNA expressed in all latency types
- Essential for: episomal replication during cell division (ori-P recognition), maintenance of viral latency
- **Glycine-alanine repeat domain** makes EBNA1 resistant to ubiquitin-proteasome degradation → evades CD8+ T-cell killing
- Not directly oncogenic; maintains viral persistence

#### LMP1 (Latent Membrane Protein 1) — The Key Oncogene
- Expressed in ~60% of NPC tumours (higher in Type II latency)
- Functions as a **constitutively active pseudo-CD40** (ligand-independent signalling)
- **Activates multiple oncogenic pathways:**
  - **NF-κB** → anti-apoptosis (upregulates BCL-2, BCL-xL), inflammation, invasion
  - **PI3K/AKT** → cell survival, mTOR activation
  - **JAK/STAT** → proliferation
  - **ERK/MAPK** → proliferation
- Downregulates E-cadherin → promotes epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) and invasion
- Upregulates VEGF → angiogenesis
- **Net effect:** LMP1 is the primary transforming protein in EBV-associated NPC

#### LMP2A and LMP2B
- LMP2A: mimics constitutively active B-cell receptor (BCR) signalling → prevents B-cell activation/differentiation, promotes viral latency
- LMP2B: modulates LMP2A activity; less studied
- Expressed in NPC; role in carcinogenesis less clear than LMP1

#### BART miRNAs (BamHI-A Rightward Transcript microRNAs)
- Cluster of ~40 EBV-encoded miRNAs; highly expressed in NPC
- **Oncogenic functions:**
  - miR-BART5: downregulates **PUMA** (p53-upregulated modulator of apoptosis) → anti-apoptosis
  - miR-BART-cluster 1/2: downregulate **TAPBP** (TAP-binding protein) → immune evasion (reduced MHC-I presentation)
  - miR-BART6: targets **PTEN** → PI3K/AKT activation
  - miR-BART7: downregulates **SMAD2** → TGF-β pathway evasion

## Multi-Step Carcinogenesis in NPC

### Sequential Model of NPC Development

```
Step 1: Pre-existing genetic susceptibility
         ↓ HLA-A2, HLA-B46 (risk alleles)
         ↓ Epigenetic silencing of tumour suppressors (CDH1, RASSF1A, p16/CDKN2A)

Step 2: EBV infection of nasopharyngeal epithelial cells
         ↓ EBER / EBNA1 expression
         ↓ Evasion of apoptosis; clonal expansion of infected epithelial cells

Step 3: LMP1 expression → oncogenic signalling
         ↓ NF-κB, PI3K/AKT activation
         ↓ Anti-apoptosis, proliferation, EMT

Step 4: Additional somatic mutations (late events)
         ↓ CDKN2A (p16) deletion / methylation
         ↓ TP53 mutation (less common than in keratinizing NPC)
         ↓ PIK3CA mutations
         ↓ EGFR amplification (subsets)

Step 5: Invasive carcinoma → metastasis
         ↓ LMP1-driven VEGF, MMP upregulation
         ↓ BART miRNA-mediated immune evasion
```

### Epigenetic Silencing (Key Early Event)
- **CDH1** (E-cadherin): silenced by promoter methylation in >80% NPC → loss of cell adhesion, EMT
- **RASSF1A** (tumour suppressor): methylation-silenced → cell cycle dysregulation
- **p16/CDKN2A**: homozygous deletion or methylation → loss of cell cycle control (CDK4/6 unrestrained → pRb phosphorylation → S-phase entry)
- These epigenetic changes precede EBV infection in some models (EBV infects pre-primed epithelial cells)

## Geographic Epidemiology of NPC

### Incidence by Region

| Population | Age-Standardised Incidence (per 100,000 per year) |
|---|---|
| Southern Chinese (Cantonese) | 25–50 (males); 10–15 (females) |
| Hong Kong / Guangdong province | 20–30 |
| Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Singapore — Chinese ethnicity) | 15–25 |
| North Africa (Algeria, Tunisia) | 8–12 |
| Arctic Inuit (Greenland, Alaska, Canada) | 10–20 |
| Western Europe / North America | <1–2 |
| Indian subcontinent | 1–2 |

### Risk Factors

#### Genetic
- **HLA alleles:** HLA-A2, HLA-B46, HLA-B58 (risk); HLA-A11 (protective)
- Family history of NPC (first-degree relatives: RR 5–10×)
- GWAS-identified loci: 6p21.3 (HLA region), 3p21.3 (RASSF1A), others

#### Environmental
- **Salted fish (Cantonese-style):** N-nitrosamines (nitrosodimethylamine) — most consistently implicated; introduced early in life correlates with highest risk
- **Incense/herbal smoke:** Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons
- **Formaldehyde exposure** (occupational)
- **Cigarette smoking:** Modest risk increase (RR ~2), especially for keratinizing type
- **Inadequate ventilation / indoor air pollution**

#### Migration Studies (Key Evidence for Environmental Component)
- Chinese immigrants to North America retain elevated NPC risk (intermediate between endemic homeland and host country risk)
- Second-generation Chinese-Americans approach host country risk level
- **Interpretation:** Combination of genetic susceptibility + early-life environmental exposure during critical period; environmental factors alone insufficient

## EBV Biomarkers in Clinical Practice

### 1. Plasma Cell-Free EBV DNA

**Mechanism:** Apoptotic/necrotic NPC tumour cells release fragmented viral DNA into circulation; detected by quantitative PCR targeting *BamHI-W* or *EBNA1* regions

| Clinical Use | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| **Pre-treatment staging** | >1500 copies/mL correlates with higher stage; >10,000 = high metastatic risk |
| **Treatment response** | Undetectable at 6–8 weeks post-RT = complete response; persistent = residual disease |
| **Post-treatment surveillance** | Rising EBV DNA precedes clinical relapse by 2–4 months |
| **Prognosis** | Independently predicts OS, FFS (stronger than T/N stage in some analyses) |

**Lin et al. 2004 (NEJM) key finding:** Pre-treatment EBV DNA >1500 copies/mL → OS HR 6.0 (p<0.001); post-treatment detectable EBV DNA → relapse RR >10

### 2. IgA Anti-VCA (Viral Capsid Antigen) Serology
- **Traditional screening marker** for NPC (especially in endemic areas)
- Elevated in 95% of NPC patients; precedes clinical presentation
- Used in mass screening programmes (Guangdong province screening programs)
- **Limitation:** 10–15% false-positive rate; low sensitivity for T1N0; does not reflect disease burden
- Sensitivity ~78%, specificity ~92% for symptomatic NPC; lower for early-stage screening

### 3. IgA Anti-EA (Early Antigen)
- Elevated in active EBV infection / NPC
- Less specific than anti-VCA; used in combination panels

### 4. EBER-ISH (Tissue Biomarker)
- **Gold standard for histological confirmation of EBV in NPC**
- Performed on paraffin sections
- EBER nuclear signal in tumour cells = EBV-positive (seen in non-keratinizing and undifferentiated NPC; absent in keratinizing)
- More sensitive than LMP1 IHC (LMP1 only in ~60%)

## AJCC 8th Edition: NPC-Specific Changes Related to EBV Biology

- AJCC 8th designed to stage **endemic (non-keratinizing, EBV-associated) NPC** — not suitable for keratinizing NPC in non-endemic populations
- Key change: **parapharyngeal extension = T2** (was T1 in 7th) — recognises worse prognosis despite seemingly limited spread
- **Rationale:** Data from endemic cohorts (predominantly UCNT/EBV+) showed T2 parapharyngeal extension had intermediate prognosis between T1 and older T3, meriting upstaging
- N staging: **bilateral retropharyngeal or unilateral cervical nodes ≤6 cm = N1** (not N2 as in some other H&N subsites), reflecting the unique bilateral lymphatic drainage pattern driven by NPC's midline location

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> EBV (HHV-4) establishes **Type II latency** in NPC cells, expressing EBER1/2, EBNA1, LMP1, LMP2A/B, and BART miRNAs. **LMP1 is the primary oncogene** — it acts as a constitutively active pseudo-CD40, activating NF-κB, PI3K/AKT, and JAK/STAT pathways to drive anti-apoptosis, proliferation, and invasion. **EBER-ISH** is the gold standard tissue marker. **Plasma EBV DNA** (cell-free) is the most important clinical biomarker: pre-treatment levels predict stage and prognosis; undetectable levels post-RT confirm complete response; rising levels during follow-up predict relapse 2–4 months before clinical evidence. NPC is geographically endemic in Southern China (Cantonese), Southeast Asia, and Arctic Inuit populations — due to a combination of genetic susceptibility (HLA-A2/B46) and environmental co-factors (salted fish / N-nitrosamines). AJCC 8th edition specifically captures EBV-associated (non-keratinizing) NPC, with parapharyngeal extension upgraded to T2.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. EBV establishes **Type II latency** in NPC (not Type I as in Burkitt lymphoma, not Type III as in PTLD) — this distinction is frequently tested.
> 2. **LMP1** is the most important EBV oncogene in NPC — it mimics activated CD40, activating NF-κB; ~60% of NPC tumours express LMP1.
> 3. **EBER-ISH** is the gold standard for tissue EBV detection — more sensitive than LMP1 IHC; positive in virtually all non-keratinizing NPC.
> 4. Keratinizing SCC (WHO Type 1) NPC is NOT EBV-associated — the EBV association is exclusive to non-keratinizing and undifferentiated (WHO Type 2/3) subtypes.
> 5. **N-nitrosamines** from Cantonese-style salted fish are the most consistently implicated environmental carcinogen — early childhood exposure carries the highest risk.
> 6. **HLA-A2 and HLA-B46** confer susceptibility to NPC in Chinese populations; HLA-A11 is protective.
> 7. Plasma EBV DNA >10,000 copies/mL pre-treatment indicates high metastatic burden — consider upfront systemic imaging (PET-CT) and possible stage IVB.
> 8. Migration studies confirm both genetic and environmental components: Chinese immigrants retain intermediate risk, second-generation approaches host-country rates.
> 9. BART miRNAs (miR-BART5, miR-BART-cluster 1/2) are highly expressed in NPC and mediate immune evasion by downregulating TAPBP and PUMA — important for understanding immune therapy resistance.
> 10. The **EBNA1 glycine-alanine repeat domain** specifically evades CD8+ T-cell recognition by proteasome resistance — this is EBV's primary mechanism for immune evasion in NPC cells.
