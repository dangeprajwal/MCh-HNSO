## WHO 2017 Classification of NPC

### Three Histological Types

| WHO Type | Histology | EBV Association | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Type 1: Keratinizing SCC | Intercellular bridges, keratin pearls | Rare / absent | 10–25% (endemic), >50% (non-endemic) |
| Type 2: Non-keratinizing, differentiated | Stratified growth, no keratinization | Strong | 15–20% |
| Type 3: Non-keratinizing, undifferentiated (UCNT) | Syncytial sheets, lymphocytic stroma, vesicular nuclei | Very strong (virtually 100%) | 60–70% in endemic areas |
| Basaloid SCC | Peripheral palisading, comedo-necrosis | Variable | Rare (<5%) |

> **Key point:** The undifferentiated carcinoma (UCNT / WHO Type 3) is the most common type in endemic populations (Southeast Asia, Southern China) and carries the strongest EBV association. It is also the most radiosensitive — hence RT is the definitive treatment.

### Lymphoepithelioma (Regaud vs Schmincke)
- **Regaud type:** Cohesive nests of carcinoma cells separated by lymphoid stroma
- **Schmincke type:** Single cells diffusely interspersed with lymphocytes
- Both are variants of UCNT (WHO Type 3); distinction has no clinical significance

## AJCC 8th Edition Staging: Nasopharynx

> NPC uses a **separate, dedicated staging system** in AJCC 8th edition, distinct from other H&N subsites. Key change from 7th edition: parapharyngeal extension is now **T2** (was T1 in 7th).

### T Staging

| Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| T1 | Limited to nasopharynx, OR extension to oropharynx and/or nasal cavity WITHOUT parapharyngeal involvement |
| T2 | Extension to parapharyngeal space AND/OR medial pterygoid muscle, lateral pterygoid muscle, or prevertebral muscles |
| T3 | Infiltration of bony structures at skull base, pterygoid structures (pterygoid plates), and/or paranasal sinuses |
| T4 | Intracranial extension, cranial nerve involvement, hypopharynx, orbit, parotid gland, or extensive soft tissue infiltration beyond the lateral surface of the lateral pterygoid muscle |

### N Staging

| Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| N0 | No regional lymph node metastasis |
| N1 | Unilateral cervical nodes ≤6 cm above the caudal border of cricoid cartilage, AND/OR unilateral or bilateral retropharyngeal nodes ≤6 cm |
| N2 | Bilateral cervical nodes ≤6 cm above the caudal border of cricoid cartilage |
| N3 | Unilateral or bilateral cervical nodes >6 cm, OR extension to supraclavicular fossa |

> **Note:** "Supraclavicular fossa" in NPC staging = Ho's triangle: the region bounded by the superior margin of clavicle, superior margin of the sternal end of the clavicle, and apex by the junction of neck and shoulder. N3 regardless of size if supraclavicular fossa involved.

### Overall Stage Groups

| Stage | T | N | M |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stage I | T1 | N0 | M0 |
| Stage II | T1 | N1 \| T2 | N0–N1 | M0 |
| Stage III | T1–2 | N2 \| T3 | N0–N2 | M0 |
| Stage IVA | T4 \| Any T | N3 | M0 |
| Stage IVB | Any T | Any N | M1 |

## Treatment by Stage

### Stage I (T1N0M0)
- **Radiotherapy alone** is standard
- Primary dose: **70 Gy in 33–35 fractions** to gross tumour volume (GTV)
- Elective nodal irradiation: 50–56 Gy to bilateral retropharyngeal nodes, levels II–V bilaterally
- IMRT is the technique of choice (see [[Answers/Nasopharynx/Radiotherapy Advances in NPC|Radiotherapy Advances in NPC]])
- Concurrent chemotherapy not indicated for T1N0; adds toxicity without OS benefit

### Stage II (T1N1 or T2 any N)
- **Concurrent chemoradiation** (CRT)
- Cisplatin 40 mg/m² weekly × 6–7 cycles OR 100 mg/m² every 3 weeks × 3 cycles
- Evidence: subgroup analyses of INT 0099 and subsequent Asian trials (Chen et al. NPC-0501) support CRT for Stage II
- Adjuvant chemotherapy after CRT: benefit debated; NPC-0501 trial showed no benefit for adjuvant CF; some guidelines omit it for Stage II

### Stage III–IVA (Locoregional Advanced)
- **Concurrent CRT + induction chemotherapy** (current preferred approach)
- Induction options:
  - **GP (gemcitabine 1000 mg/m² D1/8 + cisplatin 80 mg/m² D1)** × 3 cycles → CRT (Zhang 2019, NEJM)
  - **TPF (docetaxel + cisplatin + 5-FU)** × 3 cycles → CRT (Sun 2016, NEJM)
- CRT: cisplatin 100 mg/m² every 3 weeks × 3 or weekly 40 mg/m² × 6–7
- Adjuvant chemotherapy: NPC-0501 trial failed to show benefit; largely abandoned in favour of induction
- Radiotherapy dose: 70 Gy to primary + involved nodes; 60–63 Gy to high-risk CTV; 50–56 Gy elective

### Stage IVB (Distant Metastases)
- **Systemic chemotherapy** is primary treatment
- First-line: GP regimen (gemcitabine + cisplatin)
- Addition of pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-789, 2023): GP + pembrolizumab vs GP alone; improved PFS (HR 0.51, p<0.001) — emerging standard
- Locoregional RT may be offered for oligometastatic disease or symptom control
- See [[Answers/Nasopharynx/Recurrent NPC Management|Recurrent NPC Management]] for salvage options

## Role of Plasma EBV DNA in NPC

### Biological Basis
- NPC cells (virtually all non-keratinizing types) harbour EBV episomes
- Tumour cell necrosis/apoptosis releases cell-free viral DNA into plasma
- Quantitative PCR (targeting *BamHI-W* region) measures copies/mL
- **Normal:** undetectable or <0 copies/mL in healthy individuals

### Clinical Applications

#### 1. Pre-treatment Staging
- EBV DNA >10,000 copies/mL correlates with higher T and N stage, higher risk of distant metastases
- May upstage patients for treatment planning even when imaging appears lower stage
- Incorporated in some proposed staging revisions (8th edition does not formally include it, but UICC/AJCC guidelines acknowledge its prognostic value)

#### 2. Response Assessment
- Measured at 6–8 weeks post-treatment
- **Undetectable EBV DNA** = complete serological response (correlates with complete pathological response)
- **Persistent EBV DNA** at end of treatment = residual disease / increased risk of relapse → intensified surveillance or early salvage

#### 3. Surveillance (Post-treatment)
- Measured every 3 months for 2 years, then every 6 months
- Rising plasma EBV DNA (confirmed on repeat sampling) = harbinger of recurrence, often precedes radiological evidence by 2–4 months
- Chan et al. (1999): detectable post-treatment EBV DNA → relapse-free survival HR ~5.0 vs undetectable

#### 4. Prognostic Stratification
- Pre-treatment EBV DNA is an independent prognostic factor for OS and failure-free survival (stronger than T or N alone in several studies)
- Being incorporated in prospective risk-stratification trials (e.g., HKNPCSG trials using EBV DNA to select patients for intensified induction)

## Neck Management

### Principle
- NPC is highly radiosensitive; neck surgery is NOT performed upfront
- **Bilateral elective neck irradiation** is mandatory for all patients (high risk of bilateral nodal spread via Rouvière's node and retropharyngeal nodes)
- Elective levels: II, III, IV, Vb bilaterally; ipsilateral Ib for N+ or oropharyngeal extension

### Residual Neck Disease Post-CRT
- PET-CT at 10–12 weeks post-CRT: if PET-positive residual nodes → proceed to neck dissection
- If PET-negative: observe (false-positive PET common early post-RT)
- Evidence: NEJM PET-NECK trial (for HNSCC broadly); NPC-specific data supports PET-guided approach

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> NPC staging in AJCC 8th edition is unique: **parapharyngeal extension = T2**, not T1 as in prior editions; **T4 includes intracranial extension and cranial nerve involvement**; N3 includes supraclavicular fossa regardless of size. **Concurrent CRT** (cisplatin-based) is the backbone of treatment for all stages beyond T1N0. For locoregionally advanced disease (Stage III–IVA), **induction chemotherapy (GP or TPF) followed by CRT** is the current preferred approach based on two landmark NEJM trials (Sun 2016 — TPF; Zhang 2019 — GP). **Plasma EBV DNA** is the most important biomarker in NPC: used for staging, monitoring, and surveillance — rising post-treatment EBV DNA predicts recurrence months before clinical or radiological evidence.

---

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. WHO Type 3 (UCNT/undifferentiated) is the most common NPC subtype in endemic areas and virtually 100% EBV-positive; keratinizing SCC is the least radiosensitive and least EBV-associated.
> 2. The single most important AJCC 8th edition change for NPC: parapharyngeal extension is now **T2** (upgraded from T1 in 7th edition), reflecting its worse prognosis.
> 3. For T1N0 — RT alone is appropriate; adding concurrent cisplatin adds toxicity without benefit.
> 4. INT 0099 (Al-Sarraf 1998) established concurrent CRT as standard; Zhang 2019 (NEJM, GP induction) is the more recent landmark supporting induction before CRT for Stage III–IVA.
> 5. N3 in NPC is defined by **size >6 cm OR supraclavicular fossa extension** — this is the most common MCh exam trap regarding nodal staging.
> 6. Plasma EBV DNA should be checked at diagnosis, end of treatment, and every 3 months for 2 years post-treatment — undetectable at end of RT = complete response; rising = early recurrence.
> 7. GP (gemcitabine-cisplatin) is the standard first-line regimen for metastatic/recurrent NPC, with ORR ~64%; addition of pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-789) improves PFS.
> 8. Elective bilateral neck irradiation is mandatory in all NPC — retropharyngeal nodes (Rouvière's node) are the first-echelon nodes and must be included in all RT fields.
> 9. NPC is one of the most radiosensitive carcinomas — surgery has no role at the primary site for newly diagnosed disease; RT ± CRT is definitive.
> 10. For residual neck disease post-CRT: perform PET-CT at 10–12 weeks; proceed to salvage neck dissection only if PET-positive.
