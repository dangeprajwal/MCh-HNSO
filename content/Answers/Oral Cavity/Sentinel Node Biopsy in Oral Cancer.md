## Introduction

Sentinel node biopsy (SNB) identifies and removes the first-echelon lymph node(s) draining a primary tumour. If the sentinel node (SN) is negative for metastasis, the remainder of the nodal basin is assumed to be negative, sparing the patient an elective neck dissection. Long established in [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Melanoma of the Oral Cavity|oral melanoma]] and breast cancer, SNB has now achieved Level I evidence for early oral cavity squamous cell carcinoma (T1-T2, cN0), following the landmark Senti-MERORL RCT demonstrating equivalence to END.

## Indications

- **T1-T2 oral cavity SCC** with clinically and radiologically **N0 neck**
- All oral cavity subsites (tongue, [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Floor of Mouth Carcinoma|floor of mouth carcinoma]], buccal mucosa, alveolus, hard palate, lip)
- Patient fit for general anaesthesia and neck exploration
- **Caution**: floor of mouth tumours — higher false-negative rate due to "shine-through" phenomenon (radiotracer in primary obscures adjacent Level I nodes)

### Contraindications

- T3-T4 tumours (high occult metastasis rate >40%; END preferred)
- Clinical or radiological N+ neck
- Previous neck surgery or RT (disrupted lymphatic drainage)
- Midline tumours (bilateral SNB required)

## Technique

### Triple-Technique Protocol (Jatin Shah)

#### 1. Radioisotope (Lymphoscintigraphy)

- **Tracer**: Technetium-99m (Tc-99m) labelled sulfur colloid or nanocolloid
- **Injection**: peritumoral, 4 quadrants around primary tumour
- **Imaging**: gamma camera images at 3 min, 15 min, and 1-hour delayed
- First lymph node(s) identified = sentinel node(s)
- **>1 sentinel node** may be identified — all must be excised
- Skin marked over "hot spots" to guide incision

#### 2. Blue Dye

- **Agent**: isosulfan blue (Lymphazurin) 1% or patent blue V
- **Injection**: 4 quadrants around tumour, ≤1 mL total, 30 min before surgery
- Blue-stained lymphatic channels followed to first blue node
- **Allergy risk**: 1-2% anaphylactoid reaction — premedicate with steroids/antihistamines

#### 3. Handheld Gamma Probe

- Intraoperative localisation of radioactive nodes
- **In vivo**: any node with 10-second count **>3× background** = sentinel node
- **Ex vivo**: excised SN count must be **>10× adjacent non-sentinel node**
- If residual basin radioactivity >10% of ex vivo count of hottest node → further exploration warranted
- Combination of all three techniques **maximises identification rate and accuracy**

### Newer Techniques

| Technique | Agent | Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **ICG fluorescence** | Indocyanine green | Real-time visualisation, no radiation, no allergy risk |
| **SPECT-CT** | Tc-99m + CT fusion | 3D localisation, better anatomical correlation |
| **Superparamagnetic iron oxide (SPIO)** | Magnetic nanoparticles | No radiation; detected by handheld magnetometer |
| **Near-infrared fluorescence** | ICG + NIR camera | Enhanced detection of deep nodes |

## Pathological Processing

### Critical Principle

- **Avoid routine [[Answers/General/Frozen Section in Surgical Margins|frozen section]]** for sentinel nodes — sensitivity too low for micrometastases
- Send for **rush permanent section** with enhanced pathological protocol:

### Step-Serial Sectioning Protocol

1. Bisect SN through hilum
2. Serial sections at **150 μm intervals** (step-serial sectioning)
3. H&E staining at each level
4. **Immunohistochemistry (IHC)**: pan-cytokeratin (AE1/AE3) on alternate sections
5. Detects micrometastases (0.2-2 mm) and [[Answers/General/Isolated Tumour Cells|isolated tumour cells]] (≤0.2 mm) missed by routine H&E

### Classification of Findings

| Finding | Size | Staging | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Macrometastasis** | >2 mm | pN1 | Completion ND required |
| **Micrometastasis** | >0.2-2 mm | pN1(mi) | Completion ND required |
| **Isolated tumour cells (ITC)** | ≤0.2 mm | pN0(i+) | Significance debated; consider ND |
| **Negative** | No tumour | pN0 | Observation |

## Key Trial Evidence

### SENT Trial (Schilling et al. 2015)

- **Design**: prospective multicenter, 14 European centres
- **Patients**: 415 T1-T2 cN0 oral/oropharyngeal SCC
- **Results**:
  - SN identification rate: **99.5%**
  - Positive SN: **23%**
  - SN was only positive node in **80%** of positive cases
  - NPV: **95%**
  - False negative rate: **14%**
  - 3-year DSS: **94%**
- **Conclusion**: SNB is reliable and safe for staging cN0 neck

### Senti-MERORL RCT (Garrel et al. 2020) — Level I Evidence

- **Design**: multicenter equivalence RCT, 279 patients
- **Randomisation**: END (n=139) vs SNB (n=140)
- **Primary endpoint**: 2-year neck node recurrence-free survival
- **Results**:
  - Neck RFS: END 89.6% vs SNB **90.7%** — difference 1.1%
  - Within 10% equivalence threshold (P<0.01)
  - No difference in DSS or OS
  - **Functional outcomes significantly worse in END** up to 6 months (shoulder dysfunction, pain)
- **Conclusion**: SNB is equivalent to END and should be considered the **reference technique** for staging cN0 early oral cancer

### Alkureishi et al. 2010 (5-Year Follow-up)

- 227 SNB procedures across 6 European centres
- 134 T1/T2 N0 patients; 79 had SNB as sole staging tool
- Confirmed long-term reliability and reproducibility
- **Caution for floor-of-mouth tumours**: higher false-negative rate due to shine-through phenomenon

## Advantages and Limitations

### Advantages Over Elective Neck Dissection

| Advantage | Detail |
|---|---|
| Reduced morbidity | Less shoulder dysfunction, less pain, shorter hospital stay |
| Functional preservation | Spinal accessory nerve, IJV, SCM not at risk |
| Better QOL | Garrel RCT: significantly better functional outcomes at 6 months |
| Enhanced pathology | Step-serial + IHC detects occult metastases missed by routine ND processing |
| Bilateral staging | Can stage both sides of neck in midline tumours with single procedure |
| Identifies unexpected drainage | 10-15% of SNs found in non-expected levels (skip metastases) |

### Limitations

| Limitation | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Shine-through** | FOM tumours: primary site radioactivity obscures Level I/II SNs |
| **Learning curve** | Requires multidisciplinary team (nuclear medicine, surgeon, pathologist) |
| **False negative rate** | 14% in SENT trial — potential for missed occult metastases |
| **Not for advanced disease** | Limited to T1-T2 cN0 only |
| **Cost** | Lymphoscintigraphy, gamma probe, enhanced pathology processing |
| **Requires completion ND** | If SN positive, second surgery needed (unless combined approach) |

### Addressing the Floor-of-Mouth Problem

- **SPECT-CT** improves localisation by fusing functional and anatomical imaging
- **ICG fluorescence** provides real-time intraoperative visualisation without radiotracer-based shine-through
- Consider **combined SNB + selective ND** of Level I for FOM tumours if shine-through suspected

> [!abstract] Key Points
> - SNB is now supported by **Level I evidence** (Garrel RCT 2020) as equivalent to END for T1-T2 cN0 oral cavity SCC
> - Triple-technique protocol (lymphoscintigraphy + blue dye + gamma probe) achieves 99.5% identification rate
> - Enhanced pathological processing (step-serial sectioning + IHC) is essential — **avoid routine frozen section** for sentinel nodes
> - NPV 95% (SENT trial); SN is the only positive node in 80% of cases with metastasis
> - Functional outcomes (shoulder, pain) are significantly better with SNB vs END

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - **Avoid tooth extraction** before SNB — disrupts lymphatic drainage and may alter sentinel node identification
> - For **floor-of-mouth tumours**, consider SPECT-CT or ICG to overcome the shine-through effect from the primary site obscuring Level I nodes
> - If the sentinel node contains **micrometastasis or macrometastasis**, completion neck dissection is mandatory; for ITC (≤0.2 mm), the evidence is evolving — discuss in MDT
> - The **in vivo 3:1 rule** (SN count >3× background) and **ex vivo 10:1 rule** (>10× non-SN) ensure reliable identification — if residual basin activity >10% of hottest SN, continue exploring
> - SNB can identify **skip metastases** (10-15%) in unexpected levels — a unique advantage over routine selective ND which may miss atypical drainage patterns
