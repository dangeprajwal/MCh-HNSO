## Introduction

The American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) 8th edition staging manual, effective from January 2018, introduced the most significant changes to oral cavity cancer staging in decades. The key change was the incorporation of **depth of invasion (DOI)** into the T classification — the first time a non-size parameter was added to T-staging for any solid tumour. Additionally, **extranodal extension (ENE)** was incorporated into nodal staging. These changes addressed long-recognised limitations where tumours of similar size but vastly different biology were assigned the same stage.

## Key Changes in AJCC 8th Edition

### Summary of Three Major Changes

| Change | Applies To | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **DOI added to T-staging** | Oral cavity only | DOI independently predicts survival and nodal metastasis |
| **Separate HPV+ staging** | Oropharynx only | HPV+ OPC has fundamentally different prognosis |
| **ENE added to N-staging** | All non-HPV HNSCC (clinical and pathologic) | ENE is strongest predictor of locoregional failure and survival |

## Part A: Depth of Invasion in T-Staging

### The Problem with 7th Edition T-Staging

- T-staging based solely on **greatest dimension** (size)
- A superficial 3 cm tumour and a deeply invasive 3 cm tumour were staged the same (T2)
- DOI and tumour thickness were known prognostic factors but were not incorporated
- Size alone did not reliably predict nodal metastasis risk or survival

### Depth of Invasion vs Tumour Thickness

| Parameter | Definition | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| **Tumour thickness** | Distance from surface to deepest point of invasion | From mucosal surface |
| **Depth of invasion (DOI)** | Distance from basement membrane of adjacent normal mucosa to deepest point of invasion | From a reconstructed "normal" mucosal line |

- In **exophytic** tumours: DOI < thickness (tumour projects above mucosal surface)
- In **ulcero-infiltrative** tumours: DOI > thickness (tumour invades below destroyed surface)
- DOI better reflects **biological behaviour** than thickness — measures true invasive component

### Evidence Base for DOI Thresholds

#### Ebrahimi et al. / ICOR 2014 (n=3,149)

- International multicenter retrospective study, 11 centres
- DOI was an **independent predictor** of disease-specific survival on multivariate analysis
- Proposed T-staging modification with DOI cutpoints:
  - ≤5 mm: no upstaging
  - >5-10 mm: upstage by 1 T category
  - >10 mm: upstage by 2 T categories

#### Huang et al. 2009 Meta-analysis (16 studies, n=1,136)

- Tumour thickness is strong predictor for cervical nodal involvement
- Optimal cutoff: **4 mm** (pooled OR 7.3, 95% CI 5.3-10.1)
- Established the clinical significance of depth-based measurement for neck management decisions

### AJCC 8th Edition T Classification for Oral Cavity

| Stage | 7th Edition (Size Only) | 8th Edition (Size + DOI) |
|---|---|---|
| **T1** | ≤2 cm | ≤2 cm AND DOI ≤5 mm |
| **T2** | >2-4 cm | ≤2 cm AND DOI >5-10 mm; OR >2-4 cm AND DOI ≤10 mm |
| **T3** | >4 cm | ≤2 cm AND DOI >10 mm; OR >2-4 cm AND DOI >10 mm; OR >4 cm AND DOI ≤10 mm |
| **T4a** | Invasion through cortical bone, IAN, [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Floor of Mouth Carcinoma|floor of mouth carcinoma]], skin | >4 cm AND DOI >10 mm; OR invasion through cortical bone, IAN, floor of mouth, skin |
| **T4b** | [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Masticator Space|masticator space]], pterygoid plates, skull base, ICA | Same as 7th edition |

### DOI Upstaging Rules

- DOI ≤5 mm → **no change** to size-based T
- DOI >5-10 mm → **upstage by one T category**
- DOI >10 mm → **upstage by two T categories**

**Example**: A 1.5 cm tumour (size = T1) with DOI of 8 mm → upstaged by 1 → **T2**
**Example**: A 1.5 cm tumour (size = T1) with DOI of 12 mm → upstaged by 2 → **T3**

### Clinical Implications of DOI

- DOI **cannot be reliably measured clinically** — it is a **pathological parameter**
- Preoperative imaging (MRI with contrast, high-resolution US) provides estimates
- DOI measurement requires adequate biopsy depth — superficial biopsies may underestimate
- Impacts decision for **[[Answers/Oral Cavity/Node Negative Neck in Oral Cancer|elective neck dissection]]**: DOI >4 mm (some guidelines use DOI ≥5 mm) → consider END even for cN0 neck

## Part B: Extranodal Extension in N-Staging

### Definition

- **ENE**: tumour extends through the lymph node capsule into surrounding soft tissue
- **ENEmi (microscopic)**: identified only on histopathology
- **ENEma (macroscopic)**: clinically/radiologically apparent (matted nodes, skin involvement)

### Evidence Base

- ENE is the **strongest adverse prognostic factor** in nodal disease
- Associated with:
  - 2× increased risk of locoregional recurrence
  - 2-3× increased risk of distant metastasis
  - Significantly reduced disease-specific and overall survival
- Cooper (EORTC) and Bernier (RTOG) RCTs: concurrent cisplatin + PORT improves outcomes in patients with positive margins and/or ENE

### AJCC 8th Edition N Classification (Clinical)

| Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| cN1 | Single ipsilateral node ≤3 cm, ENE(-) |
| cN2a | Single ipsilateral node 3-6 cm, ENE(-) |
| cN2b | Multiple ipsilateral nodes ≤6 cm, ENE(-) |
| cN2c | Bilateral/contralateral nodes ≤6 cm, ENE(-) |
| **cN3a** | Any node >6 cm, ENE(-) |
| **cN3b** | **Any node(s) with clinical ENE(+)** |

### AJCC 8th Edition N Classification (Pathologic)

| Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| pN1 | Single ipsilateral node ≤3 cm, ENE(-) |
| pN2a | Single ipsilateral node ≤3 cm ENE(+); OR single ipsilateral 3-6 cm ENE(-) |
| pN2b | Multiple ipsilateral ≤6 cm, ENE(-) |
| pN2c | Bilateral/contralateral ≤6 cm, ENE(-) |
| **pN3a** | Any node >6 cm, ENE(-) |
| **pN3b** | **Single ipsilateral >3 cm ENE(+); OR multiple nodes with ENE(+); OR contralateral ENE(+)** |

**Key change**: any ENE presence can upstage to N3b — reflecting its dominant prognostic impact.

## Part C: Stage Migration and Validation

### Lee et al. 2019

- Compared 7th vs 8th edition staging in oral cavity SCC cohort
- **Upstaging**: 12.4% for pT, 13.3% for pN, 24.8% for overall stage group
- DOI and ENE were prognostic in all groups
- Improved prognostic discrimination with 8th edition

### Practical Impact on Treatment

| Finding | Treatment Implication |
|---|---|
| DOI-based T upstaging | More patients qualify for [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Adjuvant Radiation Therapy in Oral Cancer|adjuvant RT]] |
| DOI >4 mm in cN0 | Consider elective neck dissection |
| ENE on pathology | Adjuvant concurrent CRT (not RT alone) |
| Stage migration (24.8%) | Approximately 1 in 4 patients reclassified |

> [!abstract] Key Points
> - The AJCC 8th edition added **DOI** to oral cavity T-staging — the first non-size parameter in T classification for any solid tumour
> - DOI cutpoints: ≤5 mm (no upstage), >5-10 mm (upstage ×1), >10 mm (upstage ×2)
> - DOI differs from tumour thickness — measured from the basement membrane of adjacent normal mucosa to deepest invasion point
> - **ENE** was added to N-staging — any ENE can upstage to N3b, reflecting its dominant prognostic impact
> - ICOR study (Ebrahimi 2014, n=3,149) provided the evidence base; ~25% of patients undergo stage migration from 7th to 8th edition

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - DOI is a **pathological parameter** — it cannot be reliably assessed clinically; preoperative MRI/US provides estimates but definitive staging is postoperative
> - For clinical decision-making in cN0 oral cavity cancer, **DOI >4 mm** (or ≥5 mm by some guidelines) is the threshold for elective neck dissection
> - Ensure biopsy specimens are **deep enough** to estimate DOI preoperatively — superficial biopsies will underestimate invasion depth
> - ENE detection on imaging (irregular nodal border, infiltration into fat) has moderate sensitivity — **pathological assessment** remains the gold standard
> - The combination of **positive margins + ENE** on pathology mandates concurrent cisplatin + PORT (Cooper/Bernier RCTs) — not RT alone
