## Introduction

Mandibular reconstruction following ablative surgery for oral cancer requires restoration of bony continuity, dental occlusion, facial contour, and oral competence. The free fibula flap remains the gold standard for osseous reconstruction. Traditionally, intraoperative contouring of the fibula to match the mandibular defect relied on the surgeon's experience, preoperative study of imaging, and intraoperative judgement. Computer-aided design/computer-aided manufacturing (CAD/CAM) technology and 3D printing have introduced a paradigm shift by enabling virtual surgical planning (VSP), patient-specific cutting guides, and custom reconstruction plates — improving accuracy, reducing operative time, and facilitating prosthetic-driven backward planning for dental rehabilitation.

## The VSP Workflow

### Preoperative Planning

1. **CT acquisition** — High-resolution CT of the mandible and donor site (fibula/iliac crest) with thin-slice (0.5–1 mm) axial images
2. **Segmentation** — 3D reconstruction of mandible and fibula from DICOM data using dedicated software (e.g., Materialise ProPlan CMF, Synthes, Stryker)
3. **Virtual tumour resection** — Margins defined on the 3D model; osteotomy lines planned
4. **Fibula segmentation** — Number and angulation of osteotomies determined to match the mandibular defect; fibula segments virtually positioned into the mandibular gap
5. **Prosthetic-driven backward planning** — [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Dental Implants after Mandibular Reconstruction|dental implants]] positions planned first (if immediate implant placement is intended), and the fibula positioned to optimize implant axis and prosthetic occlusion (the "Jaw-in-a-Day" concept)
6. **Plate design** — Patient-specific reconstruction plate designed to match the planned neo-mandible contour

### Manufacturing

- **3D-printed biomodels** of the mandible and fibula for intraoperative reference
- **Patient-specific cutting guides** (mandibular resection guides and fibula osteotomy guides) — ensure accurate osteotomy angles and segment lengths
- **Patient-specific reconstruction plates** — pre-bent or custom-milled titanium plates that precisely match the planned contour
- Typical turnaround time: 2–4 weeks from CT to delivery of guides

### Intraoperative Use

- Cutting guides secured to bone with temporary screws
- Mandibular resection performed through guide slots
- Fibula osteotomized through donor-site guides
- Segments assembled on the pre-bent plate → transferred to the mandibular defect
- Reduces reliance on intraoperative judgement for plate bending and segment positioning

## Types of 3D Models and Guides

| Type | Material | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Stereolithographic biomodel | Resin/polymer | Tactile 3D reference for preoperative planning |
| Mandibular cutting guide | Titanium/nylon | Precise osteotomy lines on mandible |
| Fibula cutting guide | Titanium/nylon | Accurate segment lengths and angles |
| Patient-specific plate | Titanium alloy | Pre-contoured reconstruction plate |
| Prosthetic planning model | Resin | Implant position planning |

## 3D Printing Technologies in Head and Neck Surgery

| Technology | Principle | Advantages |
|---|---|---|
| Stereolithography (SLA) | UV laser cures photopolymer resin layer by layer | High detail, smooth surface |
| Selective laser sintering (SLS) | Laser fuses powder (nylon/metal) | Durable, functional guides |
| Fused deposition modelling (FDM) | Thermoplastic filament extruded layer by layer | Low cost, rapid |
| Direct metal laser sintering (DMLS) | Laser melts metal powder (titanium) | Custom metal implants and plates |

## Advantages of VSP and 3D Printing

- **Improved accuracy** — Mandibular asymmetry <2 mm achievable (61% with VSP vs 12.5% conventional)
- **Reduced operative time** — Fibula shaping and plate bending performed preoperatively; ischaemia time reduced by 30–60 minutes
- **Reduced surgeon stress** — Eliminates intraoperative guesswork for complex multi-segment reconstructions
- **Prosthetic-driven planning** — Enables immediate implant placement and dental rehabilitation planning
- **Better aesthetic outcomes** — Precise restoration of facial contour and mandibular symmetry
- **Preoperative communication** — Facilitates discussion between ablative surgeon, reconstructive surgeon, and prosthodontist
- **Educational value** — 3D biomodels useful for patient counselling and surgical training

## Limitations

- **Cost** — VSP software, 3D printing, and custom plates are expensive; estimated additional cost of $5,000–$15,000 per case
- **Lead time** — Typically requires 2–4 weeks from CT to delivery of guides; not suitable for emergency or rapidly progressing tumours
- **Rigidity of plan** — Intraoperative findings may necessitate wider margins than planned; cutting guides become unusable if the resection changes
- **Accuracy dependent on imaging quality** — Poor CT resolution or motion artefact compromises the 3D model
- **Learning curve** — Requires surgeon familiarity with planning software and collaboration with biomedical engineers
- **Not available universally** — Requires specialised infrastructure (3D printers, biomedical engineering support)

## In-House vs Commercial VSP

| Parameter | Commercial (Outsourced) | In-House |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per case | High ($5,000–$15,000) | Lower (after initial investment) |
| Turnaround time | 2–4 weeks | 24–72 hours possible |
| Flexibility | Limited (changes require re-order) | High (modifications in real-time) |
| Software | Proprietary (ProPlan, Synthes) | Open-source options available |
| Quality control | Standardised | Operator-dependent |

Recent trends favour in-house VSP setups using open-source software (3D Slicer, Meshmixer) combined with affordable desktop 3D printers, reducing both cost and turnaround time while maintaining accuracy.

## Indications for VSP in Mandibular Reconstruction

- Complex defects requiring multiple osteotomies (≥3 fibula segments)
- Reconstruction crossing the midline or involving the condyle
- Planned immediate dental implant placement
- Bilateral mandibular defects
- Revision reconstruction after failed primary reconstruction
- Any case where precise restoration of occlusion and facial contour is critical

## Clinical Outcomes

VSP-guided reconstruction demonstrates superior accuracy compared to freehand techniques across multiple studies. A systematic review of 413 VSP-planned fibula reconstructions showed general inaccuracy ranging from 0–12.5 mm and 0.9°–17.5°, which was significantly better than freehand. VSP also reduces the rate of hardware complications, nonunion, and need for revision surgery.

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. VSP enables preoperative virtual tumour resection, fibula segmentation, and plate contouring on 3D-reconstructed CT models
> 2. Patient-specific cutting guides ensure accurate osteotomy angles and segment lengths intraoperatively
> 3. VSP reduces ischaemia time by 30–60 minutes through preoperative plate bending and fibula preparation
> 4. Mandibular asymmetry <2 mm is achievable in >60% of VSP cases vs ~12% with freehand
> 5. Prosthetic-driven backward planning enables immediate implant placement ("Jaw-in-a-Day")
> 6. Lead time of 2–4 weeks and high cost are the main limitations
> 7. In-house VSP with open-source software and desktop 3D printers is an emerging cost-effective alternative
> 8. The free fibula flap remains the gold standard donor for vascularised mandibular reconstruction

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - Always obtain thin-slice CT (0.5–1 mm) of both mandible and donor site for accurate 3D reconstruction
> - Plan a "safety margin" in the virtual resection — cutting guides should allow 5 mm extra resection if intraoperative [[Answers/General/Frozen Section in Surgical Margins|frozen section]] require wider margins
> - For edentulous patients with atrophic mandible, VSP is particularly valuable as it allows pre-planning the minimum bone stock needed
> - In multi-segment reconstructions (≥3 segments), the benefit of VSP over freehand is most pronounced
> - Consider the vascular pedicle length when planning fibula segmentation — the peroneal artery limits segment mobilisation
