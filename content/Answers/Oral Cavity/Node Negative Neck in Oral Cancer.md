## Introduction

The management of the clinically node-negative (cN0) neck in oral squamous cell carcinoma is one of the most debated topics in head and neck oncology. The fundamental dilemma is: should a patient with no clinical or radiographic evidence of cervical metastasis undergo prophylactic (elective) neck dissection at the time of primary tumour excision, or should the neck be observed and treated only if metastasis becomes clinically apparent? The debate has been resolved by the landmark D'Cruz et al. (2015) NEJM trial, which provides Level I evidence favouring elective neck dissection in early-stage oral cancer.

## The 20% Rule

The widely accepted principle is that **elective treatment of the neck is indicated when the risk of occult metastasis exceeds 20%** (Weiss et al. 1994). Below this threshold, the morbidity of treatment outweighs the benefit.

### Occult Metastasis Rates by Site and Stage

| Site | T1 | T2 | T3-T4 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tongue (oral) | 20–30% | 30–40% | 40–60% |
| [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Floor of Mouth Carcinoma|floor of mouth carcinoma]] | 20–25% | 30–35% | 40–50% |
| Buccal mucosa | 10–15% | 20–30% | 30–40% |
| Lower gingiva | 15–20% | 25–30% | 35–45% |
| Retromolar trigone | 15–25% | 25–35% | 35–50% |
| Hard palate | <10% | 10–20% | 20–30% |

For oral tongue cancer, even T1 tumours exceed the 20% threshold — hence END is recommended for virtually all oral tongue cancers.

## The D'Cruz Trial (NEJM 2015) — Landmark Evidence

- **Design:** Prospective RCT at Tata Memorial Hospital, Mumbai
- **Population:** 596 patients with T1-T2 oral SCC (lateralised), cN0 neck
- **Arms:** Elective neck dissection (END, n=245) vs Therapeutic neck dissection (TND, n=251) — TND performed only when nodal metastasis became clinically apparent during follow-up
- **Results:**
  - **Overall survival at 3 years:** END 80.0% vs TND 67.5% (HR 0.64, p=0.01)
  - **Disease-free survival at 3 years:** END 69.5% vs TND 45.9% (HR 0.45, p<0.001)
  - Occult metastasis detected in **30%** of END specimens
  - In the TND arm, neck recurrence occurred in 45.9% (with salvage possible in only a minority)
- **Conclusion:** END significantly improves overall and disease-free survival compared to watchful waiting in early oral SCC

This trial definitively established END as the standard of care for cT1-T2, cN0 oral cancer.

## [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Depth of Invasion in Oral Cancer|depth of invasion]] and the N0 Neck ([[Answers/Oral Cavity/AJCC 8th Edition Oral Cavity Staging|AJCC 8th edition staging]] Edition)

The AJCC 8th Edition incorporated **depth of invasion (DOI)** into T-staging for oral cavity cancers, partly based on its strong correlation with occult nodal metastasis:

| DOI | T Stage Modifier | Occult Metastasis Risk | END Recommended? |
|---|---|---|---|
| ≤5 mm | T1/T2 | ~10–20% | Consider based on other factors |
| >5–10 mm | Upstaged | 20–35% | Yes |
| >10 mm | Upstaged | >35% | Yes |

- DOI >4–5 mm is the most consistent cut-off associated with occult metastasis rates exceeding 20%
- Some centres use DOI >4 mm as the threshold for recommending END

## Extent of Neck Dissection

### Selective Neck Dissection (Levels I–III)

For cN0 oral cavity cancers, **selective neck dissection of levels I–III (supraomohyoid neck dissection — SOHND)** is the standard:

- Shah (1990) demonstrated that oral cavity cancers metastasise sequentially: Level I → II → III
- Skip metastases to levels IV and V are rare (<3% for oral cavity)
- SOHND has equivalent regional control to modified radical neck dissection for cN0 oral cancer

### Super-Selective Neck Dissection

- Dissection of only levels I–II (omitting level III)
- Proposed for tumours with very low risk of level III metastasis
- **Controversial** — while level III involvement in cN0 oral tongue is only 5–10%, the consequences of missing occult disease are significant
- May be considered for: very early tumours (T1, DOI <5 mm), favourable histology, anterior floor of mouth tumours
- Not widely adopted due to insufficient evidence

### Contralateral Neck

- Midline or near-midline tumours (floor of mouth, anterior tongue) have bilateral drainage
- Consider bilateral selective neck dissection when:
  - Tumour crosses or approaches the midline
  - Floor of mouth tumours (bilateral lymphatic drainage)
  - Tongue tumours within 1 cm of midline

## Alternatives to Elective Neck Dissection

### Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (SLNB)

- See [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Sentinel Node Biopsy in Oral Cancer|Sentinel Node Biopsy in Oral Cancer]]
- SENT trial and other multicentre studies validate SLNB as an alternative to END in T1-T2 cN0 oral cancer
- Negative predictive value >95%
- Avoids the morbidity of formal neck dissection in patients with truly N0 necks (~70% of patients)
- Requires institutional expertise in lymphoscintigraphy and serial step-sectioning/IHC

### Elective Neck Irradiation

- 50 Gy to levels I–III as an alternative to surgical END
- Typically used when the primary tumour is treated with radiation
- For surgically treated primaries, surgical END is preferred (provides staging information and avoids adding RT morbidity)

### Observation (Watch and Wait)

- Following the D'Cruz trial, observation is **no longer recommended** for T1-T2 oral tongue and floor of mouth cancers
- May still be considered for very superficial tumours (DOI <3–4 mm) in favourable locations (hard palate, superficial buccal mucosa) where occult metastasis risk is truly <15%
- Requires meticulous follow-up with clinical examination and ultrasound every 4–8 weeks

## Role of Imaging in the N0 Neck

| Modality | Sensitivity | Specificity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clinical examination | 60–70% | 70–80% | Misses occult disease |
| Ultrasound ± FNAC | 70–90% | 80–95% | Best non-invasive modality |
| CT | 65–80% | 70–85% | Size criteria: >10 mm |
| MRI | 70–85% | 75–90% | Better for retropharyngeal nodes |
| [[Answers/General/PET-CT in Head and Neck Cancer|PET-CT]] | 70–90% | 85–95% | Best for N2-3; poor for micrometastasis |

No imaging modality reliably detects micrometastases (<3 mm) — this is why END/SLNB remains necessary despite negative imaging.

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. D'Cruz et al. (NEJM 2015): END improves overall survival vs observation in T1-T2 cN0 oral cancer (80% vs 67.5% at 3 years)
> 2. The 20% rule: elective treatment indicated when occult metastasis risk exceeds 20% (Weiss 1994)
> 3. Oral tongue T1 tumours already have 20–30% occult metastasis rate — END recommended
> 4. Selective neck dissection of levels I–III (SOHND) is the standard for cN0 oral cancer
> 5. DOI >4–5 mm correlates strongly with occult metastasis and supports END recommendation
> 6. SLNB is a validated alternative to END in T1-T2 cN0 oral cancer (NPV >95%)
> 7. Skip metastases to level IV/V are rare (<3%) in oral cavity cancers
> 8. Bilateral neck dissection indicated for midline/near-midline tumours
> 9. Observation is no longer standard for T1-T2 oral tongue/FOM cancers after the D'Cruz trial
> 10. No imaging modality reliably detects micrometastases — surgical staging remains essential

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - After the D'Cruz trial, the only scenario where observation of the N0 neck is still reasonable is for very superficial tumours (DOI <3–4 mm) in low-risk subsites (hard palate, superficial lip)
> - END provides critical staging information — if occult metastasis is found (especially with [[Answers/General/Extranodal Extension|ENE]]), adjuvant chemoradiation is indicated
> - For oral tongue cancer, always assess DOI on the preoperative MRI — if >5 mm, END is virtually mandatory
> - The morbidity of SOHND is minimal (shoulder function preserved, no SCM or IJV sacrifice) — the benefit-to-risk ratio strongly favours END
> - Ultra-staging of the sentinel node (serial step-sectioning + IHC) detects micrometastases that routine H&E would miss
