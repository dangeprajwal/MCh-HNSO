## Introduction

Depth of invasion (DOI) is arguably the single most important pathological parameter in oral cavity squamous cell carcinoma. It reflects the biological aggressiveness of the tumour — its capacity to invade deeper tissue planes, access lymphovascular channels, and metastasise to cervical lymph nodes. The significance of DOI was formally recognised in the AJCC 8th edition (2017), which for the first time incorporated DOI into the T classification of oral cavity cancers. This was the most impactful staging change for oral cancer since the TNM system was introduced. DOI influences decisions about elective neck dissection, adjuvant therapy, and overall prognostication.

## Definitions: DOI vs Tumour Thickness

| Parameter | Definition | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Depth of invasion (DOI) | Distance from the basement membrane of the nearest normal mucosa to the deepest point of tumour invasion | Measured from a reconstructed mucosal surface |
| Tumour thickness | Total vertical distance from the tumour surface to the deepest point of invasion | Includes exophytic component |

DOI is measured by drawing a "plumb line" from the surface of the basement membrane of adjacent normal mucosa down to the deepest point of invasion. An exophytic tumour may have significant tumour thickness but relatively shallow DOI. Conversely, an ulcerated/endophytic tumour may have DOI exceeding its tumour thickness. **DOI is the preferred and AJCC-endorsed measurement** because it better reflects the invasive component and is more reproducible.

## AJCC 8th Edition T Staging — Oral Cavity

The key change in the 8th edition was incorporating DOI as a modifier of T stage. Extrinsic tongue muscle invasion was removed as a T4a criterion.

| T Stage | Size | DOI Modifier |
|---|---|---|
| T1 | ≤2 cm | DOI ≤5 mm |
| T2 | ≤2 cm with DOI >5 mm, OR >2–4 cm | DOI ≤10 mm |
| T3 | >2 cm with DOI >10 mm, OR >4 cm | DOI >10 mm |
| T4a | Moderately advanced local disease | Invades adjacent structures |
| T4b | Very advanced local disease | Masticator space, skull base, ICA |

**Important:** A small tumour (≤2 cm) with deep invasion (>5 mm) is upstaged to T2; a ≤2 cm tumour with DOI >10 mm becomes T3. This reflects the clinical reality that deeply invasive small tumours behave aggressively despite their deceptively small surface dimensions.

## Clinical Significance of DOI

### 1. Prediction of Occult Nodal Metastasis

DOI is the strongest predictor of occult cervical lymph node metastasis in clinically N0 oral cavity cancer:

| DOI | Occult Nodal Metastasis Risk |
|---|---|
| <2 mm | <5% — observation acceptable |
| 2–4 mm | 10–15% — borderline for END |
| 4–5 mm | ~20% — threshold for END |
| >5 mm | >20–25% — END strongly recommended |

The **4 mm DOI cutoff** has emerged as the most widely accepted threshold for recommending elective neck dissection (END) in clinically N0 oral SCC. At this depth, the occult metastasis rate exceeds the 15–20% threshold at which END is considered beneficial over observation.

### 2. Decision-Making for Elective Neck Dissection

- DOI ≤4 mm and clinically N0 → observation with close surveillance may be acceptable (sentinel node biopsy is an alternative)
- DOI >4 mm and clinically N0 → **elective neck dissection is recommended** (levels I–III or I–IV)
- This DOI-based approach has been validated in multiple international studies and is now incorporated into NCCN guidelines

### 3. Adjuvant Therapy Decision-Making

DOI influences the decision to recommend adjuvant radiotherapy even in the absence of other adverse features:

**Adjuvant RT indications in oral SCC (NCCN):**
- DOI >5 mm (some guidelines use >4 mm)
- Perineural invasion
- Lymphovascular invasion
- Close margins (<5 mm)
- pN+ disease
- Extranodal extension (indication for concurrent chemoRT)

DOI as a **sole criterion** for adjuvant RT: A deeply invasive tumour (DOI >5 mm) with clear margins and no other adverse features still warrants consideration for adjuvant RT because of the high risk of occult nodal disease and local recurrence. The TMH 2019 question specifically addresses this — the answer is that DOI alone can justify adjuvant treatment.

### 4. Prognostication

- DOI independently predicts disease-free survival, overall survival, and local recurrence
- Almangush et al. (2015) showed DOI and worst pattern of invasion (WPOI) are the two strongest pathological predictors of locoregional recurrence in early oral tongue cancer
- The combination of DOI ≥4 mm + WPOI type 4–5 identifies the highest-risk group

## Measurement Techniques

### Pathological Measurement (Gold Standard)

- Measured on H&E-stained sections by a pathologist
- "Plumb line" method from the reconstructed basement membrane surface
- Must be distinguished from tumour thickness
- The specimen must be oriented and sectioned through the deepest area

### Radiological Assessment (Pre-operative)

| Modality | Accuracy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| MRI | Best; r = 0.87–0.95 | T2-weighted or contrast-enhanced |
| Intraoral ultrasound | Good for anterior tongue | r = 0.90; limited for posterior sites |
| CT | Moderate | Less soft tissue contrast than MRI |

Pre-operative radiological DOI assessment allows planning of:
- Extent of surgical resection
- Need for elective neck dissection
- Reconstruction planning
- Multidisciplinary discussion

MRI tends to overestimate DOI by 1–3 mm compared to pathological measurement due to peri-tumoural oedema and inflammation.

## DOI and the Node-Negative Neck

The management of the clinically N0 neck in oral cavity cancer is heavily influenced by DOI. See [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Node Negative Neck in Oral Cancer|Node Negative Neck in Oral Cancer]] for a detailed discussion. In summary:

- DOI >4 mm → END recommended
- DOI 2–4 mm → consider sentinel lymph node biopsy (SLNB) as an alternative to END
- DOI <2 mm → observation with surveillance imaging

## Limitations of DOI

- **Interobserver variability** — measurement can vary between pathologists, particularly in ulcerated tumours where the "reconstructed" mucosal surface is subjective
- **Pre-operative assessment** — radiological DOI is an estimate; final pathological DOI may differ
- **Does not capture biological heterogeneity** — two tumours with the same DOI may behave differently based on WPOI, perineural invasion, and molecular features
- **Not applicable to all subsites** — DOI is only incorporated into AJCC staging for the **oral cavity**, not for oropharynx, larynx, or hypopharynx

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - DOI changed the game for oral cancer staging — a 1.5 cm tongue tumour with 8 mm DOI is now T2 (not T1), reflecting its aggressive biology. Always report DOI alongside tumour size
> - The 4 mm DOI threshold for END is the most commonly tested cutoff — when in doubt about neck management in a cN0 oral cancer, check the DOI
> - DOI as a sole criterion for adjuvant RT is a nuanced but important concept — a pT2N0 oral tongue SCC with 7 mm DOI and clear margins still warrants adjuvant RT discussion because the biology is aggressive
> - Pre-operative MRI DOI measurement guides surgical planning, but always correlate with final pathological DOI — MRI overestimates by 1–3 mm due to peri-tumoural inflammation
> - Extrinsic tongue muscle invasion is no longer a T4a criterion in AJCC 8th edition — it was removed because DOI captures the same prognostic information more reproducibly
