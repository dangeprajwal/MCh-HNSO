## The Brandwein-Gensler Histologic Risk Assessment Model

### Overview

In 2005, Brandwein-Gensler et al. proposed a histologic risk assessment model for oral SCC based on analysis of 300+ oral tongue and [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Floor of Mouth Carcinoma|floor of mouth carcinoma]] cancers. The model integrates three independent histopathological parameters to classify tumours into **low-risk, intermediate-risk, and high-risk** categories. Critically, this model was shown to be **a stronger predictor of local recurrence and overall survival than [[Answers/General/Surgical Margins in Head and Neck Cancer|surgical margins]] status**.

### The Three Parameters

**1. Worst Pattern of Invasion (WPOI)**

The invasive front of oral SCC is scored on a 5-point scale:

| WPOI | Pattern | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Type 1 | Broad pushing front | Well-delineated, pushing border |
| Type 2 | Finger-like projections | Broad cords infiltrating |
| Type 3 | Large islands (>15 cells) | Islands of tumour in stroma |
| Type 4 | Small islands (<15 cells) | Small clusters/cords in stroma |
| Type 5 | Tumour satellites | Dispersed tumour satellites ≥1 mm from main tumour |

- WPOI 4 and 5 are the **aggressive patterns** associated with significantly worse outcomes
- WPOI 5 (tumour satellites) is the highest risk — associated with occult nodal metastasis, local recurrence, and poor survival
- The distinction between WPOI 3 and 4 is the most clinically relevant threshold

**2. Perineural Invasion (PNI)**

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 0 | No PNI |
| 1 | Small nerve PNI (nerve diameter <1 mm) |
| 2 | Large nerve PNI (nerve diameter ≥1 mm) |

Large nerve PNI carries significantly worse prognosis and is an independent predictor of locoregional recurrence.

**3. Lymphocytic Host Response (LHR)**

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Strong continuous band of lymphocytes at invasive front |
| 2 | Patchy lymphocytic response |
| 3 | Weak or absent lymphocytic response |

Strong LHR (score 1) indicates an active immune response against the tumour and is associated with better prognosis.

### Risk Scoring

| Risk Category | Criteria | Outcomes |
|---|---|---|
| Low risk | WPOI 1–3, no PNI, strong LHR | Low recurrence; excellent survival |
| Intermediate risk | WPOI 4, or small nerve PNI, or weak LHR | Moderate recurrence risk |
| High risk | WPOI 5, or large nerve PNI | High recurrence; poor survival |

### Clinical Relevance

- The high-risk group has significantly worse local recurrence and overall survival **even when margins are clear**
- This suggests that margin status alone is insufficient for prognostication — the biology of the invasive front matters
- Potential clinical implications:
  - High-risk patients may benefit from adjuvant therapy even with clear margins
  - Low-risk patients with close margins may not need aggressive re-excision
  - Can guide decision for [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Node Negative Neck in Oral Cancer|elective neck dissection]] in early oral cancer

## Patterns of Invasion and Cervical Metastasis

### WPOI and Nodal Metastasis

| WPOI | Occult Nodal Metastasis Rate |
|---|---|
| Types 1–3 | ~10% |
| Type 4 | ~20–30% |
| Type 5 | ~40–50% |

WPOI 5 (tumour satellites) is strongly associated with occult cervical metastasis, making it a potential criterion for recommending elective neck dissection in early-stage (cT1–T2 cN0) oral cancer, even when DOI is borderline (<4 mm).

### Combined DOI + WPOI

Almangush et al. (2015) demonstrated that the combination of **DOI ≥4 mm + WPOI 4–5** identifies the highest-risk group for locoregional recurrence in early oral tongue cancer. These two parameters together are stronger predictors than either alone.

## Tumour Budding

### Definition

Tumour budding refers to the presence of isolated single tumour cells or clusters of <5 cells at the invasive front, detached from the main tumour mass. It is considered a histological marker of **epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT)**.

### Scoring

- **Low budding:** <5 buds per high-power field (HPF)
- **High budding:** ≥5 buds per HPF

### Prognostic Significance

Almangush et al. (2018) meta-analysis:
- High tumour budding is significantly associated with **worse overall survival** (HR 2.58), **lymph node metastasis** (OR 3.73), and **disease recurrence** (HR 2.18)
- Tumour budding is independent of tumour stage and grade
- May be incorporated into future histological risk models

### Relationship to WPOI

Tumour budding overlaps conceptually with WPOI 4–5 — both describe dispersed tumour cells at the invasive front. However, tumour budding is more precisely quantified and may offer better reproducibility.

## Established and Upcoming Prognostic Factors

### Established Prognostic Factors

| Category | Factor | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| T stage | Tumour size + DOI | AJCC 8th edition; DOI strongest |
| N stage | Nodal metastasis + ENE | Single most important prognostic factor |
| Margins | Positive, close (<5 mm), clear | Positive margins → worst outcomes |
| DOI | [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Depth of Invasion in Oral Cancer|Depth of invasion]] | 4 mm threshold for END |
| WPOI | Brandwein-Gensler type 1–5 | Types 4–5 predict recurrence |
| PNI | Perineural invasion | Independent poor prognostic factor |
| LVI | Lymphovascular invasion | Predicts nodal metastasis |
| ENE | [[Answers/General/Extranodal Extension|extranodal extension]] | Indication for chemoRT |
| Grade | Differentiation | Poorly differentiated → worse |

### Upcoming/Emerging Prognostic Factors

| Factor | Evidence Level | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Tumour budding | Meta-analysis validated | EMT marker; predicts recurrence |
| Tumour-stroma ratio | Moderate evidence | High stroma → better prognosis |
| PD-L1 expression | Emerging | Predicts immunotherapy response |
| p16 status | Established for oropharynx | Limited role in oral cavity |
| HPV status | Not significant for oral cavity | Unlike oropharynx |
| [[Answers/General/MicroRNAs in Oral Cancer|microRNAs]] profiles | Early research | Potential diagnostic/prognostic biomarkers |
| Circulating tumour DNA | Emerging | Non-invasive recurrence monitoring |
| Tumour-infiltrating lymphocytes | Growing evidence | High TILs → better prognosis |
| LOH at 3p/9p | Phase II validated | Risk of premalignant transformation |
| Molecular margins (p53/eIF4E) | Research stage | More sensitive than histological margins |

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. Brandwein-Gensler model integrates WPOI, PNI, and LHR to classify oral SCC into low/intermediate/high risk — stronger predictor than margin status alone
> 2. WPOI is scored 1–5; Types 4 (small islands <15 cells) and 5 (tumour satellites ≥1 mm from main tumour) are aggressive patterns
> 3. WPOI 5 is associated with ~40–50% occult nodal metastasis rate — may justify END even when DOI is borderline
> 4. Strong lymphocytic host response (LHR score 1) indicates active anti-tumour immunity and predicts better prognosis
> 5. Large nerve PNI (≥1 mm) is an independent predictor of locoregional recurrence and worse survival
> 6. Combination of DOI ≥4 mm + WPOI 4–5 identifies the highest-risk early oral tongue cancers (Almangush 2015)
> 7. Tumour budding (≥5 buds/HPF) is a validated prognostic marker associated with worse OS (HR 2.58) and nodal metastasis (OR 3.73)
> 8. Tumour budding reflects epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) at the invasive front
> 9. Emerging biomarkers include PD-L1 expression, circulating tumour DNA, tumour-infiltrating lymphocytes, and molecular margins
> 10. Nodal status with ENE remains the single most important prognostic factor in oral SCC overall

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - The Brandwein-Gensler model's most important finding: high-risk histology predicts recurrence even with clear margins. This challenges the traditional reliance on margin status as the primary prognostic indicator
> - For exam answers on WPOI, draw the 5-type diagram: pushing front → finger-like → large islands → small islands → satellites. The Type 3–4 boundary is the critical clinical threshold
> - When asked about prognostic factors, organise your answer into patient factors (age, comorbidity, tobacco), tumour factors (T, N, margins, DOI, WPOI, PNI, LVI, ENE), and treatment factors (completeness of resection, adjuvant therapy, timing). This structure scores well
> - Tumour budding is the "newest" established histological marker — mentioning it demonstrates contemporary knowledge. Remember: it reflects EMT, which is the same biological process driving invasion and metastasis
> - The combination answer for "patterns of invasion and cervical metastasis" should emphasise WPOI 4–5 → high occult nodal rate → supports elective neck dissection
