## Rationale for HPV Testing in Oropharyngeal Cancer

HPV-driven oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma (OPSCC) is a distinct biological and clinical entity with markedly different prognosis compared to HPV-negative disease. Accurate determination of HPV status is essential for:

1. **Prognostication** -- HPV-positive OPSCC has 3-year OS ~82% vs ~57% for HPV-negative (RTOG 0129)
2. **Staging** -- AJCC 8th edition introduced separate staging systems for p16-positive and p16-negative OPSCC
3. **Clinical trial eligibility** -- de-escalation trials require accurate HPV status determination
4. **Treatment planning** -- emerging de-escalation strategies for HPV-positive, low-risk patients
5. **Counselling** -- patient expectations regarding prognosis and treatment outcomes

The ideal HPV test should be accurate, reproducible, applicable to formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded (FFPE) tissue, cost-effective, and have rapid turnaround time.

## HPV Detection Methods

### 1. p16 Immunohistochemistry (IHC) -- Surrogate Marker

p16 (CDKN2A/INK4a) is a cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor that is paradoxically **overexpressed** in HPV-driven cancers due to HPV E7-mediated Rb inactivation and loss of negative feedback on the p16 promoter.

**Criteria for Positivity (CAP/ASCO Guideline):**
- **Diffuse** nuclear AND cytoplasmic staining
- In **>=70%** of tumour cells (strong, continuous, block-type staining)
- Both nuclear and cytoplasmic compartments must stain

**Performance Characteristics:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Sensitivity | 90--94% |
| Specificity | 80--83% |
| PPV (in oropharynx) | 90--95% |
| NPV | 85--90% |
| Turnaround time | 1--2 days |
| Cost | Low |

**Advantages:**
- Widely available in all pathology laboratories
- Inexpensive, rapid, and reproducible
- High sensitivity -- excellent screening test
- Recommended as first-line test by CAP, ASCO, NCCN

**Limitations:**
- Not HPV-specific -- p16 can be overexpressed by non-HPV mechanisms (RB1 mutation, gene amplification)
- Lower specificity than direct HPV testing, particularly outside the oropharynx
- False positives: ~10--15% of p16-positive OPSCC are HPV DNA-negative
- p16 positivity in non-oropharyngeal sites does NOT reliably indicate HPV-driven cancer

### 2. HPV DNA In Situ Hybridisation (ISH)

Detects HPV DNA directly within tumour cells on tissue sections, preserving histological architecture.

**Technique:**
- Chromogenic ISH (CISH) or fluorescent ISH (FISH)
- Probes targeting high-risk HPV types (HPV-16, 18, etc.)
- Punctate nuclear signal indicates integrated virus; diffuse signal indicates episomal virus

**Performance:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Sensitivity | 85--90% |
| Specificity | 88--95% |
| Turnaround time | 1--2 days |
| Cost | Moderate |

**Advantages:**
- Direct detection of HPV DNA within tumour cells
- Morphological correlation -- confirms HPV in tumour (not adjacent normal tissue)
- Can distinguish integration pattern from episomal

**Limitations:**
- Lower sensitivity than PCR-based methods
- Signal amplification issues with low viral copy numbers
- Inter-observer variability in interpretation
- Not widely available in all laboratories

### 3. HPV DNA PCR

Amplification-based detection of HPV DNA using consensus or type-specific primers.

**Common Primer Systems:**

| Primer | Target | HPV Types Detected |
|---|---|---|
| GP5+/6+ | L1 region | Broad-spectrum (~40 types) |
| SPF10 | L1 region | Broader spectrum (>50 types) |
| MY09/MY11 | L1 region | ~30 types |
| Type-specific primers | E6/E7 of HPV-16, 18 | Specific types only |

**Performance:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Sensitivity | 95--98% |
| Specificity | 84--90% |
| Turnaround time | 1--3 days |
| Cost | Moderate--high |

**Advantages:**
- Highest sensitivity of DNA-based methods
- Can be performed on FFPE tissue
- Allows HPV genotyping
- Quantitative PCR (qPCR) can assess viral load

**Limitations:**
- Does not confirm transcriptional activity -- detects latent/passenger HPV
- Contamination risk (PCR artifacts)
- Requires dedicated molecular pathology laboratory
- Cannot determine if HPV is within tumour cells or stroma

### 4. HPV E6/E7 mRNA Detection -- Gold Standard

Detection of transcriptionally active HPV through E6/E7 oncogene mRNA expression confirms that HPV is biologically active and driving the tumour.

**Methods:**
- **RT-PCR (quantitative)** -- most sensitive and specific
- **RNA in situ hybridisation (RNA ISH)** -- e.g., RNAscope technology
- **Aptima HPV assay** -- clinically validated for cervical screening

**Performance:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Sensitivity | 95--98% |
| Specificity | 96--100% |
| Turnaround time | 2--5 days |
| Cost | High |

**Advantages:**
- Confirms transcriptionally active, tumour-driving HPV infection
- Distinguishes clinically relevant HPV from bystander infection
- RNA ISH preserves morphological context
- Best discriminator of favourable prognosis (Schache 2011)

**Limitations:**
- RNA degradation in FFPE tissue -- requires good tissue handling
- High cost and specialised equipment
- Limited availability outside academic centres
- Longer turnaround time

### 5. Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) Approaches

- Targeted NGS panels can simultaneously detect HPV DNA, determine genotype, assess viral integration sites, and identify somatic mutations
- Whole-genome/exome sequencing identifies HPV integration patterns
- Emerging liquid biopsy approaches: circulating HPV DNA (ctHPVDNA) in plasma
- Currently limited to research settings; potential future clinical application

## Recommended Algorithm for HPV Testing

### Step 1: p16 IHC on All Oropharyngeal SCC

- **p16 negative (< 70% staining)** --> classify as HPV-negative OPSCC; no further HPV testing required
- **p16 positive (>=70% diffuse nuclear + cytoplasmic)** --> proceed to Step 2 if confirmatory testing desired

### Step 2: Confirmatory HPV-Specific Testing (When Indicated)

- **HPV DNA PCR** or **HPV DNA ISH** to confirm HPV infection
- **HPV E6/E7 mRNA** if available (gold standard confirmation)

### When to Pursue Confirmatory Testing Beyond p16

1. Non-oropharyngeal head and neck SCC with p16 positivity
2. Clinical trial enrolment requiring confirmed HPV status
3. Discordant clinical features (e.g., heavy smoker, unusual presentation)
4. Unknown primary with cervical metastasis
5. Research settings

## Discordant Results: p16-Positive / HPV DNA-Negative

This discordance occurs in approximately **10--15%** of p16-positive OPSCC cases. Possible explanations:

1. **Non-HPV mechanisms of p16 overexpression** -- RB1 mutation, CDKN2A amplification
2. **Technical failure** -- HPV DNA degraded in FFPE, low sensitivity of ISH
3. **Hit-and-run hypothesis** -- HPV initiated carcinogenesis but viral DNA subsequently lost
4. **Low viral copy number** -- below detection threshold of assay used

**Clinical Significance:**
- Lewis et al. (2010) showed that p16-positive/HPV DNA-negative tumours still had better prognosis than p16-negative tumours
- However, prognosis may be intermediate between p16+/HPV+ and p16-/HPV- groups
- The AJCC 8th edition stages based on **p16 status alone** (not HPV DNA)
- For clinical practice, p16 positivity alone is sufficient for staging and prognostication in oropharyngeal SCC

## Guideline Recommendations

### College of American Pathologists (CAP) -- 2018

- p16 IHC testing is **recommended** for all newly diagnosed oropharyngeal SCC
- Cutoff: >=70% diffuse nuclear and cytoplasmic staining
- Additional HPV-specific testing (ISH, PCR) is **not required** for routine clinical use in oropharyngeal SCC
- p16 should **NOT** be used as a surrogate for HPV in non-oropharyngeal sites

### ASCO Guidelines

- p16 IHC is the recommended initial test for all oropharyngeal SCC
- Supports p16 as sufficient for AJCC 8th edition staging
- HPV-specific testing recommended in research/trial settings

### NCCN Guidelines

- p16 IHC testing recommended for all oropharyngeal SCC
- p16-positive OPSCC should be staged using the HPV-mediated (p16+) staging system
- p16-negative OPSCC staged using the standard AJCC staging system

## Emerging and Future Directions

### Circulating HPV DNA (ctHPVDNA)

- Detected in plasma of patients with HPV-positive OPSCC
- Potential for non-invasive diagnosis, treatment monitoring, and surveillance
- Serial ctHPVDNA measurements can detect recurrence earlier than imaging
- Sensitivity >90% for detecting residual/recurrent disease

### Saliva-Based HPV Testing

- Non-invasive oral rinse-based HPV DNA detection
- Potential screening tool for high-risk populations
- Sensitivity lower than tissue-based testing; research stage

### HPV Serology

- HPV-16 E6/E7 antibodies in serum
- Present years before clinical diagnosis -- potential early detection
- Sensitivity ~90%, specificity ~95% for HPV-16-driven OPSCC
- Not yet validated for clinical use

## Clinical Pearls for Examinations

1. **p16 IHC is the current standard** for HPV testing in oropharyngeal SCC -- simple, cheap, widely available
2. **>=70% diffuse nuclear AND cytoplasmic** staining is the validated cutoff -- focal or cytoplasmic-only staining is negative
3. **HPV E6/E7 mRNA is the gold standard** but not required for routine clinical practice
4. **p16 is NOT a reliable surrogate for HPV outside the oropharynx** -- do not apply favourable prognosis to p16-positive oral cavity or laryngeal SCC
5. **AJCC 8th edition uses p16 alone** to determine staging pathway -- no confirmatory HPV testing needed for staging
6. **Discordant p16+/HPV-** cases still carry better prognosis than p16-negative tumours
7. The **Smeets algorithm** (p16 IHC then GP5+/6+ PCR on p16+ cases) achieves 100% sensitivity and specificity
