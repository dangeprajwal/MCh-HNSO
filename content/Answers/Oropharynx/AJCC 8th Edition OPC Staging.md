## Overview

The AJCC 8th Edition (implemented January 2018) introduced the most significant change in head and neck cancer staging in decades: a **completely separate staging system for HPV-associated (p16-positive) oropharyngeal cancer**. This was driven by the recognition that HPV-positive OPC behaves as a biologically distinct disease with dramatically superior outcomes, and that the conventional staging system (used for HPV-negative tumours) grossly over-staged these patients, providing poor prognostic discrimination.

## Rationale for Separate Staging

### The Problem with AJCC 7th Edition

Under the 7th edition, HPV-positive OPC patients were staged identically to HPV-negative patients. This created several problems:

1. **Stage migration**: >80% of HPV-positive patients were classified as stage III-IV despite excellent prognosis
2. **Poor prognostic discrimination**: 5-year OS for stage III HPV+ was ~90%, virtually identical to stage IV HPV+ (~80%), while stage III HPV- was ~50%
3. **Inappropriate risk communication**: Patients told they had "stage IV cancer" when their actual prognosis was excellent
4. **Confounding of clinical trials**: Mixing HPV+ and HPV- patients in the same stage groups distorted trial results
5. **Potential for over-treatment**: Stage IV designation might drive more aggressive therapy than necessary

### Evidence for Separation

The ICON-S study (O'Sullivan 2016) analysed 1,907 HPV-positive OPC patients and demonstrated:
- The conventional N classification provided poor discrimination in HPV+ disease
- A simplified 4-tier staging system (I-IV) with N categories based on **laterality and number of nodes** (rather than size) provided superior prognostic separation
- 5-year OS ranged from 94% (stage I) to 38% (stage IV), compared with no meaningful separation under AJCC 7th edition

## Determining HPV Status: p16 as the Defining Biomarker

### p16 Immunohistochemistry (IHC)

- **The standard**: p16 IHC is the sole biomarker used to classify oropharyngeal cancer as HPV-associated in the AJCC 8th edition
- **Positive definition**: Diffuse, strong nuclear **and** cytoplasmic staining in **≥70%** of tumour cells
- **Why p16 and not HPV testing**: p16 IHC is widely available, reproducible, inexpensive, and has high sensitivity (~90%) and adequate specificity (~80%) for HPV in oropharyngeal tumours
- **Important**: p16 positivity is only valid as an HPV surrogate in **oropharyngeal** SCC. p16+ tumours at other head and neck sites (oral cavity, larynx, hypopharynx) should NOT be staged using the HPV-associated system

### When to Confirm with HPV Testing

- p16-positive OPC with **atypical features** (heavy smoker, older age, unusual histology)
- Research settings requiring definitive HPV confirmation
- HPV testing methods: HPV ISH (in situ hybridisation), HPV DNA PCR, E6/E7 mRNA detection

## AJCC 8th Edition: p16-Positive (HPV-Associated) OPC — Clinical Staging

### T Classification (Clinical)

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| T0 | No primary identified |
| T1 | Tumour ≤2 cm |
| T2 | Tumour >2 cm and ≤4 cm |
| T3 | Tumour >4 cm or extension to lingual surface of epiglottis |
| T4 | Tumour invades any of: larynx, extrinsic tongue muscles, medial pterygoid, hard palate, mandible, lateral pterygoid muscle, pterygoid plates, lateral nasopharynx, skull base, or encases internal carotid artery |

**Key differences from HPV-negative T staging**:
- T4 is **not subdivided** into T4a and T4b (all T4 is a single category)
- Extension to lingual epiglottis is T3 (not T4a as in HPV-negative staging)

### N Classification (Clinical)

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| N0 | No regional lymph node metastasis |
| N1 | One or more **ipsilateral** nodes, none >6 cm |
| N2 | **Contralateral or bilateral** nodes, none >6 cm |
| N3 | Any node >6 cm |

**Key differences from HPV-negative N staging**:
- Based on **laterality** and size >6 cm, NOT the complex N1/N2a/N2b/N2c system
- [[Answers/General/Extranodal Extension|Extranodal extension]] (ENE) does **not** affect clinical N staging in HPV+ OPC (unlike HPV-negative, where ENE upstages to N3b)
- The simplified system reflects the observation that in HPV+ OPC, laterality of nodes matters more prognostically than the traditional size-based subcategories

### M Classification (Clinical)

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| M0 | No distant metastasis |
| M1 | Distant metastasis |

### Clinical Stage Groups (p16-Positive)

| Stage | T | N | M |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I** | T0-T2 | N0-N1 | M0 |
| **II** | T0-T2 | N2 | M0 |
| | T3 | N0-N2 | M0 |
| **III** | T4 | N0-N2 | M0 |
| | Any T | N3 | M0 |
| **IV** | Any T | Any N | M1 |

**Critical observation**: There is **no stage IV for locoregional disease** in HPV-positive OPC. Stage IV is reserved exclusively for distant metastatic disease. This reflects the excellent prognosis even for locally advanced HPV+ tumours.

**Comparison with AJCC 7th edition**: A patient with T2N2bM0 HPV+ OPC would have been stage IVA under the 7th edition but is now **stage II** under the 8th edition.

## AJCC 8th Edition: p16-Positive OPC — Pathological Staging

A separate pathological staging system applies to surgically treated p16-positive OPC:

### pN Classification (Pathological)

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| pN0 | No regional nodal metastasis |
| pN1 | Metastasis in ≤4 lymph nodes |
| pN2 | Metastasis in >4 lymph nodes |

**Key difference from clinical N staging**: Pathological N is based on **number of positive nodes**, not laterality or size. This reflects surgical-pathological data (Haughey 2016) showing that node count is the strongest prognostic factor in surgically treated patients.

### Pathological Stage Groups (p16-Positive)

| Stage | T | N | M |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I** | T0-T2 | pN0-pN1 | M0 |
| **II** | T0-T2 | pN2 | M0 |
| | T3-T4 | pN0-pN1 | M0 |
| **III** | T3-T4 | pN2 | M0 |
| **IV** | Any T | Any N | M1 |

**Note**: Pathological staging differs from clinical staging because different prognostic factors emerge after surgical resection. The pathological system was developed separately and validated by Haughey et al.

## AJCC 8th Edition: p16-Negative (HPV-Unassociated) OPC

Tumours that are **p16-negative** are staged using the **conventional HPV-negative staging system**, which is the same system applied to all other mucosal subsites of the head and neck (oral cavity, larynx, hypopharynx).

### T Classification (p16-Negative)

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| T1 | Tumour ≤2 cm |
| T2 | Tumour >2 cm and ≤4 cm |
| T3 | Tumour >4 cm or extension to lingual surface of epiglottis |
| T4a | Moderately advanced: invades larynx, extrinsic tongue muscles, medial pterygoid, hard palate, mandible |
| T4b | Very advanced: invades lateral pterygoid, pterygoid plates, lateral nasopharynx, skull base, or encases carotid artery |

### N Classification (p16-Negative)

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| N0 | No nodal metastasis |
| N1 | Single ipsilateral node ≤3 cm, no ENE |
| N2a | Single ipsilateral node >3 cm and ≤6 cm, no ENE |
| N2b | Multiple ipsilateral nodes ≤6 cm, no ENE |
| N2c | Bilateral/contralateral nodes ≤6 cm, no ENE |
| N3a | Any node >6 cm, no ENE |
| N3b | Any node(s) with clinical ENE |

**New in AJCC 8th edition for HPV-negative**: The addition of **N3b** for clinical ENE. [[Answers/General/Extranodal Extension|Extranodal extension]] upstages to N3b regardless of node size or number.

### Stage Groups (p16-Negative)

| Stage | T | N | M |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | T1 | N0 | M0 |
| II | T2 | N0 | M0 |
| III | T3 | N0 | M0 |
| | T1-T3 | N1 | M0 |
| IVA | T4a | N0-N1 | M0 |
| | T1-T4a | N2 | M0 |
| IVB | T4b | Any N | M0 |
| | Any T | N3 | M0 |
| IVC | Any T | Any N | M1 |

## Comparison: AJCC 7th vs 8th Edition

### Stage Distribution Changes

For a typical cohort of HPV-positive OPC patients:

| AJCC 7th Edition | AJCC 8th Edition (p16+) |
|---|---|
| Stage I: ~2% | Stage I: ~65% |
| Stage II: ~3% | Stage II: ~25% |
| Stage III: ~10% | Stage III: ~8% |
| Stage IVA: ~75% | Stage IV: ~2% (M1 only) |
| Stage IVB: ~8% | — |
| Stage IVC: ~2% | — |

The 8th edition achieves **far better prognostic separation** and appropriately downstages the majority of HPV+ patients.

### Prognostic Performance

Under the AJCC 8th edition (p16+ clinical staging):

| Stage | 5-year OS (approximate) |
|---|---|
| I | 90-95% |
| II | 80-85% |
| III | 65-75% |
| IV | 25-40% |

This provides meaningful prognostic discrimination, unlike the 7th edition where stages III and IVA had similar survival in HPV+ patients.

## Impact on Treatment Decisions

### What the Staging Change Does NOT Mean

1. **Stage I/II HPV+ is NOT "early" cancer requiring less aggressive treatment**: Many stage I patients under AJCC 8th edition had T2N2b disease under AJCC 7th edition and still require intensive treatment
2. **De-escalation should NOT be based on stage downstaging**: Treatment intensity should be guided by clinical trials, not by the new staging alone
3. **The standard of care has not changed**: Cisplatin-based CRT or primary surgery with adjuvant therapy remains standard

### What the Staging Change DOES Mean

1. **Better prognostic counselling**: Patients receive more accurate survival estimates
2. **Improved clinical trial stratification**: Trials can better stratify patients by risk
3. **Clearer identification of high-risk HPV+ patients**: Stage III-IV under the new system identifies patients with genuinely poor prognosis who may need intensified therapy
4. **Recognition of distinct biology**: Formal acknowledgment that HPV+ and HPV- OPC are different diseases

## Special Considerations

### Unknown Primary with p16-Positive Neck Nodes

- T0 category is included in the p16-positive staging system
- If p16-positive SCC is found in cervical lymph nodes and the primary is not identified, it is staged as T0 N(x) and uses the p16-positive staging system
- This reflects the high likelihood that the occult primary is in the oropharynx

### Non-SCC Histologies

- p16-positive staging applies **only** to squamous cell carcinoma (including basaloid, papillary, and spindle cell variants)
- Adenocarcinoma, mucoepidermoid carcinoma, and other non-SCC tumours of the oropharynx are staged separately

### Limitations of p16 as Sole Biomarker

- False-positive p16 can occur in non-HPV driven tumours (rare in oropharynx, more common at other sites)
- ~5-10% of p16-positive OPC may behave aggressively (heavy smokers, immunosuppressed)
- Future staging editions may incorporate additional biomarkers (e.g., circulating tumour HPV DNA, smoking status)

## Key Points for Exam

1. AJCC 8th edition introduced **separate staging** for p16-positive vs p16-negative OPC
2. p16 IHC (≥70% diffuse strong staining) is the defining biomarker; only valid for oropharyngeal SCC
3. p16+ clinical N staging is based on **laterality** (ipsilateral vs contralateral/bilateral) and **size >6 cm**
4. p16+ pathological N staging is based on **number of positive nodes** (≤4 vs >4)
5. There is **no stage IV for locoregional disease** in p16+ OPC; stage IV = distant metastasis only
6. ENE does **not** affect clinical N staging in p16+ OPC (unlike p16-negative where ENE → N3b)
7. T4 is **not subdivided** (no T4a/T4b) in p16+ staging
8. Stage distribution shifts dramatically: ~80% stage III-IV (7th ed) → ~65% stage I (8th ed) for HPV+ patients
9. New staging does **not** change treatment recommendations; de-escalation requires clinical trial evidence
10. The ICON-S study (O'Sullivan 2016) was the key validation study that formed the basis of the new system
