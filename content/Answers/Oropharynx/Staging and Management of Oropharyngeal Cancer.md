> [!cite] Landmark Articles
>
> Parsons et al. (2002) published a comprehensive analysis of treatment outcomes in oropharyngeal SCC comparing surgery with or without adjuvant radiation versus radiation alone with or without neck dissection, demonstrating equivalent locoregional control and survival but significantly more complications with surgery, supporting primary radiation for the majority of oropharyngeal cancers. Ang et al. (2010) published the landmark RTOG 0129 analysis establishing HPV status as a strong independent prognostic factor for survival in oropharyngeal cancer, with 58% reduction in risk of death for HPV-positive patients, forming the basis for risk stratification. Adelstein et al. (2003) published the intergroup phase III trial comparing standard radiation with two schedules of concurrent chemoradiotherapy in unresectable head and neck SCC, demonstrating superior survival with concurrent high-dose cisplatin and radiation. Calais et al. (1999) published the GORTEC randomized trial showing that concomitant cisplatin plus radiation significantly improved overall survival (51% vs 31% at 3 years) and locoregional control compared with radiation alone in advanced oropharyngeal carcinoma. Holsinger et al. (2005) described the transoral lateral oropharyngectomy technique for tonsillar SCC with detailed analysis of functional results and oncologic outcomes, establishing the transoral approach as a viable alternative to open mandibulotomy.

## Anatomy of the Oropharynx

### Boundaries

| Boundary | Landmark |
|---|---|
| Superior | Hard-soft palate junction, superior surface of soft palate |
| Inferior | Plane of hyoid bone; valleculae (pharyngoepiglottic folds) |
| Anterior | Circumvallate papillae (tongue base), anterior tonsillar pillar |
| Posterior | Posterior pharyngeal wall, prevertebral fascia |
| Lateral | Tonsillar fossae, palatopharyngeal arches |

### Subsites (AJCC)

1. **Base of tongue (BOT)** -- posterior one-third of tongue, from circumvallate papillae to valleculae; includes lingual tonsils
2. **Tonsillar region** -- palatine tonsil, tonsillar fossa, tonsillar pillars (anterior and posterior)
3. **Soft palate** -- includes uvula; superior surface only (inferior surface = nasopharynx)
4. **Posterior pharyngeal wall** -- from level of soft palate superiorly to level of hyoid bone inferiorly

### Lymphatic Drainage

| Subsite | Primary Nodal Levels | Frequency of Nodal Metastasis |
|---|---|---|
| Tonsil | II, III (ipsilateral) | 60--75% |
| BOT | II, III (bilateral) | 70--85% |
| Soft palate | II, retropharyngeal | 40--50% |
| Posterior wall | II, retropharyngeal (bilateral) | 50--65% |

- Bilateral drainage is common, especially for **BOT** and **midline** tumours
- Retropharyngeal nodes (of Rouviere) are involved in 15--20% of cases, particularly posterior wall and BOT tumours

## Staging -- AJCC 8th Edition

The AJCC 8th edition introduced **separate staging systems** for p16-positive and p16-negative oropharyngeal SCC, reflecting the dramatically different prognosis.

### p16-Positive (HPV-Mediated) OPSCC -- Clinical Staging

| T Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| T0 | No primary identified |
| T1 | <=2 cm |
| T2 | >2 cm to <=4 cm |
| T3 | >4 cm or extension to lingual surface of epiglottis |
| T4 | Moderately advanced: invasion of larynx, extrinsic tongue muscles, medial pterygoid, hard palate, mandible, or beyond |

| N Stage (Clinical) | Definition |
|---|---|
| N0 | No regional lymph node metastasis |
| N1 | One or more ipsilateral nodes, none >6 cm |
| N2 | Contralateral or bilateral nodes, none >6 cm |
| N3 | Any node >6 cm |

| Overall Stage | Grouping |
|---|---|
| Stage I | T0-T2, N0-N1 |
| Stage II | T0-T2 N2; T3 N0-N2 |
| Stage III | T4 or N3; T0-T4 N3 |

### p16-Negative OPSCC -- Uses Standard AJCC 8th Staging

Identical to prior AJCC 7th edition staging with T4a/T4b distinction and more detailed N staging (N1, N2a/b/c, N3a/b).

## Workup

### Clinical Assessment

- **History** -- dysphagia, odynophagia, otalgia (referred via CN IX/X), voice change, weight loss, tobacco/alcohol use, sexual history (HPV risk)
- **Physical examination** -- oral cavity inspection, bimanual palpation of tongue base, neck palpation, flexible nasopharyngolaryngoscopy
- **Examination under anaesthesia (EUA)** -- essential for accurate assessment of extent, especially BOT tumours; combined with biopsy

### Imaging

| Modality | Role |
|---|---|
| Contrast-enhanced CT | Bone invasion, nodal staging, chest staging |
| MRI with gadolinium | Soft tissue extent (BOT, tongue base invasion, perineural spread) |
| PET-CT (FDG) | Distant metastasis screening, unknown primary, post-treatment surveillance |
| Chest CT | Lung metastases, second primary |
| Panoramic radiograph | Mandible invasion assessment |

### Biopsy

- Direct biopsy of visible lesion (punch or incisional)
- Fine-needle aspiration cytology (FNAC) of neck nodes
- **p16 IHC** on all oropharyngeal SCC specimens -- see [[Answers/Oropharynx/HPV Testing in Oropharyngeal Cancer|HPV Testing in OPC]]
- Core biopsy if FNAC non-diagnostic

### Essential Investigations

- Full blood count, liver and renal function, electrolytes
- Nutritional assessment (albumin, prealbumin, weight loss percentage)
- Dental assessment (pre-radiation dental clearance if RT planned)
- Swallowing assessment (baseline MBS/FEES) -- see [[Answers/Oropharynx/Swallowing Rehabilitation in Oropharyngeal Cancer|Swallowing Rehabilitation]]
- Speech-language pathology consultation
- MDT discussion -- mandatory for all cases

## Treatment Algorithm by Stage

### Early-Stage OPSCC (T1-T2, N0-N1)

**Option 1: Primary Radiation Therapy**
- Definitive RT (66--70 Gy in 33--35 fractions) to primary + elective bilateral neck
- IMRT preferred to reduce toxicity to parotid, contralateral structures, and DARS
- 5-year local control: 80--90% for T1-T2
- Advantages: organ preservation, avoidance of surgical morbidity

**Option 2: Primary Surgery (Transoral)**
- Transoral robotic surgery (TORS) or transoral laser microsurgery (TLM)
- Appropriate for accessible T1-T2 tumours of tonsil and BOT
- Selective neck dissection (levels II-IV) ipsilateral +/- contralateral
- Adjuvant RT or CRT based on pathological risk factors

**Decision Factors:**
| Factor | Favours RT | Favours Surgery |
|---|---|---|
| Tumour location | Posterior wall, soft palate | Tonsil, lateral BOT |
| Access | Poor transoral access | Good transoral exposure |
| Functional outcome | Similar | Potentially superior without adjuvant RT |
| Patient factors | Comorbidities limiting surgery | Young patient, prior RT field |
| HPV status | Either | Either |

### Advanced-Stage OPSCC (T3-T4, N2-N3)

**Standard of Care: Concurrent Chemoradiotherapy (CRT)**
- Cisplatin 100 mg/m2 days 1, 22, 43 + definitive RT 70 Gy
- Alternative: weekly cisplatin 40 mg/m2 (inferior evidence but commonly used)
- Cetuximab + RT is **NOT** equivalent to cisplatin + RT (De-ESCALaTE, RTOG 1016)

**Surgery + Adjuvant RT/CRT:**
- Reserved for selected cases requiring mandibulotomy or with bone invasion
- Composite resection + reconstruction + adjuvant treatment
- Pathological risk factors guiding adjuvant therapy:
  - **Adjuvant RT alone:** close margins, pT3-T4, pN2+, perineural invasion, lymphovascular invasion
  - **Adjuvant CRT:** positive margins, [[Answers/General/Extranodal Extension|extranodal extension]] (ENE)

### Role of Induction Chemotherapy

- **Not standard of care** for oropharyngeal cancer
- TPF (docetaxel, cisplatin, 5-FU) regimen studied in sequential induction + CRT
- Potential role in organ preservation for very advanced/unresectable disease
- May allow response assessment and risk stratification
- Higher toxicity when added to definitive CRT
- **Not recommended** as routine approach (NCCN, ESMO guidelines)

## Surgical Approaches

### Transoral Approaches

**1. Transoral Robotic Surgery (TORS)**
- da Vinci robotic system; 3D magnified visualization
- Indications: T1-T2 tonsil, BOT, soft palate; selected T3
- Advantages: magnified view, articulating instruments, minimal access morbidity
- Contraindications: trismus, limited mouth opening, mandible in path, carotid involvement

**2. Transoral Laser Microsurgery (TLM)**
- CO2 laser via direct laryngoscope or oral retractor
- Established for tonsil and BOT tumours
- En-bloc or piecemeal resection with margin assessment

**3. Transoral Lateral Oropharyngectomy (Holsinger technique)**
- Open transoral approach without mandibulotomy
- Lateral oropharyngeal wall resection including tonsil, tonsillar fossa, soft palate margin
- Superior functional outcomes compared with mandibulotomy approaches

### Open Surgical Approaches

**1. Lateral Pharyngotomy**
- Transcervical approach through thyrohyoid membrane
- Access to BOT, valleculae, and piriform sinus

**2. Mandibulotomy (Lip-Split Mandibulotomy)**
- Midline or paramedian mandibular osteotomy to swing mandible laterally
- Wide exposure of entire oropharynx, BOT, parapharyngeal space
- Indications: T3-T4 tumours, limited transoral access, bone invasion
- Morbidity: mandibular non-union, malocclusion, inferior alveolar nerve injury

**3. Visor Flap / Suprahyoid Pharyngotomy**
- Lower lip degloving approach
- Access to anterior BOT and floor of mouth

### Mandibular Management

| Scenario | Approach |
|---|---|
| No mandible involvement | Transoral or pull-through |
| Periosteal involvement only | [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Marginal Mandibulectomy|Marginal mandibulectomy]] |
| Cortical bone invasion | Segmental mandibulectomy + reconstruction |
| Mandibulotomy for access | Paramedian osteotomy with plate fixation |

## Neck Management

### Clinically Node-Positive Neck

- **Comprehensive neck dissection** (levels I-V) if primary surgery performed
- Modified radical neck dissection (MRND) -- preserve CN XI, IJV, SCM when possible
- If primary CRT: planned neck dissection for residual disease (PET-CT guided)

### Clinically Node-Negative Neck

- **Elective treatment** of at least ipsilateral levels II-IV is recommended for all oropharyngeal SCC (high rate of occult nodal metastasis)
- **Bilateral neck treatment** recommended for:
  - BOT tumours (bilateral drainage)
  - Midline or crossing-midline tumours
  - Soft palate tumours
  - Posterior pharyngeal wall tumours

### Post-CRT Neck Management

- PET-CT at 12 weeks post-CRT
- Complete metabolic response --> observation with close surveillance
- Incomplete response or residual mass --> salvage neck dissection
- HPV-positive tumours have higher rates of complete nodal response

## HPV-Based Risk Stratification and De-escalation

### Ang Risk Classification (RTOG 0129)

| Risk Group | Criteria | 3-Year OS |
|---|---|---|
| Low risk | HPV+, <=10 pack-year smoking OR HPV+, >10 pack-year + N0-N2a | 93% |
| Intermediate risk | HPV+, >10 pack-year + N2b-N3 OR HPV-, <=10 pack-year + T2-T3 | 68% |
| High risk | HPV-, >10 pack-year OR HPV-, T4 | 46% |

### Active De-escalation Trials

- **ECOG 3311** -- reduced adjuvant RT dose after TORS
- **OPTIMA** -- response-adapted therapy based on induction chemotherapy
- **MC1273** -- reduced RT dose for HPV+ low-risk
- **NRG HN002** -- reduced dose RT with or without cisplatin

**Current Standard:** De-escalation should **only** occur within clinical trials. Standard cisplatin + RT remains the standard of care outside trials.

## Post-Treatment Surveillance

| Time Point | Assessment |
|---|---|
| First 2 years | Every 1--3 months: clinical exam, flexible scope |
| Years 3--5 | Every 3--6 months |
| After 5 years | Annually |
| Baseline post-treatment | PET-CT at 12 weeks; MRI or CT at 3--6 months |
| Thyroid function | TSH every 6--12 months (post-RT) |
| Dental follow-up | Regular (fluoride trays, ORN prevention) |
| Swallowing | Ongoing rehabilitation and monitoring |

## Salvage Surgery

- Reserved for local or regional recurrence after definitive RT/CRT
- Higher complication rates in irradiated field
- Requires careful patient selection (resectability, performance status, comorbidities)
- Often requires free flap reconstruction -- see [[Answers/Oropharynx/Pharyngeal Reconstruction|Pharyngeal Reconstruction]]
- Salvage TORS emerging as option for selected recurrences
- Survival after salvage: 25--40% 5-year OS for resectable recurrence

## Outcomes by Subsite and HPV Status

| Subsite | 5-Year OS (HPV+) | 5-Year OS (HPV-) |
|---|---|---|
| Tonsil | 75--85% | 40--55% |
| Base of tongue | 70--80% | 35--50% |
| Soft palate | 65--75% | 30--45% |
| Posterior wall | 55--65% | 25--40% |

## Clinical Pearls for Examinations

1. **AJCC 8th edition** mandates separate staging for p16+ and p16- OPSCC -- know both systems
2. **Concurrent cisplatin + RT is standard** for advanced OPSCC -- cetuximab is NOT equivalent (De-ESCALaTE, RTOG 1016)
3. **TORS/TLM** is increasingly used for early-stage OPSCC and may reduce the need for adjuvant therapy if pathology is favourable
4. **BOT tumours require bilateral neck treatment** due to rich bilateral lymphatic drainage
5. **HPV-positive OPSCC** has dramatically better outcomes -- but de-escalation outside trials is premature
6. **Parsons 2002** supports primary RT for majority of OPSCC -- equivalent control with fewer complications than surgery
7. **Retropharyngeal nodes** must be included in RT fields for posterior wall and advanced BOT tumours
