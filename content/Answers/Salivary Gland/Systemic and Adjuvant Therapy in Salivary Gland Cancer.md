> [!cite] Landmark Articles
>
> Locati et al. (2016) conducted a retrospective series demonstrating clinical activity of androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) in 17 patients with metastatic or relapsed androgen receptor-positive salivary gland cancers, achieving an objective response rate of 64.7%, firmly establishing ADT as a first-line systemic option in AR-positive disease. Fushimi, Tada et al. (2018) published the first prospective phase II study of combined androgen blockade (leuprorelin + bicalutamide) in 36 patients with AR-positive metastatic or locally advanced unresectable salivary gland carcinoma, demonstrating a clinical benefit rate of 72% and median progression-free survival of 8.8 months, confirming single-institution findings in a structured trial. Takahashi et al. (2019) reported a phase II trial of trastuzumab and docetaxel in 57 patients with HER2-positive salivary duct carcinoma, achieving a confirmed overall response rate of 70.2% with manageable toxicity — establishing trastuzumab-based HER2-directed therapy as the standard for this molecular subgroup. Cohen et al. (2018) reported findings from the phase Ib KEYNOTE-028 cohort of pembrolizumab in 26 patients with advanced PD-L1-positive salivary gland carcinoma, demonstrating a 12% objective response rate and 58% disease control rate — confirming limited but present activity for PD-1 blockade in unselected salivary cancer. Rossi and Faquin (2018) formalised the Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytopathology (MSRSGC) as an international standardisation effort that now frames cytopathological reporting for all major and minor salivary gland lesions, directly underpinning treatment allocation decisions.

## Systemic and Adjuvant Therapy in Salivary Gland Cancer

### Principles of Adjuvant Treatment

Surgery remains the primary treatment for all resectable salivary gland malignancies. Adjuvant therapy is guided by pathological risk features identified on the resection specimen. Because no large randomised controlled trials exist specifically for salivary gland cancers, adjuvant indications are extrapolated from RTOG 9501 and EORTC 22931 data for head and neck cancers, combined with salivary-specific retrospective series (Shah, Ch 14).

### Indications for Adjuvant Radiotherapy

| Risk Feature | Adjuvant RT Recommended | Grade |
|---|---|---|
| T3 or T4 primary | Yes | Strong |
| High-grade histology (MEC grade 3, SDC, carcinoma ex PA) | Yes | Strong |
| Perineural invasion (PNI) — named nerve or extensive small-nerve | Yes | Strong |
| Lymphovascular invasion (LVI) | Yes | Moderate |
| Extranodal extension (ENE) of nodal metastasis | Yes (consider CRT) | Strong |
| Close margin (<5 mm) or positive margin | Yes | Strong |
| Deep lobe involvement or bone erosion | Yes | Strong |
| N2 or N3 nodal disease | Yes | Strong |
| Recurrent disease | Yes | Strong |
| ACC (all stages) — long slow repopulation kinetics | Yes (even T1–2, N0) | Strong |

**Note:** Adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC) is treated with adjuvant RT even in early-stage disease due to the near-universal risk of perineural spread, slow repopulation, and late distant relapse. Perineural tracking should be treated to the skull base along the named nerve.

### Radiotherapy Dose and Technique

- **Standard adjuvant dose:** 60–66 Gy in 30–33 fractions using IMRT
- **Positive margin / gross residual disease:** 66 Gy; boost to 70 Gy if feasible
- **ACC — primary or adjuvant:** 66 Gy+; consider neutron therapy or proton therapy at centres with access (superior local control for ACC in skull-base disease)
- **Perineural spread (PNS) tracking:** Clinical target volume extended along the relevant named nerve (e.g., facial nerve, auriculotemporal nerve) to the skull base foramen; include pterygopalatine fossa for palatine nerve tracking in palate ACC
- **Neck:** Elective nodal irradiation (levels I–V ipsilateral) for high-grade histology even with N0 neck (occult node rate 20–40% in high-grade tumours)
- **Concurrent chemotherapy:** Indicated when positive margins and/or ENE present, using cisplatin 100 mg/m² every 3 weeks (extrapolated from RTOG 9501 / EORTC 22931)

### Molecular Biology and Targeted Therapy by Histological Subtype

#### Salivary Duct Carcinoma (SDC)

SDC is the most molecularly actionable salivary malignancy. It shares molecular features with apocrine breast cancer and high-grade urothelial carcinoma.

**Key molecular alterations:**

| Target | Frequency | Therapeutic Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Androgen receptor (AR) overexpression | 70–90% | ADT first-line in recurrent/metastatic disease |
| HER2 amplification/overexpression | ~40% | Trastuzumab + docetaxel; pertuzumab combination |
| PTEN loss → PI3K/AKT activation | ~30% | PI3K inhibitors (investigational) |
| TP53 mutation | ~60% | No direct target; prognostic marker |
| HRAS mutation | ~15% | Tipifarnib (farnesyl transferase inhibitor) |
| MDM2 amplification | Present in CXPA component | MDM2 inhibitors (investigational) |

**Treatment sequence for recurrent/metastatic SDC:**
1. **AR-positive, HER2-negative:** Combined androgen blockade (CAB) — leuprorelin (LHRH agonist) + bicalutamide. Enzalutamide (Alliance A091404 phase II) or apalutamide are emerging alternatives.
2. **AR-positive, HER2-positive:** Anti-HER2 therapy (trastuzumab + docetaxel) ± androgen blockade. Some protocols use docetaxel + trastuzumab + pertuzumab (DTP) as first-line.
3. **EORTC 1206 (HNCG/UKCRN):** Randomised phase II comparing chemotherapy vs. ADT in AR-positive recurrent/metastatic SGC — confirmed ADT non-inferior to chemotherapy with better tolerability.
4. **Platinum-based chemotherapy** (cisplatin + doxorubicin; or gemcitabine + cisplatin): Response rates 15–30%, no OS benefit; reserved for AR/HER2-negative disease or progression on targeted therapy.

#### Mucoepidermoid Carcinoma (MEC)

| Molecular Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| CRTC1-MAML2 fusion (t11;19) | Present in 50–60%; low- and intermediate-grade MEC; associated with better prognosis |
| CRTC3-MAML2 fusion | Present in ~5%; high-grade MEC |
| EGFR overexpression | ~30–40% high-grade MEC |
| HER2 amplification | ~10–20% high-grade |

- **Low-grade MEC with CRTC1-MAML2:** Excellent prognosis; surgery ± RT only; chemotherapy rarely needed
- **High-grade MEC:** Treat as per other high-grade salivary cancers; EGFR-targeted therapy (cetuximab) has limited but reported activity; platinum chemotherapy for metastatic disease

#### Adenoid Cystic Carcinoma (ACC)

ACC is characterised by slow indolent growth, relentless perineural spread, late distant metastases, and a near-complete absence of approved targeted therapy despite extensive trial activity.

**Key molecular alterations:**

| Target | Frequency | Status |
|---|---|---|
| MYB-NFIB fusion (t6;9) | 60–80% | No approved targeted therapy; transcriptional regulation |
| MYBL1 rearrangements | ~10% | |
| NOTCH1/2 activation | ~15% | NOTCH inhibitors (RO4929097 — phase II; modest activity) |
| FGF/FGFR pathway activation | ~10% | FGFR inhibitors (investigational) |
| c-Kit (CD117) expression | >80% | Imatinib — disappointing; no activating KIT mutations |
| VEGFR pathway | — | Lenvatinib, axitinib, sunitinib, regorafenib (antiangiogenic activity) |

**Treatment of metastatic ACC:**
- Observation for asymptomatic slow-growing pulmonary metastases (no benefit of chemotherapy over watchful waiting in stable disease)
- Lenvatinib (VEGFR multikinase inhibitor): Phase II data show partial response ~11%, stable disease ~75%; PFS benefit
- Axitinib, regorafenib: Similar activity profiles
- Platinum chemotherapy (cisplatin + doxorubicin): 15–25% response rate; short duration
- Immunotherapy: Limited activity in unselected ACC; high TMB or MSI-H patients may benefit
- Clinical trials are strongly preferred for all recurrent/metastatic ACC

#### Secretory Carcinoma (Formerly Mammary Analogue Secretory Carcinoma — MASC)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| ETV6-NTRK3 fusion | ~90% of secretory carcinomas — diagnostic and therapeutic target |
| ETV6-RET, ETV6-MET fusions | Rare alternative fusions (~10%) |
| Immunophenotype | S100+, mammaglobin+, GCDFP-15+, GATA3+ |

**TRK inhibitors (larotrectinib, entrectinib):** Tumour-agnostic approval for NTRK fusion-positive cancers. In secretory carcinoma with ETV6-NTRK3 fusion, response rates are markedly high (>75% in published case series). NTRK fusion testing (FISH, NGS, or pan-TRK IHC as screen) is mandatory for all secretory carcinoma at diagnosis. This represents the most actionable molecular target in salivary gland oncology.

#### Carcinoma Ex Pleomorphic Adenoma (CXPA)

- CXPA behaviour depends on the malignant component: usually SDC, adenocarcinoma NOS, or MEC
- MDM2 amplification common (especially with de-differentiation)
- Treat the malignant component as that histological entity (e.g., SDC component → AR/HER2 testing → ADT or trastuzumab)

### Hormone Receptors in Salivary Gland Tumours

> [!warning] Examiner High-Yield: Hormone receptor expression in salivary gland tumours is a common MCh question especially regarding actionability.

| Receptor | Tumour | Frequency | Clinical Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Androgen receptor (AR)** | Salivary duct carcinoma | 70–90% | Actionable — ADT is first-line treatment in metastatic/recurrent AR+ SDC |
| AR | Mammary analogue secretory carcinoma | ~30% | Not primary driver; NTRK targeted preferred |
| AR | ACC | ~15–25% | Limited data; not routinely used as target |
| **Oestrogen receptor (ER)** | ACC | ~30–40% | Not clinically actionable; ER expression is non-functional (lacks ER-α transcriptional activity in most) |
| ER | Other salivary tumours | <10% | No clinical significance |
| **Progesterone receptor (PR)** | Salivary gland tumours | Very low (<10%) | No clinical significance |
| **HER2** | Salivary duct carcinoma | ~40% | Actionable — trastuzumab ± docetaxel, pertuzumab |
| HER2 | CXPA | ~30% | Actionable in HER2-amplified cases |
| HER2 | MEC (high-grade) | ~10–20% | Emerging data; clinical trials |
| **c-Kit (CD117)** | ACC | >80% | Expressed but NO activating mutations — imatinib did not show benefit |
| **EGFR** | MEC, SDC | 30–40% | Limited activity with cetuximab; not standard of care |

**Key Teaching Point — ER/PR in Salivary Glands:**
ER expression in ACC (~30–40%) was initially exciting. However, clinical trials of tamoxifen and other anti-oestrogenic agents have shown no benefit. The ER expressed in ACC is an isoform that lacks the ligand-binding domain (ER-β or truncated ER-α), and therefore anti-oestrogen therapy has no therapeutic role. This is a common exam trap: ER is positive but is NOT a treatment target in salivary gland cancer.

### Palliative Systemic Chemotherapy

Used for recurrent/metastatic disease not amenable to targeted therapy:

| Regimen | Histology | ORR | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cisplatin + doxorubicin | All subtypes | 15–30% | Classical regimen; no OS benefit |
| Gemcitabine + cisplatin | All subtypes | ~35% | Better tolerated; increasing use |
| Carboplatin + paclitaxel | All subtypes | 25–30% | Used in cisplatin-ineligible patients |
| Vinorelbine | ACC | ~10% | Modest activity |

### Emerging and Investigational Therapies

| Agent | Target | Relevant Subtype | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Larotrectinib / entrectinib | NTRK fusion | Secretory carcinoma (ETV6-NTRK3) | Approved (tumour-agnostic); high response rates |
| Enzalutamide / apalutamide | AR | SDC (AR-positive) | Phase II data; replacing bicalutamide |
| Trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) | HER2 | SDC (HER2-positive) | Phase I/II — high activity in HER2+ SGC |
| Lenvatinib | VEGFR1-3, FGFR, RET | ACC | Phase II — modest PFS benefit |
| NOTCH inhibitors (RO4929097) | NOTCH1/2 | ACC | Phase II — limited single-agent activity |
| Pembrolizumab | PD-1/PD-L1 | High TMB, MSI-H, or PD-L1+ | Limited activity in unselected; KEYNOTE-028 ORR 12% |
| Nivolumab + ipilimumab | PD-1 + CTLA-4 | High-grade salivary cancers | Phase II — low ORR; select patients with high TMB may benefit |
| Tipifarnib | HRAS-mutant | HRAS-mutant SGC | Phase II — meaningful activity in HRAS Q61-mutant SGC |

### Summary: Adjuvant Treatment Algorithm

```
Resected Salivary Gland Malignancy
          |
          ↓
Assess Risk Features (margins, grade, PNI, LVI, ENE, T-stage)
          |
     ┌────┴────┐
  Low-risk       High-risk
 (T1-2, low-     (T3-T4, high-grade, PNI,
  grade, R0)      LVI, ENE, close/+margin)
     |                      |
  Observation            Adjuvant RT
                         60–66 Gy IMRT
                              |
                    ┌─────────┴──────────┐
               RT alone              RT + Cisplatin
           (high-grade, PNI,      (positive margins,
            LVI, T3-T4,           ENE — strongest
            no ENE/margin+)       indications)
```

For ACC specifically: adjuvant RT even for T1–2 N0 if complete excision, due to perineural spread and late failure patterns. Neutron beam or proton therapy at specialised centres for unresectable or close-margin ACC.

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Adjuvant radiotherapy is indicated for all high-risk salivary gland malignancies — T3–T4, high-grade, PNI, LVI, ENE, positive or close margins — delivered as 60–66 Gy IMRT. ACC requires adjuvant RT at all stages due to perineural tropism and perineural tracking should be treated to the skull base. Systemic therapy in salivary gland cancer is now histology- and molecular-subtype-driven: AR-positive SDC receives combined androgen blockade, HER2-positive SDC receives trastuzumab-based therapy, and ETV6-NTRK3-positive secretory carcinoma is treated with TRK inhibitors. ER and PR are expressed in some salivary tumours (ER ~30–40% in ACC) but are NOT clinically actionable — anti-oestrogen therapy shows no benefit. c-Kit is highly expressed in ACC but imatinib failed due to absence of activating mutations.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. Adjuvant RT is the workhorse of salivary gland cancer management; no salivary-specific randomised CRT data exists — extrapolation from RTOG 9501/EORTC 22931 is accepted practice.
> 2. ACC must receive adjuvant RT even in T1 N0 disease — slow repopulation and perineural spread make it unique among salivary cancers.
> 3. ACC perineural tracking: always trace the involved nerve to the skull base foramen on imaging and include this in the CTV.
> 4. SDC is the most targetable salivary cancer: AR (70–90%) and HER2 (40%) — always test both at diagnosis of SDC.
> 5. Combined androgen blockade (LHRH agonist + bicalutamide/enzalutamide) is standard first-line for AR-positive recurrent/metastatic SDC — ORR ~50–65%.
> 6. Trastuzumab + docetaxel achieves ~70% ORR in HER2-positive SDC — the highest response rate seen in any salivary cancer systemic trial.
> 7. ETV6-NTRK3 fusion in secretory carcinoma is the most actionable fusion — larotrectinib/entrectinib achieve >75% ORR; NTRK testing is mandatory.
> 8. ER positivity in ACC is a classic exam trap — it is NOT actionable; the expressed ER lacks functional ligand-binding domain.
> 9. c-Kit expression in ACC (>80%) is a known fact — but imatinib showed no activity because there are NO activating c-Kit mutations in ACC.
> 10. Immunotherapy (pembrolizumab) has limited activity in unselected salivary cancers (KEYNOTE-028 ORR 12%); high TMB or MSI-H tumours may benefit — routine PD-L1 testing is increasingly recommended.
