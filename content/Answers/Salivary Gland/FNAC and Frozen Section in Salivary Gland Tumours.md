> Layfield et al. (2018) introduced the Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytopathology (MSRSGC), a standardised six-category classification system analogous to the Bethesda System for thyroid and the Paris System for urinary tract. The system provides category-specific risk of malignancy estimates and management guidance, improving inter-institutional communication and clinical decision-making. Brennan et al. (2004) published a systematic review of 6,249 FNAC cases from 64 studies in the *Head & Neck* journal, reporting overall sensitivity of 86%, specificity of 97%, and accuracy of 90% for distinguishing benign from malignant salivary gland lesions, establishing FNAC as the standard pre-operative investigation. Griffith et al. (2015) validated the Milan System in a multi-institutional study, demonstrating that the risk of malignancy across six categories ranged from 3.8% (non-neoplastic) to 96.3% (malignant), supporting its use for standardised reporting. Sclafani et al. (1989) evaluated intraoperative frozen section in parotid surgery and found a sensitivity of 84% and a false-negative rate of 14–16% for detecting malignancy, concluding that frozen section is a useful but imperfect adjunct and should not replace the pre-operative diagnosis.

## Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC) in Salivary Gland Tumours

### Rationale and Indications

FNAC is the **first-line investigation for all palpable salivary gland masses**. It guides surgical planning — specifically, whether to observe, perform limited excision, or plan formal parotidectomy with neck dissection. Open or incisional biopsy of the parotid is **absolutely contraindicated** due to:
- Risk to the facial nerve during unplanned biopsy
- Capsule disruption of pleomorphic adenoma → tumour seeding → recurrence
- Potential seeding of malignancy along biopsy tract

### Technique

| Approach | Details |
|---|---|
| **Freehand FNAC** | Palpable superficial masses; 23-25G needle, 10mL syringe, 2-4 passes |
| **Ultrasound-guided FNAC** | Deep, poorly defined, or small lesions; reduces inadequate samples by 30-40% |
| **Core needle biopsy** | CT/US-guided; used when FNAC non-diagnostic; provides architecture for histological interpretation; 18G spring-loaded needle |

Multiple passes improve adequacy. A **cell block** should be prepared when possible to allow immunohistochemistry (IHC) for indeterminate lesions.

### Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytopathology (MSRSGC 2018)

| Category | Label | Risk of Malignancy | Recommended Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | **Non-diagnostic** | ~25% | Repeat FNAC (± US guidance) |
| II | **Non-neoplastic** | ~3% | Clinical follow-up; surgery if clinical concern |
| III | **Atypia of Undetermined Significance (AUS)** | ~20% | Repeat FNAC or surgical excision |
| IVA | **Neoplasm — Benign** | ~2% | Surgical excision (parotidectomy) |
| IVB | **Salivary Gland Neoplasm of Uncertain Malignant Potential (SUMP)** | ~35% | Surgical excision with appropriate planning |
| V | **Suspicious for Malignancy** | ~60% | Surgery + consider neck and adjuvant planning |
| VI | **Malignant** | ~96% | Definitive surgical management |

> [!note] Milan vs Prior Reporting
> The previous binary benign/malignant system was replaced by MSRSGC because many lesions cannot reliably be classified without histology. The SUMP category captures basaloid tumours (oncocytoma, basal cell adenoma, ACC) that are cytologically overlapping. This category mandates excision with full surgical planning rather than simple enucleation.

### Cytological Patterns of Common Salivary Tumours

| Tumour | Cytological Features |
|---|---|
| **Pleomorphic adenoma** | Epithelial cells in sheets + chondromyxoid stroma (metachromatic on Diff-Quik); biphasic |
| **Warthin tumour** | Oncocytic cells + dense lymphoid background + lipid-laden macrophages; characteristic |
| **Mucoepidermoid carcinoma** | Mucous cells + epidermoid cells + intermediate cells; low-grade may mimic benign; difficult to grade |
| **Adenoid cystic carcinoma** | Hyaline cylinders (pink basement membrane globules), basaloid cells surrounding globules; characteristic but can mimic pleomorphic adenoma |
| **Acinic cell carcinoma** | Serous-appearing cells with zymogen granules; granular cytoplasm; microcystic pattern |
| **Salivary duct carcinoma** | Pleomorphic cells with necrosis, comedonecrosis; high-grade; apocrine features |

### Limitations of FNAC

1. **Cannot assess capsular invasion**: Cannot distinguish pleomorphic adenoma from carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma (CXPA) — requires histology with intact capsule
2. **Cannot reliably grade**: MEC grading (low vs intermediate vs high) requires architectural pattern assessment
3. **Sampling error**: Cystic lesions (Warthin, low-grade MEC) may yield only fluid
4. **Expertise-dependent**: Requires dedicated salivary cytopathologist for optimal interpretation
5. **Indeterminate rate**: 15–20% of cases fall into AUS/SUMP categories, requiring further investigation

## Frozen Section (FS) in Parotid Surgery

### Indications

Frozen section is used intraoperatively to:

1. **Confirm malignancy** when pre-operative diagnosis is inconclusive or absent — guides extent of surgery (superficial vs total parotidectomy, neck dissection, nerve sacrifice)
2. **Assess surgical margins** in known malignancy — soft tissue and nerve margins
3. **Evaluate facial nerve involvement** — presence of tumour in perineural space
4. **Assess lymph nodes** during neck dissection — macroscopic metastasis detection

### Accuracy of Frozen Section

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Sensitivity for malignancy | ~84–87% |
| Specificity | ~97–99% |
| False-negative rate | 13–16% |
| False-positive rate | <2% |
| Accuracy for subtype determination | ~70% (lower — many subtypes overlap on FS) |

### Limitations and Pitfalls

| Limitation | Reason | Clinical Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **PA vs CXPA** | FS cannot examine entire capsule | Cannot exclude malignant transformation; requires permanent sections |
| **Low-grade MEC vs mucocele/Warthin** | Mucous cells and cystic change overlap | Risk of under-diagnosis |
| **ACC grade** | Solid vs cribriform requires full section | May underestimate aggressiveness |
| **Frozen artefact** | Ice crystal artefact distorts cytology | False positives possible |
| **Adequacy** | Small biopsies, fatty tissue | Non-diagnostic result |

### Shah's Principle on Frozen Section

Shah's textbook emphasises that **the decision regarding extent of parotid surgery should primarily be based on preoperative workup** (clinical, imaging, FNAC), not on intraoperative frozen section alone. This is because:
- FS has a 14–16% false-negative rate
- A "benign" FS in the presence of clinical features of malignancy (facial palsy, stone-hard mass, fixation) must not reassure the surgeon to perform a lesser operation
- FS is most valuable for **margin assessment** rather than primary diagnosis determination

### Intraoperative Decision Algorithm

```
Preoperative diagnosis BENIGN (e.g. PA on FNAC + MRI)
  → Superficial parotidectomy
  → FS if surface looks atypical or CXPA features
  → FS benign → complete superficial parotidectomy
  → FS malignant → convert to total + possible neck + nerve decisions

Preoperative diagnosis MALIGNANT (e.g. MEC on FNAC)
  → Total conservative parotidectomy planned
  → FS for margins during excision
  → FS of facial nerve margin if close

Preoperative diagnosis INDETERMINATE (SUMP/AUS)
  → Superficial parotidectomy
  → FS to determine extent of further resection
```

### Complementary Role of MRI

Modern MRI can reduce over-reliance on frozen section by providing preoperative characterisation:

| MRI Feature | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| **T2 high signal, well-defined** | Pleomorphic adenoma or Warthin |
| **T2 low signal ("dark on T2")** | ACC — dense fibrous stroma, poor T2 relaxation |
| **DWI — ADC < 1.0 × 10⁻³ mm²/s** | Malignant (restricted diffusion) |
| **DWI — ADC > 1.2 × 10⁻³ mm²/s** | Benign |
| **Gadolinium perineural enhancement** | ACC with PNI — track to skull base |
| **Flow voids / heterogeneous T1** | Vascular lesion (paraganglioma) or haemorrhage |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> FNAC using the Milan System is the standard pre-operative investigation for all salivary gland masses, with sensitivity ~86% and specificity ~97% for malignancy. The six MSRSGC categories each carry a defined risk of malignancy and management recommendation, replacing older binary reporting. Frozen section is a valuable intraoperative adjunct for margin assessment but has a 14–16% false-negative rate for malignancy diagnosis and cannot distinguish PA from CXPA — surgical planning should be based primarily on preoperative workup. Open incisional biopsy is contraindicated for parotid masses due to facial nerve risk and tumour seeding.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. **FNAC is the first-line investigation** for all salivary gland masses — incisional biopsy is absolutely contraindicated for parotid masses
> 2. **Milan System (MSRSGC) 2018** is the standard reporting system — 6 categories, each with defined risk of malignancy; SUMP (~35% risk) mandates surgical excision with full planning
> 3. **US-guided FNAC** improves adequacy from 75–85% to 90–95%; use for deep, small, or complex lesions
> 4. **FNAC cannot diagnose CXPA** — it cannot assess capsular integrity; only permanent histology with intact capsule can exclude malignant transformation of pleomorphic adenoma
> 5. **"Dark on T2" MRI** is the hallmark of ACC — hyalinised stroma has very low T2 signal; if seen, plan for PNI and skull base tracking even if FNAC is borderline
> 6. **ADC values on DWI-MRI**: ADC <1.0 × 10⁻³ mm²/s suggests malignancy; ADC >1.2 × 10⁻³ mm²/s suggests benign — complementary to FNAC in indeterminate cases
> 7. **Frozen section sensitivity for malignancy is ~84–87%** — a normal FS does not exclude malignancy in the presence of clinical red flags (facial palsy, fixed hard mass)
> 8. **FS is most reliable for margin assessment**, not primary diagnosis — use it to check soft tissue and nerve margins in known malignancy rather than to decide extent of surgery
> 9. **Warthin tumour cytology** is virtually pathognomonic: oncocytic cells + lymphoid background + lipid-laden macrophages; bilateral in 10%, multifocal in 20%
> 10. **Low-grade MEC on FNAC may mimic mucocele** — any cystic parotid lesion with even a few epidermoid cells should raise suspicion for MEC and prompt imaging correlation
