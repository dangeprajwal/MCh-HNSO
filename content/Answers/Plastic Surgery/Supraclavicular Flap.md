## Supraclavicular Island Flap

### Anatomy

The supraclavicular flap (SCF) is an axial-pattern fasciocutaneous flap based on the **supraclavicular artery**, a branch of the **transverse cervical artery (TCA)**, which arises from the thyrocervical trunk of the subclavian artery.

- **Pedicle:** Transverse cervical artery → supraclavicular artery
- **Course:** The TCA exits between the anterior and middle scalene muscles, crosses the posterior triangle deep to the deep cervical fascia, and gives off the supraclavicular branch, which pierces the fascia and supplies the overlying skin
- **Venous drainage:** Supraclavicular vein → transverse cervical vein → subclavian vein
- **Pivot point:** Deltopectoral groove (junction of neck and shoulder)
- **Skin territory:** Overlies the shoulder and lateral neck; skin paddle dimensions up to **22 × 7 cm**
- **Innervation:** Supraclavicular nerve (C3–C4), potentially making the flap sensate

### Design and Arc of Rotation

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Pivot point | Deltopectoral groove |
| Arc of rotation | 180° |
| Maximum paddle dimensions | 22 × 7 cm |
| Reach | Oral cavity, oropharynx, lateral face, skull base |
| Flap type | Fasciocutaneous, axial |

**Marking:**
1. Patient supine, head turned to contralateral side
2. Mark the pivot point at the deltopectoral groove (medial aspect of clavicle, 2 cm lateral to sternoclavicular joint)
3. Design skin paddle over the shoulder and lateral neck, long axis running from pivot toward the posterior shoulder
4. Island the flap by deepening incisions to the deltoid fascia

### Surgical Technique

1. **Positioning:** Patient supine, head turned 45° to opposite side; ipsilateral shoulder slightly elevated
2. **Marking:** Identify pivot point at deltopectoral groove; design elliptical skin paddle with long axis toward posterior shoulder
3. **Incision and elevation:** Elevate flap deep to deltoid fascia from distal to proximal; preserve fascia with flap for reliable perforator inclusion
4. **Islanding:** Divide and bury the proximal pedicle subcutaneously; create subcutaneous tunnel into neck defect
5. **Tunnelling:** Create wide subcutaneous tunnel from pivot point to recipient site; avoid pedicle compression
6. **Inset:** Transpose flap 180° through tunnel; inset with absorbable sutures at mucosal interface
7. **Donor site:** Primary closure if ≤7 cm wide; split-thickness skin graft if wider

### Advantages

- Single-stage reconstruction — no microsurgery required
- Thin and pliable skin — excellent functional outcome for oral/oropharyngeal defects
- **Good colour match** with facial and neck skin
- Minimal donor site morbidity
- Arc of rotation allows access to oral cavity, oropharynx, lower face, and skull base
- Can be elevated simultaneously with neck dissection (same operative field)
- Reliable axial blood supply — superior to random-pattern cervical flaps

### Disadvantages

- Cannot reliably bridge defects > 14 cm from pivot point
- **Hair-bearing skin** in male patients — may require epilation or laser treatment for intraoral use
- Post-radiation: TCA may be damaged or unreliable — limit use in heavily irradiated fields
- Limited bulk — not suitable for large volumetric defects
- Pedicle compression risk during head rotation/neck movement post-operatively
- Skin paddle tip may be vulnerable if design is too distal (random-pattern territory)

### Indications in Head and Neck Surgery

| Indication | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| Pharyngocutaneous fistula repair | Excellent coverage of post-laryngectomy fistula |
| Lateral oropharyngeal defects | Thin pliable skin ideal for tonsillar fossa |
| Buccal mucosa reconstruction | Single-stage alternative to RFFF |
| Parotid region coverage | Following parotidectomy with skin excision |
| Skull base coverage | Reaches lateral/posterior skull base |
| Post-laryngectomy cutaneous defects | Covers anterior neck skin after salvage laryngectomy |

### Variations

- **Extended SCF (supercharged):** Augment supply with direct perforator anastomosis from TCA to extend reliable territory
- **Adipofascial SCF:** De-epithelialised variant for thin subcutaneous coverage without skin paddle bulk
- **Bipaddled SCF:** Two skin islands on single pedicle for through-and-through defects

### Comparison with Deltopectoral Flap

| Feature | Supraclavicular Flap | Deltopectoral Flap |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Blood supply | Transverse cervical artery (axial) | 2nd–4th IMA perforators (axial) |
| Staging | Single-stage | Usually two-stage (delay procedure) |
| Donor scar | Shoulder (well-concealed) | Anterior chest (visible) |
| Reach | Oral cavity, oropharynx, skull base | Upper neck, lower face, partial pharynx |
| Bulk | Thin | Thin |
| Donor morbidity | Minimal | Moderate (chest skin loss) |
| Reliability after radiation | Reduced (TCA affected) | Relatively preserved (IMA perforators) |
| Current use | Preferred regional flap | Largely replaced by SCF and free flaps |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> The supraclavicular island flap is a single-stage fasciocutaneous flap based on the transverse cervical artery, pivoting at the deltopectoral groove with a 180° arc of rotation reaching the oral cavity, oropharynx, and skull base. It offers excellent colour match and minimal donor morbidity without requiring microsurgery, making it the preferred pedicled regional flap in head and neck reconstruction. Its main limitations are inadequate bulk for volumetric defects, hair-bearing skin in men, and reduced reliability in post-irradiation fields where the transverse cervical artery may be compromised.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. The supraclavicular artery branches from the transverse cervical artery approximately 2 cm above the clavicle — this is the true pedicle, not the TCA itself
> 2. The pivot point is the deltopectoral groove; the flap rotates 180° to reach ipsilateral oral cavity/oropharynx
> 3. Maximum reliable skin paddle dimensions are 22 × 7 cm; exceed distal 2–3 cm with caution (random pattern territory)
> 4. Always elevate deep to deltoid fascia — the supraclavicular artery perforators run in the fascial plane and are sacrificed if the fascia is not included
> 5. Hair-bearing skin in men is a significant intraoral concern — counsel patients pre-operatively about epilation requirements
> 6. In post-radiation salvage, perform CTA or Doppler mapping of TCA preoperatively before committing to this flap
> 7. Tunnelling must be generous — pedicle compression is the commonest cause of partial flap failure
> 8. Donor site ≤7 cm can be closed primarily; wider paddles require STSG to the shoulder
> 9. The SCF has largely replaced the deltopectoral flap as the workhorse pedicled fasciocutaneous flap in H&N reconstruction
> 10. Post-operative head position should avoid ipsilateral neck flexion for 2 weeks to prevent pedicle kinking
