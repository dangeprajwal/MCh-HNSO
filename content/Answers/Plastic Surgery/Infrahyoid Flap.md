## Infrahyoid Myocutaneous Flap

### Overview

The infrahyoid (IH) myocutaneous flap, also known as the **strap muscle flap** or **infrahyoid island flap**, is a superiorly based myocutaneous flap harvested from the anterior neck. It provides thin, pliable skin from the anterior cervical region with excellent colour match for oral cavity and lower facial reconstruction without requiring microsurgery.

### Anatomy

#### Pedicle
- **Dominant pedicle:** Superior thyroid artery (STA), a branch of the external carotid artery
- The STA enters at the level of the **thyrohyoid membrane/superior thyroid notch**, just below the hyoid bone
- Venous drainage: Superior thyroid vein → internal jugular vein
- The pedicle is **superiorly located** — flap is superiorly based

#### Constituent Muscles
The flap includes the following strap muscles:
1. **Sternohyoid** — most superficial strap muscle; origin = sternum/clavicle; insertion = body of hyoid
2. **Omohyoid** (superior belly) — origin = superior belly from hyoid; insertion = intermediate tendon at clavicle
3. **Thyrohyoid** — deepest component; origin = thyroid cartilage oblique line; insertion = body of hyoid

#### Skin Territory
- Anterior neck skin between **hyoid bone** (superior) and **clavicle** (inferior)
- Midline or paramedian orientation
- Skin paddle dimensions: up to **12 × 6 cm** (limited by anterior neck width)
- Skin perfused via musculocutaneous perforators from the STA running through strap muscles to overlie skin

#### Perforator Anatomy
- Direct musculocutaneous perforators from STA → pierce strap muscles → supply overlying skin
- Maximum density of perforators in the **thyrohyoid region** (superior portion of flap) — most reliable skin territory
- Inferior skin paddle becomes less reliable beyond 5–6 cm from pivot point

### Surgical Technique

1. **Patient position:** Supine, neck extended; shoulder roll to maximise anterior neck exposure
2. **Design:** Mark elliptical or rectangular skin paddle on anterior neck between hyoid and sternal notch; midline or paramedian orientation
3. **Incision:** Incise inferior and lateral margins of skin paddle; elevate deep to strap muscle fascia from inferior
4. **Muscle dissection:** Elevate sternohyoid and omohyoid together with skin paddle; include thyrohyoid for superior pedicle protection
5. **Pedicle identification:** Identify superior thyroid artery at thyrohyoid level; preserve pedicle and **do not divide at its ECA origin**
6. **Division of inferior muscle attachment:** Divide strap muscles from sternal/clavicular origin; now the flap is based superiorly on STA
7. **Arc of rotation:** Rotate flap superiorly and medially around the STA pedicle; reaches floor of mouth, anterior tongue, anterior oral cavity
8. **Tunnelling:** Create subcutaneous tunnel under mandible into oral cavity; must be generous to avoid pedicle compression
9. **Inset:** Suture skin paddle into oral mucosal defect; skin faces the oral cavity

### Dimensions

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Skin paddle | Up to 12 × 6 cm |
| Pedicle | Superior thyroid artery |
| Pivot point | Thyrohyoid/superior thyroid notch level |
| Arc of rotation | ~90–120° superiorly |
| Reach | Anterior oral cavity, FOM, anterior tongue |

### Advantages

| Advantage | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Thin and pliable skin | Excellent functional outcome for tongue/FOM |
| Good colour match | Anterior neck skin matches oral mucosal colour well |
| Single-stage | No microsurgery; shorter operative time |
| Minimal donor morbidity | Strap muscles can be removed without functional deficit (other strap muscles compensate) |
| Inconspicuous donor scar | Anterior neck scar in skin crease; acceptable aesthetics |
| No microsurgery | Applicable when microsurgical facilities unavailable |

### Disadvantages

| Disadvantage | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Limited arc of rotation | Cannot reach posterior oral cavity or oropharynx |
| Sacrifice of superior thyroid artery | Hemithyroid ischaemia risk if bilateral STA ligation; consider thyroid function |
| Unreliable in irradiated fields | STA may be damaged/occluded after prior RT |
| Not suitable after prior thyroid/neck surgery | STA already divided |
| Limited bulk | Not suitable for large volumetric defects |
| Pedicle compression risk | Subcutaneous tunnel under mandible must be generous |
| Small skin paddle | Width limited by anterior neck dimensions (rarely > 6 cm) |

### Oncological Considerations

- Unlike the submental flap, the **infrahyoid flap territory does not contain level Ia nodes** — the flap is below the hyoid and does not harvest submental lymph nodes
- **Oncologically safer** than submental flap for N0 oral cancer reconstruction
- However, anterior jugular and delphian nodes (level VI) lie in proximity — some surgeons include node sampling during harvest
- Suitable for T1/T2 oral cavity cancer with N0 neck

### Indications

| Defect | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Floor of mouth (anterior/central) | Primary indication — thin skin, good match |
| Anterior tongue reconstruction | For partial glossectomy defects |
| Buccal mucosa (anterior) | Limited reach posteriorly |
| Retromolar trigone (anterior) | Can reach with extended tunnel |
| Alternative to RFFF | In thin patients, limited anatomy, no microsurgery available |
| Reconstruction when submental flap contraindicated | After level I dissection (submental unavailable) |

### Comparison with Submental Flap

| Feature | Infrahyoid Flap | Submental Flap |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Pedicle | Superior thyroid artery (ECA branch) | Submental artery (facial artery branch) |
| Pivot point | Thyrohyoid level | Mandibular border (facial artery) |
| Skin territory | Anterior neck (below hyoid) | Submental triangle (chin/FOM) |
| Oncological concern | Low (no level Ia nodes) | High (level Ia submental nodes) |
| Reach | Central/anterior oral cavity | Anterior–lateral oral cavity, lower face |
| Arc of rotation | Superior (moderate) | Superior + lateral (better) |
| Prior neck dissection | Contraindicated if STA divided | Contraindicated if level I dissected |
| Thickness | Thin | Thin |
| Best indication | FOM/anterior tongue (N0 cases) | Lower lip, chin, lateral FOM |
| Hair-bearing (men) | Occasionally | Yes (submental beard area) |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> The infrahyoid myocutaneous flap is a superiorly based flap incorporating anterior neck skin, platysma, and strap muscles, pedicled on the superior thyroid artery. It provides thin, pliable skin with good colour match for anterior oral cavity reconstruction — particularly floor of mouth and anterior tongue — without microsurgery. Compared to the submental flap, it has the oncological advantage of not carrying Level Ia submental lymph nodes, making it a safer choice in N0 oral cavity cancer; however it has a more limited arc of rotation and cannot access posterior oral cavity or oropharyngeal defects.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. Pedicle is the superior thyroid artery (branch of ECA) — the flap is based superiorly, NOT inferiorly
> 2. The flap does NOT contain submental (Level Ia) lymph nodes — this is its oncological safety advantage over the submental flap
> 3. If the superior thyroid artery has been divided during prior thyroidectomy or neck dissection, the flap cannot be raised — always check surgical history
> 4. Bilateral STA ligation can cause hemithyroid ischaemia — consider thyroid function pre-operatively; unilateral flap is standard
> 5. The most reliable skin territory is the superior portion (thyrohyoid region) where perforator density is highest — design skin paddle superiorly
> 6. Subcutaneous tunnel under mandible must accommodate ≥ 2 finger-breadths — pedicle compression is the main technical failure cause
> 7. The flap cannot reach posterior oral cavity — for retromolar, tonsillar, or pharyngeal defects, use a regional pedicled or free flap
> 8. In irradiated necks, STA may be obliterated — Doppler pre-operative assessment of STA flow is advisable
> 9. Strap muscle sacrifice causes no functional deficit — other strap muscles (thyrohyoid contralateral, cricothyroid) compensate for laryngeal elevation
> 10. The anterior neck scar is cosmetically acceptable and can be camouflaged within a neck crease if correctly planned
