## Anatomy of the Fibula Flap

### Osseous Component
The fibula is a non-weight-bearing long bone of the lateral leg providing up to **25 cm of harvestable bone**. Structural requirements mandate:
- **6 cm preserved proximally** (proximal tibiofibular joint / peroneal nerve protection)
- **6 cm preserved distally** (ankle mortise / syndesmotic stability)

The fibula has dual cortical bone with substantial vertical height (~14–16 mm), making it ideal for osseointegrated implants. Periosteal blood supply is segmental, enabling multiple osteotomies while maintaining bone viability.

### Vascular Pedicle
- **Peroneal artery** (fibular artery): arises from tibial-peroneal trunk (posterior tibial artery), runs in the posterior compartment deep to flexor hallucis longus (FHL), gives segmental periosteal perforators to fibula + septocutaneous/musculocutaneous perforators to lateral leg skin
- Pedicle length: **3–4 cm** (average); pedicle diameter adequate for microsurgical anastomosis
- **Venae comitantes** (two) accompany peroneal artery; greater saphenous vein can be included as additional venous drainage

### Cutaneous Component (Skin Paddle)
- Skin paddle territory: over the **lateral leg** (middle and lower thirds)
- Blood supply to skin: **septocutaneous perforators** through the posterior crural septum (between peroneus longus and flexor hallucis longus) or **musculocutaneous perforators** through FHL
- Perforator mapping: handheld Doppler 4 cm posterior to fibula in the posterior crural septum
- Skin paddle dimensions: typically 6 × 12 cm (primary closure if width ≤5 cm)

### Nerve Supply
- **Peroneal nerve** (superficial and deep): must be protected; runs along lateral fibula proximally; superficial peroneal nerve crosses lateral aspect of fibula approximately 10 cm above ankle
- Sensory: lateral sural cutaneous nerve can be harvested for sensate flap (rarely done for mandible)

## Flap Components and Variants

| Variant | Components | Indication |
|---|---|---|
| Osseous only | Bone alone | Short mandibular defects, no mucosal need |
| Osteocutaneous | Bone + skin paddle | Mandible + floor of mouth lining |
| Osteomyocutaneous | Bone + muscle segment (FHL/peroneus) + skin | Large soft-tissue deficits |
| Double-barrel fibula | Bone folded on itself (2 cortices) | Increased vertical height for implants |

## Indications in Head and Neck Oncology

### Mandibular Reconstruction
The free fibula is the **gold standard** for segmental mandibular reconstruction, applicable for all defect classes:
- **Class I** (lateral/body): straight fibula segment; skin paddle for floor of mouth
- **Class II** (hemimandible including angle): single osteotomy at ramus; skin paddle for lateral FOM and oral tongue
- **Class III** (total or near-total mandible with condyles): multiple osteotomies; bilateral condylar prostheses or native condyle preservation at one side
- **Bilateral condyle**: fibula can simulate bilateral condyles with osteotomies and contouring

### Maxillary Reconstruction
- **Brown Class I–IIa**: fibula provides inferior alveolar reconstruction + implant support
- **Brown Class III**: fibula with titanium mesh for orbital floor support (± ALT for soft tissue volume)
- **Total maxillary reconstruction**: "Le Fort I" fibula reconstruction; 3–4 osteotomies of fibula configured to reconstruct lateral nasal wall + alveolus + palate

## Operative Technique

### Pre-operative Planning
1. **CT angiography of lower limb**: confirm peroneal artery dominance; rule out aberrant anatomy (peroneal artery magna — peroneal artery is dominant leg supply, a contraindication)
2. **Doppler mapping**: mark septocutaneous perforators in posterior crural septum
3. **Virtual Surgical Planning (VSP)**: CT-based 3D model of mandible + fibula → planned osteotomies, contouring, and bite plane; patient-specific cutting guides and reconstruction plates manufactured
4. **Dental planning**: implant positions planned on VSP model

### Harvest Technique
1. Lateral decubitus or supine with hip bump; thigh tourniquet
2. Skin paddle incision including septum; identify and protect superficial peroneal nerve
3. Anterior approach: incise anterior intramuscular septum → identify fibula anterior surface
4. Posterior approach: incise posterior crural septum → identify peroneal vessels deep to FHL
5. Peroneal artery dissected proximally and distally; peroneal vessels divided **distal to most distal osteotomy** (preserves segmental bone vascularity)
6. Osteotomies performed using oscillating saw along cutting guide marks; fibula bent/contoured to fit mandibular defect template
7. Muscle trimmed from fibula segments to allow close opposition at osteotomy sites

### Recipient Site Inset
1. Pre-bend reconstruction plate to mandibular model (VSP-derived)
2. Fibula segments arranged per planned osteotomies; plate fixated with bicortical screws
3. Skin paddle inset for floor of mouth lining; multiple skin islands possible with perforator-based independent paddles
4. Microvascular anastomosis: peroneal artery to facial artery (end-to-end) or superior thyroid / external carotid; venous to facial vein, internal jugular, or external jugular
5. **Condylar reconstruction**: fibula end placed into glenoid fossa; total TMJ prosthesis used in select cases

## Virtual Surgical Planning (VSP)

VSP has transformed mandibular reconstruction:
- **Workflow**: pre-op CT of mandible + legs → segmentation → 3D model → virtual osteotomy and fibula bending → manufacture of cutting guides (mandibular resection guide + fibula osteotomy guide) and pre-bent titanium plate
- **Benefits**: reduced operating time (flap ischaemia time), improved accuracy of condylar positioning, better dental implant planning, reproducibility
- **Evidence**: Succo et al. and Foley et al. demonstrate reduced ischaemia time and improved occlusion outcomes

---

## Dental Rehabilitation: Osseointegrated Implants

The combination of **free fibula + osseointegrated implants** is the gold standard for functional oral rehabilitation after mandibulectomy:

- **Timing**: primary implants (at flap harvest) or secondary (3–6 months post-reconstruction)
- **Implant positions**: planned on VSP; 3.75–4 mm diameter titanium implants
- **Osseointegration period**: 3–6 months; loading deferred to allow bone maturation
- **Post-radiation delay**: if adjuvant RT planned, delay implant placement 12 months post-RT; HBO therapy (20 pre-op + 10 post-op dives) may improve outcomes in irradiated bone
- **Vertical height discrepancy**: fibula height (14–16 mm) less than native mandibular height (30–35 mm); solutions: double-barrel fibula, distraction osteogenesis, implant-supported prosthesis with extended abutments

## Donor Site Morbidity

| Issue | Incidence | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Wound dehiscence/STSG loss | 5–10% | STSG regrafting; dressings |
| Peroneal nerve injury | 1–3% | Foot drop; AFO splint; prognosis good |
| Ankle instability | <1% (if 6 cm preserved distally) | Physiotherapy |
| Cold intolerance | 10–15% | Thermal protection |
| Aesthetic (donor scar) | Universal | Counselling; STSG cosmesis |

Walking ability is typically unimpaired (fibula is non-weight-bearing); peroneus muscles and ankle mortise preserved.

## Fibula vs DCIA (Deep Circumflex Iliac Artery) Flap

| Feature | Free Fibula | DCIA (Iliac Crest) |
|---|---|---|
| Max bone length | 25 cm | 12–14 cm |
| Vertical bone height | 14–16 mm | 25–30 mm |
| Pedicle length | 3–4 cm | 4–6 cm |
| Skin paddle reliability | Moderate (perforator-based) | Poorer |
| Osteotomy capacity | Excellent (multiple) | Limited |
| Implant suitability | Excellent | Excellent |
| Best for | Long defects, symphysis, hemimandible | Short posterior defects, condyle |
| Donor morbidity | Low | Higher (hernia, gait) |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> The free fibula osteocutaneous flap, introduced by Hidalgo (1989), is the gold standard for mandibular reconstruction, offering up to 25 cm of dual-cortical bone, reliable septocutaneous skin paddle, and a long peroneal artery pedicle. Multiple osteotomies guided by virtual surgical planning allow precise reconstruction of any mandibular contour including condyle; the bone's cortical quality makes it ideal for subsequent osseointegrated dental implants, completing functional oral rehabilitation.

---

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. Always obtain pre-operative CT angiography of the leg to confirm peroneal artery dominance and rule out peroneal artery magna (where it is the main leg supply), an absolute contraindication to harvest.
> 2. Preserve 6 cm proximally and 6 cm distally: proximal protects peroneal nerve and proximal tibiofibular joint; distal preserves ankle mortise stability.
> 3. Perforators for the skin paddle lie in the posterior crural septum between peroneus longus and FHL — map with Doppler 4 cm behind fibula pre-operatively.
> 4. Virtual surgical planning dramatically reduces ischaemia time by pre-bending the plate and pre-planning osteotomies; cutting guides are manufactured stereolithographically and should be used routinely for complex reconstructions.
> 5. The double-barrel fibula (folding the flap) increases vertical height to ~28–30 mm, approximating native mandibular height and improving the biomechanical environment for dental implants.
> 6. In post-radiation cases, delay implant placement at least 12 months after RT completion; HBO therapy (PreOp 20 dives, PostOp 10 dives) improves osseointegration rates in hypoxic bone.
> 7. Fibula is preferred over DCIA for hemimandibulectomy defects because of greater harvestable length and superior multiple-osteotomy capacity; DCIA is preferred for short posterior body defects where greater vertical height is advantageous.
> 8. The skin paddle can be split into two independent islands (each with its own perforator) to simultaneously reconstruct floor of mouth and external skin if a through-and-through defect is present.
