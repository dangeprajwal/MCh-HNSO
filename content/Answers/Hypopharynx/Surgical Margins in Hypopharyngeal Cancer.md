(2004) published simultaneous randomised controlled trials (EORTC 22931 and RTOG 9501 respectively) establishing that positive surgical margins are one of the two indications (alongside extranodal extension) that mandate adjuvant concurrent chemoradiation — rather than radiotherapy alone — after head and neck resection, demonstrating significantly improved locoregional control and disease-free survival with cisplatin-based CRT. ## Surgical Margins in Hypopharyngeal Cancer

### Why Hypopharyngeal Margins Are Particularly Challenging

Hypopharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma has several unique features that make margin assessment both critical and technically difficult:

1. **Submucosal spread and skip lesions:** Hypopharyngeal SCC spreads extensively in the submucosal plane, often beyond the visible or palpable tumour. Skip lesions — separate nests of tumour cells in submucosal lymphatics or perineural spaces — can be identified up to 1–2 cm from the macroscopic tumour edge. The surgeon cannot rely on visual or tactile assessment alone.

2. **Thin, pliable mucosa:** The hypopharyngeal mucosa is thin and easily torn, making en-bloc resection with adequate cuffs technically demanding, particularly at the postcricoid region and piriform apex.

3. **Complex three-dimensional anatomy:** The hypopharynx wraps around the larynx posterolaterally. The medial wall of the piriform sinus is the lateral surface of the aryepiglottic fold and arytenoid cartilage. The apex of the piriform sinus lies at the cricoarytenoid joint level — tumour at the apex directly abuts cartilage.

4. **Field cancerisation:** Synchronous and metachronous carcinomas, dysplasia, and carcinoma in situ can be present at multiple mucosal sites simultaneously, making the identification of true margins difficult even with frozen section.

5. **Circumferential involvement:** Postcricoid carcinomas and large piriform sinus tumours may involve 270° or 360° of the hypopharyngeal circumference, leaving no safe margin anteriorly or posteriorly without sacrificing the larynx or extending into the oesophagus.

---

### Margin Definitions

| Category | Distance | Implications |
|---|---|---|
| **Clear (negative)** | ≥5 mm tumour-free tissue at inked margin | Acceptable; no specific action beyond standard surveillance |
| **Close** | 1–5 mm | Considered at risk; adjuvant radiotherapy to tumour bed; consideration for re-resection if feasible |
| **Positive** | <1 mm or tumour at ink / transected through tumour | High-risk; **mandate adjuvant CRT** (per EORTC 22931 / RTOG 9501) |

**Distinction:** A **close margin** (≥1 mm) may be managed with adjuvant RT alone (50–60 Gy to the tumour bed). A **positive margin** is one of two indications for adjuvant **concurrent chemoradiation** with cisplatin.

---

### Subsite-Specific Margin Considerations

#### Pyriform Sinus
- **Mucosal margin:** ≥1 cm from macroscopic tumour edge recommended; 5 mm minimum
- **Medial wall:** aryepiglottic fold and arytenoid cartilage are the medial boundary; medial wall involvement generally mandates ipsilateral arytenoidectomy (hemilaryngectomy component) or total laryngectomy for adequate clearance
- **Apex:** the most critical margin — apex involvement directly abuts the cricoarytenoid joint and cricoid cartilage; frozen section of apex margin mandatory; positive apex = cartilage invasion (T4a)
- **Lateral wall:** thyroid cartilage inner perichondrium is the lateral boundary; invasion of thyroid cartilage upgrades to T4a; resection of ipsilateral thyroid ala required for clearance

#### Posterior Pharyngeal Wall
- **Deep margin:** pre-vertebral fascia is the anatomical deep limit; the plane between the posterior pharyngeal constrictor and pre-vertebral fascia is the correct plane; fascia itself (if not invaded) is an acceptable deep margin
- **Mucosal margins:** bilateral extension is common; mucosal margins of ≥1 cm laterally and 2 cm inferiorly
- **Pre-vertebral fascia invasion:** indicates T4b — unresectable in most centres (no margin achievable posterior to fascia without spine involvement)

#### Postcricoid Region
- **Circumferential involvement:** if >50% circumference involved, reconstruction with tubed free flap (radial forearm, ALT) or free jejunum; total pharyngolaryngectomy
- **Oesophageal margin:** inferior margin taken at cervical oesophagus; frozen section of distal oesophageal margin essential; submucosal spread into the oesophagus common — must be excluded
- **Tracheal margin:** if total laryngopharyngectomy performed, tracheal division margin assessed by frozen section; peritracheal lymph node dissection (paratracheal, Delphian) performed concurrently

### Role of Frozen Section in Hypopharyngeal Surgery

Intraoperative frozen section (FS) is **essential** in hypopharyngeal resection:

**What to send:**
1. **Mucosal margins** — systematic shave margins from all surgical edges of specimen in situ, labelled by anatomical location
2. **Deep/soft-tissue margins** — separate sections perpendicular to the inked deep margin
3. **Piriform apex** — sent separately and specifically; direct surgeon response if positive
4. **Oesophageal transaction margin** — sent separately; if positive, resect further inferiorly
5. **Tracheal/subglottic margin** (if total laryngectomy component)

**Limitations:**
- Frozen section has ~5–10% false-negative rate due to sampling error and tissue artefact
- Skip lesions may not be within the sampled margin area — frozen section cannot exclude all submucosal disease
- Final paraffin section is the definitive assessment — discrepancy with frozen section requires adjuvant CRT

**Protocol:**
- All margins labelled anatomically before specimen removal
- FS reported in real time; positive result → surgeon re-resects before wound closure
- Re-resection margin also sent for FS; if still positive, document for adjuvant planning

---

### Management of Positive Margins

| Situation | Management |
|---|---|
| Positive margin identified intraoperatively | Re-resect immediately; send new margin for frozen section |
| Re-resection achieves clear margin (≥5 mm) | Adjuvant RT 60–66 Gy; no mandatory CRT unless ENE also present |
| Re-resection not achievable (fixed, vital structure) | Document; adjuvant concurrent CRT (cisplatin 100 mg/m² d1, 22, 43 + RT 60–66 Gy) |
| Positive margin on final paraffin (not identified intraoperatively) | Adjuvant concurrent CRT; discuss at MDT for consideration of surgical re-excision |

**EORTC 22931 / RTOG 9501 indications for adjuvant CRT (not RT alone):**
- Positive surgical margins, **AND/OR**
- Extranodal extension (ENE)

Other "high-risk" features (≥2 positive nodes, T3-T4, perineural invasion, vascular invasion, level IV-V nodes) indicate adjuvant RT but not mandatorily CRT.

---

### Skip Lesions — Practical Implications

Skip lesions are defined as foci of invasive SCC separated from the main tumour by normal mucosa or submucosa. They occur in:
- 15–25% of hypopharyngeal resection specimens
- Most commonly in the submucosal plane
- Significance: if within the margin of resection, classified as **positive margin** regardless of distance from the main tumour
- Implication: resection with ≥2 cm mucosal margins for hypopharyngeal SCC is advocated at high-volume centres precisely because of skip lesions
- Field cancerisation must also be considered — carcinoma in situ or severe dysplasia at margins should prompt re-resection even if invasive carcinoma is absent

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Margin assessment in hypopharyngeal cancer is technically demanding due to submucosal spread (skip lesions up to 2 cm beyond visible tumour), thin mucosa, complex anatomy, and frequent field cancerisation. A clear margin is ≥5 mm tumour-free tissue; positive is <1 mm or tumour at ink. Positive margins are one of two indications (with extranodal extension) for adjuvant concurrent chemoradiation (cisplatin + 60–66 Gy), as established by EORTC 22931 and RTOG 9501. Subsite-specific concerns include piriform apex involvement (abutting cricoarytenoid joint → T4a), postcircoid circumferential resection requiring free flap reconstruction, oesophageal margin extension, and pre-vertebral fascia as the deep limit of posterior wall resection. Intraoperative frozen section of systematic anatomically labelled mucosal margins, piriform apex, and oesophageal transaction margins is mandatory; the ~5–10% false-negative rate means final paraffin sections are definitive.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. Skip lesions — foci of invasive SCC separated from the main tumour by normal mucosa — occur in 15–25% of hypopharyngeal specimens; ≥2 cm mucosal margins are advocated at high-volume centres for this reason.
> 2. Piriform **apex** is the single most critical margin in pyriform sinus resection — a positive apex margin = T4a (cartilage invasion) and mandates total laryngectomy for clearance.
> 3. Positive margin + concurrent CRT (EORTC 22931 / RTOG 9501): these two trials define the indications for CRT vs RT alone post-resection. The only two features that mandate CRT are **positive margin** and **extranodal extension**.
> 4. Pre-vertebral fascia as deep margin: if fascia is intact and not invaded, it is an acceptable oncological deep margin for posterior pharyngeal wall resection — do not attempt resection posterior to the fascia.
> 5. Frozen section has a 5–10% false-negative rate due to sampling error — final paraffin sections are definitive; discrepancy requires adjuvant CRT planning.
> 6. Oesophageal and tracheal transaction margins must always be assessed by frozen section in total laryngopharyngectomy — submucosal spread into the oesophagus occurs in postcricoid carcinoma and is easily missed clinically.
> 7. Field cancerisation means carcinoma in situ or severe dysplasia at margins should prompt re-resection even if invasive carcinoma is absent — a positive CIS margin has the same prognostic implications as invasive margin involvement.
> 8. Re-resection in the hypopharynx is often not technically feasible (fixed to larynx/vertebra) — this is why frozen sections must be performed before wound closure, not as a post-operative surprise.
> 9. Close margin (1–5 mm): adjuvant RT alone (not CRT) is typically used unless ENE also present; this is different from a frankly positive margin.
> 10. Thyroid cartilage inner perichondrium invasion = T4a; if the outer perichondrium is intact, cartilage resection with the specimen may still achieve clear margins.
