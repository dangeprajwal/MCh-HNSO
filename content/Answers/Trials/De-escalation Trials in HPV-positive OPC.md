## Introduction and Rationale

HPV-positive oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma (HPV+ OPSCC) has emerged as a distinct oncological entity with a dramatically better prognosis than tobacco-driven (HPV-negative) HNC. The 3-year overall survival for HPV+ OPSCC treated with standard concurrent chemoradiotherapy (CRT) is **80–90%**, compared to 50–60% for HPV-negative disease.

This excellent prognosis has generated the **de-escalation hypothesis**: if many HPV+ OPSCC patients are cured with standard treatment, the incremental survival benefit of maximum-intensity treatment may be outweighed by the burden of long-term toxicity in younger, otherwise healthy patients who will live decades with the consequences of treatment.

### The Toxicity Burden of Standard CRT

Standard concurrent CRT (70 Gy + cisplatin 100 mg/m² × 3 cycles) causes significant late toxicity in long-term survivors:

| Late Toxicity | Incidence |
|---|---|
| Severe dysphagia / PEG dependency at 2 years | 20–40% |
| Grade ≥2 xerostomia | 40–60% |
| Fibrosis and trismus | 15–30% |
| Osteoradionecrosis | 3–8% |
| Cisplatin ototoxicity (significant hearing loss) | 15–30% |
| Cisplatin nephrotoxicity (eGFR reduction >10%) | 10–20% |
| Cisplatin neurotoxicity | 10–25% |

### Three Broad De-escalation Strategies

1. **Chemotherapy substitution** — replace cisplatin with a less toxic systemic agent (e.g., cetuximab)
2. **Radiation dose reduction** — reduce from 70 Gy to 50–60 Gy ± modified concurrent chemotherapy
3. **Surgery-first (TORS-based)** — TORS resection to achieve pathological risk stratification, then risk-adapted adjuvant therapy (RT alone if low risk; avoid concurrent chemotherapy)

## Part A: Chemotherapy Substitution Trials

### 1. RTOG 1016 (Gillison et al., 2019)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase III non-inferiority randomised controlled trial |
| **Population** | 849 patients with HPV+ stage III–IVB OPSCC, ECOG PS 0–1 |
| **Intervention** | RT (70 Gy IMRT) + cetuximab (loading dose 400 mg/m², then 250 mg/m² weekly × 7) |
| **Control** | RT (70 Gy IMRT) + cisplatin (100 mg/m² on days 1 and 22) |
| **Non-inferiority margin** | 5-year OS difference of ≤7% |
| **Primary endpoint** | 5-year overall survival |

**Key results:**
- 5-year OS: **77.9% (cetuximab) vs 84.6% (cisplatin)**
- The non-inferiority of cetuximab was **not demonstrated**
- HR for OS: 1.45 (95% CI 1.07–1.96) — cetuximab arm significantly worse
- 5-year PFS: 67.7% vs 78.4% — also worse with cetuximab

**Conclusion:** Cetuximab cannot replace cisplatin in HPV+ OPSCC. Cisplatin-based CRT remains the standard of care.

### 2. De-ESCALaTE HPV Trial (Mehanna et al., 2019)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase III open-label randomised controlled trial |
| **Population** | 334 patients with low-risk HPV+ OPSCC (non-smokers or ≤10 pack-years, T1–3 N0–2b) |
| **Intervention** | RT (70 Gy) + cetuximab |
| **Control** | RT (70 Gy) + cisplatin (40 mg/m² weekly × 7) |
| **Primary endpoint** | Severe (Grade ≥3) toxicity at 24 months |

**Key results:**
- Severe toxicity: similar between arms (cetuximab 95.3% vs cisplatin 86.5%)
- 2-year OS: **97.5% (cisplatin) vs 89.4% (cetuximab)** — cisplatin superior (p = 0.001)
- Cetuximab showed **more severe toxicity and worse survival** — a doubly negative result

**Conclusion:** Cetuximab is neither less toxic nor oncologically equivalent to cisplatin. This trial definitively ended the cetuximab substitution hypothesis.

## Part B: Radiation Dose Reduction Trials

### 3. NRG HN002 (Yom et al., 2021)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase II randomised, 2-arm study |
| **Population** | 306 patients with p16+ HPV+ OPSCC (T1–2 N1–2b or T3 N0–2b, low-risk features) |
| **Arm A** | 60 Gy IMRT in 30 fractions + weekly cisplatin 40 mg/m² |
| **Arm B** | 60 Gy IMRT in 30 fractions alone (no chemotherapy) |
| **Primary endpoint** | 2-year PFS ≥85%; patient-reported swallowing at 1 year |

**Key results:**
- Arm A (60 Gy + cisplatin): 2-year PFS **90.5%** — met pre-specified threshold
- Arm B (60 Gy alone): 2-year PFS **87.6%** — did not meet pre-specified threshold (≥85%)
- Swallowing outcomes improved in both arms vs historical standard CRT

**Conclusion:** RT dose reduction to 60 Gy + concurrent cisplatin is feasible and will be tested in phase III (NRG HN005).

### 4. E1308 Trial (Marur et al., 2014)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase II single-arm study |
| **Intervention** | 3 cycles of induction TPF chemotherapy followed by, in complete responders, reduced-dose RT (54 Gy) + weekly cetuximab |
| **Non-responders** | Received standard 70 Gy |
| **Endpoint** | 2-year PFS in complete responders receiving 54 Gy |

**Key results:**
- Complete clinical response to induction chemo: 71%
- 2-year PFS in complete responders receiving 54 Gy: **80%**
- Swallowing outcomes significantly better in the 54 Gy group
- Established proof of concept for induction chemo → bioselection → reduced RT dose

### 5. OPTIMA Trial (Seiwert et al., 2019)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase II randomised; uses induction chemotherapy for risk stratification |
| **Strategy** | Induction carboplatin/nab-paclitaxel → bioselect: complete responders get 45 Gy + weekly carboplatin; partial/non-responders get standard 75 Gy + weekly carboplatin |
| **Key concept** | Uses induction chemotherapy response as a biomarker to identify who can be safely de-escalated |

**Key results:**
- 2-year OS: 97% overall
- Complete responders to induction (receiving 45 Gy): 95% 2-year OS
- Concept of **bioselection** — identifying truly low-risk patients using tumour response

## Part C: Surgery-First (TORS) De-escalation Trials

### 6. ECOG-ACRIN E3311 (Price et al., 2020)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase II single-arm, risk-stratified adjuvant therapy trial |
| **Intervention** | Transoral robotic surgery (TORS) ± neck dissection, then risk-stratified adjuvant therapy |
| **Risk stratification:** | |
| Low risk | ≤pT3, clear margins, ≤2 nodes without ENE → observation |
| Intermediate risk | Close margins or 2–4 nodes without ENE → reduced-dose adjuvant RT (50 Gy) |
| High risk | Positive margins or ENE → standard adjuvant CRT (60 Gy + cisplatin) |
| **Primary endpoint** | 2-year PFS |

**Key results:**
- 2-year PFS: **95.9% (intermediate-risk, 50 Gy RT)**
- 2-year PFS: 95.7% (high-risk, standard CRT)
- Swallowing function superior in intermediate-risk patients receiving 50 Gy vs 60 Gy

**Conclusion:** TORS with risk-stratified adjuvant therapy is safe and oncologically sound; forms the basis for the definitive PATHOS trial.

### 7. PATHOS Trial (ongoing)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase II/III randomised, non-inferiority (UK multicentre) |
| **Eligibility** | p16+ OPSCC treated with TORS ± neck dissection |
| **Randomisation:** | |
| Low-risk (pT1–2, ≤4 nodes, no ENE) | RT 50 Gy vs observation |
| Intermediate-risk (≤pT3 or 2–4 nodes) | RT 50 Gy vs RT 60 Gy |
| High-risk (positive margins or ENE) | CRT 60 Gy + cisplatin vs cisplatin alone |
| **Primary endpoint** | Swallowing function (MDADI) as co-primary endpoint alongside disease-specific survival |
| **Status** | Accrual complete; results awaited |

**Significance:** PATHOS is the largest prospectively randomised trial testing TORS-based de-escalation with patient-reported swallowing as a primary outcome.

### 8. ORATOR Trial (Palma et al., 2020)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase II randomised pilot trial |
| **Population** | 68 patients with T1–2 N0–2 HPV+ OPSCC |
| **Arm A** | Radiotherapy (± cetuximab for N+ disease): 70 Gy IMRT |
| **Arm B** | Transoral robotic surgery (TORS) ± neck dissection ± adjuvant RT |
| **Primary endpoint** | Swallowing function at 1 year (MDADI score) |

**Key results:**
- Primary endpoint (MDADI at 1 year): **RT arm superior** (mean 84.3 vs 74.4, p = 0.042)
- 2-year OS: similar between arms (RT: 91%, TORS: 91%)
- TORS arm had more early surgical complications; RT had more late dysphagia

**Conclusion:** TORS did not demonstrate superior swallowing function at 1 year vs radiotherapy. The larger ORATOR2 trial is examining this in a phase III setting.

### 9. ORATOR2 Trial (ongoing)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase III randomised, non-inferiority |
| **Comparison** | TORS + risk-adapted adjuvant therapy vs primary RT (de-escalated to 60 Gy for low-risk) |
| **Primary endpoint** | 2-year OS (non-inferiority) with swallowing as key secondary |
| **Status** | Ongoing; results awaited |

## Part D: Radiation Fractionation Modification Trials

### 10. TROG 12.01 / HN.6 (Arm-based)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase III TROG 12.01; comparing standard CRT (70 Gy) vs de-escalated RT (60 Gy) in favourable HPV+ OPSCC |
| **Primary endpoint** | Oncological outcome equivalence + patient-reported outcomes |
| **Status** | Published; 60 Gy not non-inferior — standard 70 Gy remained the benchmark |

### 11. QUARTERBACK Trial

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Dose reduction to 56 Gy using 3D conformal RT with concurrent cisplatin in selected HPV+ OPSCC |
| **Rationale** | Using lower RT doses while maintaining concurrent chemotherapy to reduce late dysphagia |
| **Status** | Phase II; preliminary results showed acceptable disease control |

### 12. DART-HPV (Dose Adapted Radio-Therapy — HPV)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Risk-adapted RT dose: 60 Gy for PET-CR, 66 Gy for partial response to induction, 70 Gy for stable/progressive disease |
| **Approach** | Induction chemotherapy (docetaxel + cisplatin) × 2 cycles, then FDG-PET assessment at 6 weeks to guide RT dose |
| **Key finding** | Metabolic response to induction chemotherapy predicts local control in HPV+ OPSCC |

## Part F: Risk Stratification for De-escalation

Not all HPV+ OPSCC patients are candidates for de-escalation. Risk factors that predict worse outcomes even within HPV+ disease include:

| Risk Factor | Significance |
|---|---|
| Heavy smoking (>10 pack-years) | Worse 3-year OS (72% vs 93%) — Ang et al. 2010 |
| Advanced T stage (T4) | Higher risk of local failure |
| High nodal burden (N3 or ≥5 nodes) | Increased risk of distant metastasis |
| p16-negative (true HPV-) | Standard treatment; no de-escalation |
| Low ECOG performance status | May not tolerate reduced regimen |

**Current standard of care:** De-escalation is NOT recommended outside clinical trials. Patients should be treated with standard CRT (70 Gy + cisplatin) unless enrolled in a clinical trial, given that cetuximab substitution has been definitively shown to be inferior.

## Part G: ORATOR Trial — Detailed Discussion

The ORATOR trial deserves detailed discussion as a specific exam question (Q12, TMH 2020).

### Background
- Growing adoption of TORS for oropharyngeal cancer was not supported by level I evidence prior to ORATOR
- TORS was proposed as a de-escalation strategy: surgical resection → risk stratification → reduce or avoid CRT

### Trial Design
- **Phase II** randomised, open-label, multicentre (4 Canadian centres)
- **68 patients** (34 per arm)
- Eligible: T1–T2 N0–N2 (AJCC 7th) OPSCC (HPV+ or HPV-)
- **Arm A — Radiotherapy:** IMRT to 70 Gy ± cetuximab for node-positive disease
- **Arm B — TORS:** Primary TORS ± neck dissection; adjuvant treatment based on pathology: low risk → observation; high risk → RT ± chemotherapy

### Primary Endpoint
**Swallowing function at 1 year** (MD Anderson Dysphagia Inventory — MDADI)

### Results

| Outcome | RT Arm | TORS Arm | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| MDADI at 1 year | 84.3 (better) | 74.4 | 0.042 |
| 2-year OS | 91% | 91% | NS |
| 2-year PFS | 91% | 91% | NS |
| Grade ≥3 acute toxicity | 53% | 66% | NS |
| Grade ≥3 late toxicity | 26% | 38% | NS |

### Interpretation
- TORS did **not** demonstrate superior swallowing function at 1 year — the primary endpoint favoured RT
- However, the trial was underpowered (pilot phase II, only 34/arm)
- Disease control was equivalent
- The ORATOR2 trial (phase III) is designed to answer the definitive question

### Limitations
- Small sample size (phase II pilot)
- Mixed HPV+ and HPV- population
- Adjuvant treatment varied between TORS patients
- 1-year MDADI may not reflect long-term trajectory (RT toxicity worsens over time)

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. HPV+ OPSCC has 80–90% 3-year OS with standard CRT, but survivors face decades of treatment-related toxicity — this drives the de-escalation agenda
> 2. Three strategies: (a) cetuximab substitution for cisplatin, (b) RT dose reduction, (c) TORS-based surgery-first with risk-adapted adjuvant therapy
> 3. Both RTOG 1016 and De-ESCALaTE HPV definitively showed cetuximab is inferior to cisplatin — cetuximab substitution is abandoned
> 4. Current standard: 70 Gy IMRT + cisplatin-based CRT for HPV+ OPSCC (outside clinical trials)
> 5. ECOG E3311: TORS with risk-stratified adjuvant RT (50 Gy intermediate risk) achieves 95.9% 2-year PFS — supports TORS-based de-escalation pathway
> 6. PATHOS (phase II/III): Largest TORS de-escalation trial; uses MDADI swallowing as co-primary endpoint; results awaited
> 7. NRG HN002: 60 Gy + cisplatin achieved 90.5% 2-year PFS — supports RT dose reduction to 60 Gy; phase III NRG HN005 ongoing
> 8. ORATOR trial: TORS did NOT demonstrate superior swallowing vs RT; RT arm had better MDADI at 1 year (84.3 vs 74.4); disease control equivalent
> 9. De-escalation candidates are low-risk HPV+ OPSCC: non-smokers, T1–2, limited nodal burden; heavy smokers (>10 pack-years) have worse prognosis even with HPV+ disease
> 10. Until phase III de-escalation trial results mature, standard-intensity CRT should be recommended outside of clinical trials

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - For the exam, know which trials are COMPLETED vs ONGOING and the key results: RTOG 1016 and De-ESCALaTE = completed negative for cetuximab; PATHOS and NRG HN005 = ongoing
> - The ORATOR trial (TMH 2020 question) is phase II, not phase III; the definitive ORATOR2 is still ongoing
> - ECOG E3311 showed that adjuvant RT can be safely reduced to 50 Gy post-TORS in intermediate-risk patients without compromising PFS — this is the foundation of the PATHOS trial
> - Heavy tobacco exposure (>10 pack-years) negates the HPV-associated prognosis benefit — this is a critical selection criterion for de-escalation
> - Cetuximab is NOT equivalent to cisplatin in HPV+ OPSCC despite being less nephrotoxic and ototoxic — this is one of the most important recent trial results in HNC
> - The MDADI (MD Anderson Dysphagia Inventory) is the primary swallowing outcome measure used across de-escalation trials — know the instrument
