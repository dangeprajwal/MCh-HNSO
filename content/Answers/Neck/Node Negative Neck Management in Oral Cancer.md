## Introduction

The management of the clinically node-negative (cN0) neck in oral cavity squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) is one of the most contested questions in head and neck surgical oncology. Approximately 20–30% of patients with early oral cancer who appear N0 on clinical examination and imaging harbour occult nodal metastases — micrometastatic deposits undetectable by any current imaging modality. The fundamental dilemma is whether to perform prophylactic elective neck dissection (END) at the time of primary tumour resection, or to observe the neck and intervene therapeutically only if metastasis becomes apparent during follow-up. This question has been resolved with Level I evidence by the D'Cruz et al. NEJM trial (2015), which established END as standard of care for T1-T2 cN0 oral cavity cancer.

## The N0 Neck Dilemma

### Magnitude of the Problem

- Clinically N0 does not mean pathologically N0: occult nodal metastasis rates in oral cavity SCC range from **20–40%** depending on subsite and T stage
- No imaging modality — including PET-CT — reliably detects nodes <5–8 mm or micrometastatic deposits <3 mm
- Late nodal recurrence after observation carries a substantially worse prognosis because: (a) salvage neck dissection succeeds in only ~50% of cases, (b) multiple nodal levels become involved, and (c) extranodal extension (ENE) is more prevalent at therapeutic recurrence

### The 20% Threshold (Weiss, 1994)

The widely accepted principle is that **elective treatment of the neck is warranted when the risk of occult metastasis exceeds 20%** — the level at which the benefit of END (preventing lethal recurrence) outweighs its morbidity. For oral tongue cancer, even T1 lesions regularly exceed this threshold, making END nearly universally recommended.

### Treatment Options for the cN0 Neck

| Option | Indication | Evidence Level |
|---|---|---|
| Elective neck dissection (END) | T1-T4 oral SCC with >20% occult risk; standard of care post-D'Cruz | Level I (D'Cruz 2015) |
| Sentinel lymph node biopsy (SLNB) | T1-T2 oral SCC cN0; institutional expertise required | Level I (Senti-MERORL 2020) |
| Elective neck irradiation | Radiation-treated primaries; concurrent RT | Level III |
| Watchful waiting / observation | DOI <3–4 mm; very superficial T1; subsite with <15% occult risk | Level III; not post-D'Cruz standard |

## The D'Cruz Trial (NEJM 2015) — Definitive Evidence

This landmark RCT at Tata Memorial Hospital, Mumbai, resolved the decades-long controversy:

- **Design:** Prospective RCT, 2004–2014
- **Population:** 596 patients; lateralised T1 or T2 oral SCC; cN0 neck by clinical examination and imaging
- **Arms:**
  - END (n=245): ipsilateral selective neck dissection at time of primary surgery
  - Therapeutic ND (TND, n=251): observation; neck dissection only if metastasis became clinically apparent
- **Primary endpoint:** Overall survival at 3 years
- **Results:**

| Outcome | END | TND | HR / p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overall survival (3-year) | **80.0%** | **67.5%** | HR 0.64, p=0.01 |
| Disease-free survival (3-year) | **69.5%** | **45.9%** | HR 0.45, p<0.001 |
| Occult metastasis rate in END arm | **30%** | — | — |
| Neck recurrence in TND arm | — | **45.9%** | — |

- **Conclusion:** END significantly improves overall and disease-free survival compared to watchful waiting. This trial provided the first Level I evidence for END and ended the "watch and wait" debate for early oral SCC.
- **Clinical implication:** END is now the **standard of care** for T1-T2 cN0 oral cavity cancer. The only remaining alternative with Level I support is SLNB (Senti-MERORL 2020), which requires specialist infrastructure.

## Neck Dissection Classification (Robbins 2002)

### Terminology Framework (AHNS/AAO-HNS Consensus)

| Type | Levels Removed | Key Structures Preserved | Indication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Radical neck dissection (RND) | I–V | SCM, IJV, SAN all sacrificed | N2-N3 with vessel/nerve encasement |
| Modified radical ND (MRND) | I–V | ≥1 non-lymphatic structure preserved | N+ with resectable disease |
| Selective ND (SND) | ≤4 contiguous levels | SCM, IJV, SAN always preserved | cN0 elective; selected N+ |
| Extended ND | I–V + additional | Extra-regional nodes or non-lymphatic structures | Advanced nodal disease |

### Sublevel System (Robbins 2002)

| Level | Boundaries | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| IA | Submental triangle | Submental nodes |
| IB | Submandibular triangle | Submandibular nodes + gland |
| IIA | Anterior to SAN | Upper jugular nodes (most common site of oral/oropharyngeal met) |
| **IIB** | **Posterior to SAN** | **Upper posterior jugular nodes; less commonly involved** |
| III | Middle jugular | Mid-jugular nodes |
| IV | Lower jugular | Lower jugular nodes |
| VA | Above SAN | Spinal accessory nodes |
| VB | Below SAN | Transverse cervical, supraclavicular nodes |
| VI | Central compartment | Pre/paratracheal nodes |

## Level IIB: To Dissect or Not?

### Anatomical Considerations

Level IIB lies **posterior to the spinal accessory nerve (SAN)**. Dissection requires retraction or mobilisation of the SAN, carrying risk of:
- Shoulder dysfunction (trapezius paresis)
- Chronic shoulder pain and reduced abduction
- Winged scapula in severe cases

### Prevalence of IIB Metastasis in Oral Cancer

| Primary Site | IIB Involvement (isolated) | Overall |
|---|---|---|
| Oral tongue | 2–5% | Rare |
| Floor of mouth | 2–4% | Rare |
| Buccal mucosa | <2% | Very rare |
| Retromolar trigone | 3–6% | Rare |
| Oropharynx | 8–16% | More common (not oral cavity) |

Key finding: IIB is almost **never involved in isolation** — it is involved only when IIA is also positive. In cN0 oral cavity SCC, IIB involvement without IIA involvement is documented in <2% of cases.

### The Pandey RCT (WJSO 2018) — IIB Sparing

The only RCT addressing IIB preservation in cN0 oral SCC:
- **Intervention:** IIB-sparing superselective ND vs conventional SOHND (Levels I–III)
- **Result:** Disease-free survival 83% (IIB-sparing) vs 91% (conventional) — difference not statistically significant
- **Shoulder function:** Significantly better electromyography scores and clinical shoulder abduction in the IIB-sparing group
- **Conclusion:** IIB preservation is oncologically safe in cN0 oral cavity SCC and improves functional outcomes

### Indications for IIB Dissection

IIB dissection is **recommended** when:
1. IIA nodes are clinically or pathologically positive (immediate frozen section triggers IIB dissection)
2. Oropharyngeal primary (higher IIB risk — 8–16%)
3. Large T3-T4 primary with bilateral or posterior nodal spread
4. Level II bulk disease intraoperatively

IIB dissection may be **omitted** (IIB-sparing) in:
- cN0 oral cavity SCC (tongue, FOM, buccal, gingiva)
- Elective (prophylactic) setting
- IIA negative on intraoperative assessment

## Superselective Neck Dissection

### Definition

Superselective neck dissection (SSND) = removal of **≤3 contiguous lymph node levels**, tailored to the expected drainage pattern of the primary tumour. This is distinct from the conventional selective neck dissection (SND), which typically encompasses 3–4 levels.

The term is sometimes used to describe dissection of only **2 levels** (e.g., Levels I–II alone), though most authorities define SSND as dissection of ≤3 non-standard or abbreviated levels based on site-specific oncological rationale.

### Evidence for Superselective ND

- Supported by Shah's (1990) mapping data showing sequential, predictable spread patterns from oral cavity primaries
- Retrospective series and the Pandey RCT (2018) support oncological equivalence with SOHND for selected cN0 cases
- European Archives review (2013) — Superselective ND: rationale, indications, and results — validated SSND for elective cN0 and post-chemoradiation settings (DOI 10.1007/s00405-012-2344-5)
- Reduced morbidity: less shoulder dysfunction, faster recovery, reduced operative time

### SSND Approaches by Site

| Primary Site | Standard SND Levels | Superselective Approach | Omit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oral tongue (lateral) | I, II, III (± IV for T3-T4) | I, IIA, III | IIB |
| Floor of mouth | I, II, III | I, IIA, III | IIB ± IV |
| Buccal mucosa | I, II, III | I, IIA, III | IIB |
| Retromolar trigone | I, II, III (± IV) | I, IIA, III | IIB |
| Lower lip | I only | I (IA + IB) | II, III, IV |
| Hard palate | I, II, III | I, IIA | IIB, III |

## Site-Specific Nodal Drainage Patterns

### Shah's Mapping Data (1990) — Oral Cavity

Nodal metastasis from oral cavity SCC follows a generally predictable, level-by-level pattern (sentinel node concept):

| Site | Most Frequent Levels (in order) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Oral tongue | I → II → III → IV | Sequential; Level IV involved in ~6% cN0 |
| Floor of mouth | I → II → III | Bilateral drainage; crosses midline |
| Buccal mucosa | I → II → III | Level IB most common first-echelon node |
| Lower gingiva/alveolus | I → II → III | Level I dominant |
| Retromolar trigone | I → II → III → IV | Higher skip rate; consider Level IV |
| Upper gingiva/hard palate | II → III | Level I less frequently involved |
| Lip (lower) | IA → IB | Primarily Level I; bilateral for central lip |

### Skip Metastasis

**Definition:** Nodal metastasis to Level IV or V without involvement of Levels I, II, or III.

- **Incidence:** 5–16% in oral tongue SCC; up to 20% in floor of mouth
- **Clinical significance:** Skip metastases to Level IV can be missed if dissection is limited to Levels I–III
- **Risk factors:** Advanced T stage (T3-T4), DOI >10 mm, posterior tongue location, midline tumours
- **Implications:** For T3-T4 oral tongue or posterior oral cavity primaries, extend SOHND to include Level IV (Levels I–IV = anterolateral ND)

| Tumour Feature | Skip Met Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| T1-T2, DOI <10 mm, anterior tongue | Low (5-8%) | SOHND (Levels I–III) adequate |
| T3-T4, DOI >10 mm | Higher (10-16%) | Extend to Level IV |
| FOM, midline | Bilateral drainage | Bilateral ND; consider IV bilaterally |
| Posterior oral tongue | Increased | Include IV |

## Staging Upstaging After END

END provides critical pathological staging information that changes clinical management:

- **30% upstaging** to pN+ found in D'Cruz et al. END arm
- **Prognostic value:** pN+ discovered at END triggers adjuvant therapy planning (adjuvant RT ± chemotherapy)
- **ENE detection:** Microscopic ENE found in 20–30% of pN+ nodes — triggers adjuvant chemoradiation (per NCCN and ESMO)
- **Number of nodes:** Isolated pN1 (single node, no ENE) → adjuvant RT only; ≥2 nodes or ENE → chemoradiation
- **Pathological node ratio:** Ratio of involved nodes to total nodes dissected has independent prognostic value
- Without END, these findings — and their therapeutic implications — are missed entirely

## Contralateral Neck Management

### Indications for Bilateral Neck Dissection

1. **Midline tumours** (crossing or within 1 cm of midline): bilateral lymphatic drainage makes contralateral occult disease likely
2. **Floor of mouth carcinoma**: bilateral lymphatic channels (Stensen's duct lymphatics cross midline) — bilateral dissection is standard
3. **Tongue tumours crossing or approaching midline**: bilateral risk
4. **Ipsilateral Level I involvement** at time of primary surgery: contralateral risk elevated
5. **T3-T4 primaries with bilateral drainage patterns**
6. **Posterior oral tongue**: bilateral drainage via lingual lymphatics

### Contralateral Risk by Site

| Site | Contralateral Met Risk | Bilateral ND? |
|---|---|---|
| Floor of mouth | 20–25% | Yes — standard bilateral |
| Tongue (crosses midline) | 15–25% | Yes |
| Tongue (within 1 cm midline) | 10–15% | Consider bilateral |
| Tongue (lateral, >1 cm from midline) | <5% | Ipsilateral only |
| Buccal mucosa | 5–8% | Ipsilateral preferred |
| Lip (central) | 15–20% | Bilateral Level I |

## Watchful Waiting — When Is It Still Acceptable?

Following the D'Cruz trial, routine observation is no longer standard for T1-T2 oral tongue or FOM cancers. However, observation with close surveillance may remain appropriate in selected circumstances:

### Criteria for Considering Observation

- DOI <3–4 mm (occult metastasis risk <10–15%)
- T1 tumour in a low-risk subsite (e.g., superficial buccal mucosa, hard palate)
- No lymphovascular invasion or perineural invasion on biopsy
- Favourable histology (well-differentiated, pushing border)
- Patient preference with fully informed consent
- Patient medically unfit for surgery

### Surveillance Protocol if Observation Chosen

- Clinical examination every **4–6 weeks** for first 2 years, then every 2–3 months
- Ultrasound ± FNAC of the neck every **6–8 weeks** (ultrasound most sensitive for early nodal change)
- MRI neck at 3 and 6 months
- Salvage neck dissection at first sign of nodal enlargement

### Imaging Modalities in cN0 Neck Assessment

| Modality | Sensitivity | Specificity | Key Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clinical examination | 60–70% | 70–80% | First-line; limited for deep nodes |
| Ultrasound + FNAC | 75–90% | 85–95% | Best for serial surveillance |
| CT neck with contrast | 65–80% | 70–85% | Structural; size criteria >10–15 mm |
| MRI neck | 70–85% | 75–90% | Best soft tissue; retropharyngeal nodes |
| PET-CT | 70–90% | 85–95% | Useful for N2-N3; unreliable <8 mm nodes |

No imaging modality reliably detects micrometastases (<3 mm): this is the fundamental reason END or SLNB remains necessary even after negative imaging.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. The D'Cruz trial is the most important landmark in the management of the cN0 neck: END improves 3-year OS from 67.5% to 80% and DFS from 45.9% to 69.5% — cite this in any exam answer on N0 neck
> 2. The 20% occult metastasis threshold for elective treatment (Weiss 1994) means oral tongue cancer warrants END at T1 stage — do not wait for T2 or larger
> 3. Superselective ND = dissection of ≤3 levels tailored to primary site drainage; for oral tongue, this is classically Levels I, IIA, III (omitting IIB)
> 4. Level IIB sparing is oncologically safe in cN0 oral cavity SCC (Pandey RCT 2018) and reduces shoulder morbidity by avoiding SAN retraction — state this when asked about IIB management in elective ND
> 5. Skip metastasis to Level IV occurs in 5–16% of oral tongue SCC — for T3-T4 or posteriorly located tumours, always extend SOHND to include Level IV (anterolateral ND)
> 6. Floor of mouth SCC has bilateral lymphatic drainage — bilateral selective neck dissection is standard, not optional, even for lateralised FOM tumours
> 7. END provides staging information that changes management in 30% of patients (pN+ found on pathology) — this is an argument for END over SLNB in resource-limited settings
> 8. Occult ENE detected at END is an indication for adjuvant chemoradiation — this finding is missed entirely with observation, worsening the window for curative intent treatment
> 9. IIB dissection is triggered intraoperatively if IIA nodes are positive on frozen section — this decision can be made after examining the IIA specimen before closing
> 10. After the D'Cruz trial, "watchful waiting is acceptable for early oral cancer" is no longer a defensible exam answer unless qualified with DOI <3–4 mm and specific low-risk criteria
