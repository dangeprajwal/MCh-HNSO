## Middle Cranial Fossa Anatomy

### Floor of the Middle Cranial Fossa
The MCF floor is formed by three bones:
- **Squamous temporal bone** — anterolateral; thinner
- **Greater wing of sphenoid** — anteromedial; contains foramen spinosum, foramen ovale, foramen rotundum
- **Petrous temporal bone** — posteromedial; contains arcuate eminence, hiatus of facial canal, tegmen tympani, tegmen mastoideum

### Key Foramina in the MCF Floor

| Foramen | Bone | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| **Foramen ovale** | Greater wing of sphenoid | CN V3 (mandibular), accessory meningeal artery, lesser petrosal nerve |
| **Foramen spinosum** | Greater wing of sphenoid | Middle meningeal artery (+ vein) |
| **Foramen rotundum** | Greater wing of sphenoid | CN V2 (maxillary nerve) |
| **Hiatus of facial canal** | Petrous bone | Greater superficial petrosal nerve (GSPN) + lesser petrosal nerve |
| **Superior orbital fissure** | Between greater and lesser wings | CN III, IV, V1, VI, superior ophthalmic vein |

### Critical Structures in the MCF

#### GSPN (Greater Superficial Petrosal Nerve)
- Origin: **geniculate ganglion** of facial nerve (CN VII) within the petrous bone
- Exits through **hiatus of facial canal** onto the MCF floor
- Runs in the groove on the MCF floor, medially toward foramen lacerum
- Joins **deep petrosal nerve** (sympathetic, from ICA plexus) → **vidian nerve** → **pterygopalatine ganglion**
- **Surgical importance**: GSPN is the **key landmark** to identify the geniculate ganglion and the labyrinthine segment of CN VII
- Injury → **dry eye** (loss of lacrimal secretion via pterygopalatine ganglion)

#### Arcuate Eminence
- Bony prominence on the MCF floor created by the underlying **superior semicircular canal (SCC)**
- Landmark for the position of the labyrinth
- Drilling directly on the arcuate eminence risks entering the superior SCC → **sensorineural hearing loss**

#### Tegmen Tympani and Tegmen Mastoideum
- Thin bony plate forming the roof of the middle ear and mastoid air cells
- Cholesteatoma can erode tegmen → intracranial extension
- Defects repaired via MCF approach

#### Meckel's Cave
- Dural pouch at the petrous apex containing the **trigeminal ganglion**
- Lies at the posteromedial part of the MCF floor

## Middle Cranial Fossa (MCF) Approach — Technique

### Indications
1. **Acoustic neuroma (vestibular schwannoma)** — hearing preservation surgery when:
   - Tumour confined to IAC (intracanalicular) or extending <1.5 cm into CPA
   - Useful hearing present pre-operatively: pure tone average <50 dB, speech discrimination >50%
   - Patient age and choice favour hearing preservation attempt
2. **Petroclival meningioma** — with Kawase extension
3. **Labyrinthine fistula repair** from superior SCC dehiscence (Minor's syndrome)
4. **CSF leak from tegmen defect** (repair of tegmen defect)
5. **Cochlear implant revision** — electrode migration or malposition assessment

### Position
- Supine, head turned 90° (operative ear up) and fixed
- Shoulder roll

### Craniotomy
- Temporal craniotomy: 5 × 5 cm bone flap centred over root of zygoma, just above the EAC
- Positioned two-thirds anterior and one-third posterior to EAC

### Elevation of Temporal Lobe Dura
- Dura elevated off the MCF floor from posterior to anterior (avoids tearing GSPN which enters anteriorly from hiatus)
- House-Urban retractor placed to elevate temporal lobe dura and hold it

### Landmark Identification Sequence
1. **Arcuate eminence** — most prominent bony landmark; confirms position over superior SCC
2. **GSPN** — blue-grey groove running anteromedially from hiatus of facial canal; followed to geniculate ganglion
3. **Macular cribrosa (Ganglion of Scarpa)** — lateral wall of IAC; 60° between GSPN and arcuate eminence (Bill's island)
4. **IAC** (internal auditory canal) — drilled from MCF floor superior surface; opened from porus to fundus
5. **Facial nerve** — identified at geniculate ganglion (superior and anterior in IAC); labyrinthine segment — most vulnerable
6. **Superior vestibular nerve** — posterior and superior; **transverse crest** separates superior from inferior vestibular nerves at IAC fundus

### Tumour Removal
- Tumour removed from IAC via House-Urban retractor
- Preserve CN VII at all stages; CNVII laterally, cochlear nerve anteroinferiorly
- Wax and fat packing to seal opened IAC and prevent CSF leak

## Kawase's Extended MCF Approach (Anterior Petrosectomy)

### Concept
Kawase (1985) extended the MCF approach by drilling the **petrous apex** (Kawase's rhomboid/triangle) — creating a window from the MCF into the posterior cranial fossa to access the **petroclival junction** and **ventral brainstem** without cerebellar retraction.

### Kawase's Rhomboid (Safe Drilling Zone)
The boundaries of the safe petrosectomy zone:
- **Anterior**: GSPN + internal carotid artery (petrous horizontal segment)
- **Posterior**: arcuate eminence (superior SCC)
- **Medial**: cochlea
- **Lateral**: trigeminal impression (Meckel's cave)

> Drilling within this rhomboid exposes the **petrous apex**, **Dorello's canal** (CN VI), and the **posterior fossa dura** between the trigeminal nerve and IAC.

### Applications
- **Petroclival meningioma** (with anterior and middle fossa extensions)
- **Basilar artery aneurysm** (midbasilar, basilar-tip)
- **Epidermoid cyst** of CPA
- **Chondrosarcoma** of petroclival junction — see [[Answers/Skull Base/Skull Base Chondrosarcoma|Skull Base Chondrosarcoma]]

## Comparison: Approaches to Acoustic Neuroma

| Approach | Hearing Preservation | Facial Nerve Risk | Tumour Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Middle cranial fossa (MCF)** | Yes (primary goal) | Low–moderate | <1.5 cm IAC |
| **Retrosigmoid (suboccipital)** | Possible | Moderate | Any CPA size |
| **Translabyrinthine** | No (labyrinth sacrificed) | Lowest | Any; non-functional hearing |

**MCF advantages over retrosigmoid**: Direct superior IAC access without cerebellar retraction; better exposure of IAC fundus (critical for hearing preservation by ensuring complete tumour removal at the fundus without pulling on cochlear nerve).

## Complications of MCF Approach

| Complication | Mechanism | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **Temporal lobe injury** | Retraction contusion | Minimal retraction; intraoperative steroids |
| **GSPN injury → dry eye** | Avulsion during dural elevation | Careful posterior-to-anterior dural elevation; lubricating eye drops post-op |
| **Facial nerve injury** | Drilling near geniculate ganglion | IONM; identify geniculate before drilling IAC |
| **Sensorineural hearing loss** | Drilling into superior SCC or cochlea | Avoid arcuate eminence; neuronavigation |
| **CSF leak** | Incomplete IAC sealing | Fat + bone wax packing; lumbar drain if persistent |
| **Epidural haematoma** | Temporal craniotomy | Drain placement; surgical technique |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> The middle cranial fossa contains the petrous bone (with arcuate eminence, GSPN, tegmen tympani), foramen ovale (CN V3), spinosum (middle meningeal artery), and Meckel's cave (trigeminal ganglion). The MCF approach accesses the IAC from above via temporal craniotomy, elevating temporal lobe dura and identifying the GSPN → geniculate ganglion → IAC sequence; it is the preferred approach for hearing-preservation acoustic neuroma surgery for small intracanalicular tumours. Kawase's extension (anterior petrosectomy within the GSPN–SCC–cochlea triangle) provides access to the petroclival junction for meningiomas and basilar aneurysms without cerebellar retraction.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. The **GSPN is the primary landmark** in the MCF approach — identify it early and follow it to the geniculate ganglion.
> 2. Elevate temporal lobe dura from **posterior to anterior** to avoid avulsing the GSPN at the hiatus (which would cause dry eye).
> 3. **Arcuate eminence** = superior SCC underneath — NEVER drill directly into it without verifying position.
> 4. MCF is the only approach that gives direct **superior access to the IAC fundus** — essential for tumours where cochlear nerve preservation depends on complete lateral-end tumour removal.
> 5. Hearing preservation via MCF requires pre-op pure tone average <50 dB and speech discrimination >50% — these are the standard criteria.
> 6. In **Kawase's approach**, the cochlea is the most medial landmark of the safe drilling zone — drilling medial to the cochlea risks profound SNHL.
> 7. **CN VI (abducens)** runs through Dorello's canal near the petrous apex — at risk during Kawase petrosectomy; seen as first posterior fossa structure exposed.
> 8. The **transverse crest** at the IAC fundus divides superior vestibular nerve (posterior, superior) from the cochlear nerve (anterior, inferior) — the facial nerve is anterior to the transverse crest.
