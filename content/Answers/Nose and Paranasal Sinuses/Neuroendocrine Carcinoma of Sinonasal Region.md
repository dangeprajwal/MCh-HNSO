> [!cite] Landmark Articles
>
> Strosberg et al. (2017) published the NETTER-1 Phase III trial in the New England Journal of Medicine, demonstrating that 177Lu-DOTATATE (peptide receptor radionuclide therapy, PRRT) produced a significantly longer progression-free survival versus high-dose octreotide in patients with progressive somatostatin receptor-positive midgut neuroendocrine tumours — this trial established PRRT as a standard-of-care option for SSTR-expressing well-differentiated NETs, including the rare sinonasal carcinoid, and validated Ga-68 DOTATATE PET-CT as the key imaging tool for patient selection. Horn et al. (2018) published the IMpower133 Phase III trial showing that atezolizumab added to carboplatin–etoposide significantly improved overall survival (12.3 vs 10.3 months) and progression-free survival in extensive-stage small cell lung cancer, establishing the atezolizumab–carboplatin–etoposide regimen as a reference first-line treatment; this paradigm is extrapolated to sinonasal SCNEC given its biological homology with pulmonary SCLC and the absence of sinonasal SCNEC-specific Phase III data. Fabbri et al. (2011) published the landmark study identifying BRD4-NUT1 fusion-positive NUT carcinoma as a distinct entity responsive to BET bromodomain inhibitors in preclinical models, establishing the scientific rationale for BET inhibitor clinical trials in NUT carcinoma of the sinonasal region and other sites.

## Overview: Spectrum of Sinonasal Neuroendocrine Neoplasms

The sinonasal region harbours a **spectrum of neuroendocrine neoplasms** ranging from well-differentiated carcinoid tumours to highly aggressive small cell and large cell neuroendocrine carcinomas. Classification follows the WHO 2022 framework, which grades these tumours by mitotic rate, Ki-67 proliferative index, and morphological features.

### WHO 2022 Classification

| Grade | Entity | Ki-67 | Mitoses/2mm² | Necrosis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| G1 | Well-differentiated NET (carcinoid) | <3% | <2 | Absent |
| G2 | Well-differentiated NET (atypical carcinoid) | 3–20% | 2–10 | May be present |
| G3 | Poorly differentiated NEC — small cell | >20% (often >80%) | >10 | Prominent |
| G3 | Poorly differentiated NEC — large cell | >20% | >10 | Prominent |
| — | NUT carcinoma | Variable | Variable | — |
| — | Olfactory neuroblastoma (ONB) | See dedicated file | — | Separate entity |

### Sinonasal Neuroendocrine Neoplasm Spectrum Summary

| Entity | IHC Profile | Molecular | Targeted Therapy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Well-diff NET (carcinoid) | CK+, Syn+, CgA+, SSTR2+, Ki-67<3% | RB1 intact; MEN1 in syndromic | Somatostatin analogues; PRRT (177Lu-DOTATATE) |
| Atypical carcinoid | CK+, Syn+, CgA+, SSTR2+ (variable), Ki-67 3–20% | — | SSA; PRRT; clinical trials |
| Small cell NEC (SCNEC) | CK+, CD56+, Syn+, CgA variable, TTF-1+ (40%), Ki-67 >80% | RB1 loss, TP53 mut | Cisplatin-etoposide ± atezolizumab; no specific targeted Rx |
| Large cell NEC (LCNEC) | CK+, CD56+, Syn+, CK5/6−, Ki-67 >20% | SOX2 amp, FGFR1 (subset) | FGFR inhibitors in FGFR-amp subset (investigational); cisplatin-etoposide |
| NUT carcinoma | NUT1+, p40+, CK+ | BRD4-NUT1 (or BRD3-NUT1) fusion | BET bromodomain inhibitors (birabresib, NHWD-870); clinical trials |
| ONB (esthesioneuroblastoma) | CD56+, S100+ sustentacular, Syn+, CgA+, CK− | NCAM, cyclin D1 | VEGFR inhibitors; sunitinib (case series); see dedicated file |

## Part 1: Well-Differentiated NETs (Carcinoid and Atypical Carcinoid)

### Epidemiology and Clinical Features
- **Rare** in sinonasal region; more common in the lung and GI tract
- Sinonasal carcinoid presents with nasal obstruction and epistaxis; **carcinoid syndrome uncommon** unless hepatic metastases present (5-HT inactivated in pulmonary circulation)
- Slow growing; may be present for years before diagnosis

### IHC
- **CK+, synaptophysin+, chromogranin A+, CD56+**
- **SSTR2** (somatostatin receptor subtype 2): **strongly positive in G1–G2 NETs** — this is the therapeutic target
- **Ki-67 <3% (G1) or 3–20% (G2)**

### Imaging
- **Ga-68 DOTATATE PET-CT:** images SSTR2 expression; superior sensitivity and specificity to octreoscan (In-111 DTPA-pentetreotide); now the standard imaging modality for SSTR+ NET staging and PRRT patient selection
- CT/MRI: local assessment and staging

## Part 2: Poorly Differentiated NEC — Small Cell (SCNEC)

### Characteristics
- Most aggressive sinonasal NEC; closely mirrors **pulmonary SCLC** in biology, chemosensitivity, and clinical behaviour
- Rapid growth; early regional and distant metastasis (brain, bone, liver, lung)
- **High cure potential to induction chemotherapy** (but early relapse common)

### IHC
- **CK+ (dot-like perinuclear pattern)**, CD56+, synaptophysin+, chromogranin variable
- **TTF-1 positive in ~40%** — does NOT indicate lung origin; TTF-1 is expressed in sinonasal SCNEC
- **Ki-67 >80%** typical; high mitotic rate
- Nuclei: small, oval, finely granular chromatin, **nuclear moulding**, scant cytoplasm
- **Crush artefact** on biopsy — characteristic processing artefact

### Management
- Based on SCLC paradigm (no sinonasal SCNEC-specific Phase III trials)
- **Limited/localised disease:**
  1. Induction cisplatin + etoposide (4–6 cycles)
  2. Re-staging; if response → surgery (craniofacial resection) + adjuvant RT
  3. Or: concurrent chemoradiation as primary local therapy if unresectable
  4. **Prophylactic cranial irradiation (PCI):** considered for complete responders (extrapolated from SCLC; limited sinonasal data)
- **Extensive/metastatic disease:**
  - Cisplatin/carboplatin + etoposide ± **atezolizumab** (IMpower133 regimen)
  - Atezolizumab maintenance after completion of platinum-etoposide
- **Prognosis:** 5-year OS <20%; distant failure (brain, bone) limits long-term survival

## Part 3: Poorly Differentiated NEC — Large Cell (LCNEC)

### Characteristics
- Large polygonal cells, **prominent nucleoli**, abundant cytoplasm
- Organoid nesting, peripheral palisading — preserved architecture unlike SCNEC
- IHC: CK+, CD56+, synaptophysin+, chromogranin+ — identical profile to SCNEC; distinguished by cell size and morphology
- Ki-67 >20%; mitoses >10/2mm²

### Molecular — Targetable Alterations
- **SOX2 amplification** (17q24): transcription factor; no direct inhibitor yet
- **FGFR1 amplification/mutation** (subset): potentially actionable

### Management
- Treated similarly to SCNEC (cisplatin-etoposide based)
- Limited data support TPF induction followed by surgery + RT

## Part 4: NUT Carcinoma (NUT Midline Carcinoma)

### Definition
- **NUT carcinoma** is defined by rearrangement of the **NUT gene (NUTM1)** on chromosome 15q14 — fuses with **BRD4** (most common, ~75%), BRD3, or other partners
- The BRD4-NUT1 fusion protein blocks differentiation of squamous progenitor cells, maintaining a proliferative undifferentiated state
- Now formally a **WHO-defined entity** separate from SNUC and NEC

### Clinical Features
- Occurs at **any age** (paediatric and young adult most common, but no age restriction)
- Aggressive: median survival ~7 months without effective systemic therapy
- Midline structures: sinonasal region, nasopharynx, mediastinum, lung

### Diagnosis
- **NUT1 IHC (C52 antibody):** diffuse nuclear speckled positivity in >50% of tumour cells — **diagnostic**; highly sensitive and specific
- FISH for BRD4-NUTM1 rearrangement confirms
- Morphology: sheets of poorly differentiated basaloid cells, often with focal abrupt keratinisation (squamous maturation islands) — pathognomonic when present
- IHC: p40+, CK+, NUT1+; NE markers negative

### Targeted Therapy — BET Bromodomain Inhibitors
- **Mechanism:** The BRD4 bromodomain in the BRD4-NUT1 fusion protein reads acetylated lysine marks on histones at super-enhancers, driving MYC and other oncogene transcription. BET bromodomain inhibitors (BETi) competitively block this interaction, displacing BRD4-NUT from chromatin → differentiation and apoptosis of NUT carcinoma cells
- **Agents in clinical trials:**
  - **Birabresib (OTX015/MK-8628):** Phase I/II (NCT01713582); demonstrated disease stabilisation in NUT carcinoma; oral BET inhibitor
  - **NHWD-870:** potent BETi; pre-clinical activity in NUT carcinoma models; early phase trials
  - **ZEN-3694:** Phase I with enzalutamide in prostate cancer; BETi activity in NUT carcinoma cell lines
- **Current approach:** Cisplatin-based chemotherapy + RT as standard; BETi on clinical trial (if enrolled); consider histology-agnostic pembrolizumab for TMB-high NUT carcinoma
- **Prognosis:** Dismal — median OS ~7 months; occasional durable responses with BETi reported

## Part 5: Targeted Therapies — Comprehensive Summary

### Somatostatin Analogues (SSA) — Well-Differentiated NETs

| Agent | Mechanism | Indication | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Octreotide LAR** | SSTR2/5 agonist; inhibits hormonal secretion and tumour proliferation | G1–G2 NET, carcinoid syndrome, antiproliferative | PROMID trial (midgut NET); extrapolated to sinonasal carcinoid |
| **Lanreotide (Autogel)** | SSTR2/3/5 agonist | G1–G2 NET | CLARINET trial |

### PRRT — Peptide Receptor Radionuclide Therapy

| Agent | Mechanism | Indication | Key Trial |
|---|---|---|---|
| **177Lu-DOTATATE (Lutathera)** | Radiolabelled somatostatin analogue → SSTR2+ cells → DNA damage | Progressive SSTR+ NET (G1–G2); Ga-68 DOTATATE PET+ | NETTER-1 (Strosberg 2017) — FDA approved 2018 |

- **Patient selection:** Ga-68 DOTATATE PET-CT must show significant SSTR expression (Krenning score ≥2; lesional uptake > liver)
- **Dosing:** 177Lu-DOTATATE 7.4 GBq every 8 weeks × 4 cycles + octreotide LAR co-treatment
- **Side effects:** nausea, fatigue, transient bone marrow suppression; renal protection with amino acid infusion
- **Application to sinonasal carcinoid:** directly applicable for unresectable or metastatic G1–G2 sinonasal NET with SSTR expression

### FGFR Inhibitors — LCNEC (Investigational)

| Agent | Target | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Erdafitinib** | Pan-FGFR (1–4) | FDA approved for FGFR2/3-altered urothelial cancer; off-label compassionate use for FGFR-amplified LCNEC |
| **Infigratinib** | FGFR1–3 | Phase II trials for FGFR-amplified solid tumours |
| **Pemigatinib** | FGFR1–3 | Approved for cholangiocarcinoma; clinical trials in FGFR-driven solid tumours |

- **Requirement:** FGFR amplification/mutation confirmed on NGS before FGFR inhibitor use
- LCNEC with FGFR1 amplification represents a potential biomarker-selected subgroup; no Phase III data in sinonasal LCNEC

### Immunotherapy — PD-1/PD-L1 Checkpoint Inhibitors

| Setting | Agent | Rationale | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| SCNEC (extensive) | Atezolizumab + carboplatin + etoposide | IMpower133 — improved OS in SCLC | Standard of care (SCLC extrapolation) |
| NUT carcinoma | Pembrolizumab | TMB-high; tumour-agnostic approval | Case reports; off-label |
| SNUC / NEC recurrent | Pembrolizumab | PD-L1+, TMB-high | Clinical trials; compassionate use |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Sinonasal neuroendocrine neoplasms span a spectrum from indolent well-differentiated carcinoid tumours (G1) to highly aggressive small cell NEC (SCNEC) and the molecularly defined NUT carcinoma; the key to management is accurate grading and molecular subtyping using a comprehensive IHC panel including somatostatin receptor expression, NUT1, INI1, and Ki-67. Targeted therapy options are histotype-specific: G1–G2 NETs express SSTR2 and are treated with somatostatin analogues (octreotide, lanreotide) and PRRT with 177Lu-DOTATATE (NETTER-1 trial) for progressive disease; SCNEC is treated with the SCLC paradigm of cisplatin-etoposide with atezolizumab (IMpower133) for extensive-stage disease; NUT carcinoma is treated with BET bromodomain inhibitors (birabresib, NHWD-870) targeting the BRD4-NUT1 fusion on clinical trials; and FGFR-amplified LCNEC may benefit from FGFR inhibitors (erdafitinib, infigratinib) in biomarker-selected patients. Ga-68 DOTATATE PET-CT has replaced Octreoscan as the standard imaging modality for SSTR-expressing NETs and is required for PRRT patient selection.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. **Ga-68 DOTATATE PET-CT** is the current standard staging and PRRT selection tool for G1–G2 NETs — it supersedes the older In-111 Octreoscan with far superior sensitivity and spatial resolution.
> 2. **177Lu-DOTATATE (PRRT)** is FDA-approved (2018) based on the NETTER-1 trial — it requires high SSTR2 expression on Ga-68 DOTATATE PET (Krenning score ≥2) and is the most powerful targeted option for progressive sinonasal carcinoid/atypical carcinoid.
> 3. **TTF-1 positivity does not mean the tumour is from the lung** — sinonasal SCNEC is TTF-1 positive in ~40% of cases; clinical context and site of origin, not TTF-1, determine primary site.
> 4. **NUT1 IHC** should be performed on all poorly differentiated sinonasal tumours in young patients — NUT carcinoma can masquerade as SNUC, poorly differentiated SCC, or NEC; NUT1 positivity identifies a BET bromodomain inhibitor-treatable target.
> 5. **BET bromodomain inhibitors** (birabresib, NHWD-870) target the BRD4-NUT1 fusion oncoprotein by displacing it from super-enhancers — this is the most specific targeted approach in any sinonasal NEC subtype and the only potentially disease-modifying option for NUT carcinoma.
> 6. **Imatinib fails in ACC** (c-Kit-expressing tumour) — but do not confuse this with c-Kit signalling in other tumours; always confirm the molecular target is functionally active, not just expressed.
> 7. The **IMpower133 regimen** (atezolizumab + carboplatin + etoposide) is extrapolated to sinonasal SCNEC — there are no sinonasal SCNEC-specific Phase III data, but the biological homology with SCLC justifies this approach.
> 8. **FGFR inhibitors are restricted to FGFR-altered tumours** confirmed by NGS — never use FGFR inhibitors empirically; erdafitinib is FDA-approved for FGFR2/3-altered urothelial cancer and applicable off-label to other FGFR-driven tumours.
> 9. **Prophylactic cranial irradiation (PCI)** is considered for sinonasal SCNEC achieving complete response (extrapolated from limited-stage SCLC data) — the risk of brain metastasis is substantial and PCI reduces it at the cost of neurocognitive effects.
> 10. **Sinonasal NEC carries the worst prognosis** of all NEC sites in the head and neck — even with multimodality treatment, 5-year OS for SCNEC is <20%; frank counselling and active clinical trial enrolment are priorities.
