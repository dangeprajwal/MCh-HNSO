(2017) published the ACR TI-RADS white paper establishing a points-based scoring system across five ultrasound categories (composition, echogenicity, shape, margin, echogenic foci), creating a standardised, reproducible framework that assigns thyroid nodules to one of five risk levels (TR1-TR5) with corresponding size thresholds for FNAC or follow-up — the most widely adopted system internationally. Russ et al. (2017) published the European Thyroid Association EU-TIRADS guidelines, providing a pattern-based stratification from EU-TIRADS 1 (normal) to EU-TIRADS 5 (high suspicion), with emphasis on simplicity and practical applicability in European clinical settings, differing from ACR TIRADS in its categorical rather than points-based approach. Haugen et al. (2016) published the 2015 ATA guidelines incorporating a US pattern classification system for thyroid nodules into five categories (high suspicion, intermediate suspicion, low suspicion, very low suspicion, benign) with specific size-based FNAC thresholds for each category, becoming the dominant reference for North American practice. Rago et al. (2007) published the pioneering study on thyroid elastography demonstrating that an elasticity score of 4-5 had 97% sensitivity and 100% specificity for malignancy in a series of 96 thyroid nodules, establishing the clinical utility of strain elastography as an adjunctive tool for thyroid nodule evaluation. Asteria et al. (2008) developed and validated a 4-point elastographic classification system (score 1-4) for thyroid nodules, reporting 94.1% sensitivity and 81% specificity, and providing the scoring system that became widely used in clinical practice alongside the Rago classification.

## Introduction

Ultrasound is the primary imaging modality for evaluation of thyroid nodules and cervical lymph nodes. With thyroid nodules present in up to 67% of the adult population on high-resolution ultrasound, the challenge lies not in detection but in accurate risk stratification to identify the 5-10% that are malignant while avoiding unnecessary biopsies of benign nodules. Structured reporting systems (TIRADS) and adjunctive technologies such as elastography have been developed to standardise this process, improve specificity, and guide clinical decision-making.

## Role of Ultrasound in Thyroid Nodule Evaluation

### Primary Indications

| Indication | Details |
|---|---|
| Palpable thyroid nodule | Confirm nodule, assess characteristics, measure size |
| Incidental nodule (thyroid incidentaloma) | Found on CT, MRI, PET, or carotid US |
| Abnormal thyroid function tests | Identify structural correlate |
| Family history of thyroid cancer or MEN | Screening |
| History of head/neck radiation | Increased thyroid cancer risk |
| Pre-operative planning | Map nodule(s), assess lymph nodes |
| Post-treatment surveillance | Detect recurrence in thyroid bed and nodes |
| Guided FNAC | Real-time needle guidance |

### Key US Features Assessed

| Feature | Benign Indicators | Malignant Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| Composition | Purely cystic, spongiform | Solid |
| Echogenicity | Hyperechoic, isoechoic | Hypoechoic, markedly hypoechoic |
| Shape | Wider-than-tall | **Taller-than-wide** (growth against tissue planes) |
| Margins | Smooth, well-defined | Irregular, microlobulated, ill-defined, extrathyroidal extension |
| Echogenic foci | Comet-tail artefacts (colloid) | Microcalcifications (psammoma bodies) |
| Vascularity | Peripheral | Intranodular, chaotic |
| Halo sign | Complete, uniform hypoechoic halo | Incomplete or absent halo |

## ATA Ultrasound Pattern Classification (Haugen 2016)

The ATA 2015 guidelines classify thyroid nodules into five sonographic patterns with corresponding estimated malignancy risk and FNAC thresholds:

### High Suspicion

| Criteria | Details |
|---|---|
| US features | Solid hypoechoic nodule (or hypoechoic component of partially cystic nodule) with ONE OR MORE: microcalcifications, irregular margins, taller-than-wide shape, rim calcification with soft tissue extrusion, extrathyroidal extension |
| Estimated malignancy risk | >70-90% |
| FNAC threshold | >=1 cm |
| Management if <1 cm | Active surveillance or FNAC based on clinical context; observation acceptable if not adjacent to trachea/RLN |

### Intermediate Suspicion

| Criteria | Details |
|---|---|
| US features | Hypoechoic solid nodule with smooth margins, without microcalcifications, extrathyroidal extension, or taller-than-wide shape |
| Estimated malignancy risk | 10-20% |
| FNAC threshold | >=1 cm |

### Low Suspicion

| Criteria | Details |
|---|---|
| US features | Isoechoic or hyperechoic solid nodule, or partially cystic nodule with eccentric solid component, without microcalcifications, irregular margins, extrathyroidal extension, or taller-than-wide |
| Estimated malignancy risk | 5-10% |
| FNAC threshold | >=1.5 cm |

### Very Low Suspicion

| Criteria | Details |
|---|---|
| US features | Spongiform nodule or partially cystic nodule without any suspicious features |
| Estimated malignancy risk | <3% |
| FNAC threshold | >=2 cm (consider observation) |

### Benign

| Criteria | Details |
|---|---|
| US features | Purely cystic (no solid component) |
| Estimated malignancy risk | <1% |
| FNAC threshold | No FNAC required (aspiration for symptomatic cysts) |

## ACR TI-RADS (Tessler 2017)

The American College of Radiology Thyroid Imaging Reporting and Data System uses a points-based scoring system across five categories:

### Scoring Categories

#### 1. Composition (Choose one)

| Feature | Points |
|---|---|
| Cystic or almost completely cystic | 0 |
| Spongiform | 0 |
| Mixed cystic and solid | 1 |
| Solid or almost completely solid | 2 |

#### 2. Echogenicity (Choose one)

| Feature | Points |
|---|---|
| Anechoic | 0 |
| Hyperechoic or isoechoic | 1 |
| Hypoechoic | 2 |
| Very hypoechoic | 3 |

#### 3. Shape (Choose one)

| Feature | Points |
|---|---|
| Wider-than-tall | 0 |
| Taller-than-wide | 3 |

#### 4. Margin (Choose one)

| Feature | Points |
|---|---|
| Smooth | 0 |
| Ill-defined | 0 |
| Lobulated or irregular | 2 |
| Extrathyroidal extension | 3 |

#### 5. Echogenic Foci (Choose all that apply)

| Feature | Points |
|---|---|
| None or large comet-tail artefacts | 0 |
| Macrocalcifications | 1 |
| Peripheral (rim) calcifications | 2 |
| Punctate echogenic foci (microcalcifications) | 3 |

### TI-RADS Level Assignment

| TI-RADS Level | Total Points | Risk Designation | FNAC Threshold | Follow-Up |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TR1 | 0 | Benign | No FNAC | No follow-up |
| TR2 | 2 | Not suspicious | No FNAC | No follow-up |
| TR3 | 3 | Mildly suspicious | >=2.5 cm FNAC; >=1.5 cm follow-up | Follow-up at 1, 3, 5 years |
| TR4 | 4-6 | Moderately suspicious | >=1.5 cm FNAC; >=1 cm follow-up | Follow-up at 1, 2, 3, 5 years |
| TR5 | >=7 | Highly suspicious | >=1 cm FNAC; >=0.5 cm follow-up | Annual follow-up |

### Strengths and Limitations of ACR TI-RADS

| Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|
| Points-based — reproducible, objective | Complex — requires familiarity with scoring |
| Accounts for multiple features simultaneously | May underestimate risk of follicular neoplasms (isoechoic, smooth) |
| Specific size thresholds reduce unnecessary biopsies | Ill-defined margins scored as 0 (same as smooth) — debatable |
| Includes follow-up recommendations | Does not account for clinical context (radiation history, family history) |

## EU-TIRADS (Russ 2017)

The European Thyroid Association developed a pattern-based (not points-based) classification:

### Categories

| EU-TIRADS | Pattern | Malignancy Risk | FNAC Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Normal — no nodule | — | — |
| 2 | Benign — purely cystic, entirely spongiform | ~0% | No FNAC |
| 3 | Low risk — ovoid, smooth, isoechoic/hyperechoic, no high-suspicion features | 2-4% | >=2 cm |
| 4 | Intermediate risk — ovoid, smooth, mildly hypoechoic, no high-suspicion features | 6-17% | >=1.5 cm |
| 5 | High risk — at least 1 of: irregular shape, irregular margins, microcalcifications, markedly hypoechoic, solid | 26-87% | >=1 cm |

### High-Suspicion Features in EU-TIRADS

The presence of any one of the following places a nodule in EU-TIRADS 5:
- Non-ovoid shape (taller-than-wide)
- Irregular margins
- Microcalcifications
- Marked hypoechogenicity (darker than strap muscles)

## Comparison of TIRADS Systems

### Head-to-Head Comparison

| Feature | ATA (Haugen 2016) | ACR TI-RADS (Tessler 2017) | EU-TIRADS (Russ 2017) | K-TIRADS (Korean 2016) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Approach | Pattern-based | Points-based | Pattern-based | Pattern-based |
| Categories | 5 (high to benign) | 5 (TR1-TR5) | 5 (1-5) | 5 (1-5) |
| Size threshold — high suspicion | >=1 cm | >=1 cm | >=1 cm | >=1 cm |
| Size threshold — intermediate | >=1 cm | >=1.5 cm | >=1.5 cm | >=1.5 cm |
| Size threshold — low suspicion | >=1.5 cm | >=2.5 cm | >=2 cm | >=2 cm |
| Microcalcifications | High suspicion criterion | 3 points | EU-TIRADS 5 | High suspicion |
| Taller-than-wide | High suspicion criterion | 3 points | EU-TIRADS 5 | High suspicion |
| Spongiform nodule | Very low suspicion | TR1/TR2 (0 points) | EU-TIRADS 2 | Benign |
| Most commonly used | North America | Radiology-dominant centres | Europe | South Korea |
| Inter-observer agreement | Moderate | Good | Moderate-good | Good |

### Sensitivity and Specificity Comparison

| System | Sensitivity | Specificity | Unnecessary biopsy rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| ATA | 85-95% | 40-55% | Higher |
| ACR TI-RADS | 80-90% | 55-65% | Lower (higher size thresholds) |
| EU-TIRADS | 85-92% | 45-60% | Intermediate |
| K-TIRADS | 85-93% | 50-65% | Intermediate |

ACR TI-RADS tends to recommend fewer biopsies (higher size thresholds) at the cost of slightly lower sensitivity, while ATA tends to be more sensitive but recommends more biopsies.

## Elastography

### Principles

Elastography is an ultrasound-based technique that assesses tissue stiffness (elasticity). Malignant nodules are typically stiffer than benign nodules due to increased cellularity, fibrosis, and desmoplastic reaction. Two main techniques are used:

### 1. Strain Elastography (SE)

**Principle:** Tissue is deformed by manual compression (freehand) or physiological pulsation (carotid artery, breathing), and the relative strain (deformation) between the nodule and surrounding tissue is displayed as a colour-coded elastogram.

| Characteristic | Details |
|---|---|
| Compression source | Manual compression by operator or carotid artery pulsation |
| Output | Qualitative colour map — red/green (soft) to blue (hard) |
| Scoring | Rago score (1-5) or Asteria score (1-4) |
| Quantitative option | Strain ratio (SR) = strain of normal tissue / strain of nodule |
| SR malignancy cutoff | Generally >2.0-4.0 (varies by study) |
| Advantage | Widely available, integrated into standard US machines |
| Limitation | Operator-dependent (compression force), qualitative, not standardised |

#### Rago Elastographic Score (2007)

| Score | Elastographic Pattern | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Elasticity in the entire nodule | Benign |
| 2 | Elasticity in a large part of the nodule | Probably benign |
| 3 | Elasticity only at the periphery of the nodule | Indeterminate |
| 4 | No elasticity in the nodule | Suspicious for malignancy |
| 5 | No elasticity in the nodule or surrounding tissue | Highly suspicious (invasive) |

- Rago et al. (2007): Score 4-5 had **97% sensitivity and 100% specificity** for malignancy
- Scores 1-3: recommend observation; Scores 4-5: recommend FNAC

#### Asteria Elastographic Score (2008)

| Score | Elastographic Pattern | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Elasticity in the entire nodule (entirely soft) | Benign |
| 2 | Elasticity in a large part of the nodule (mostly soft) | Probably benign |
| 3 | Stiffness in a large part of the nodule (mostly hard) | Suspicious |
| 4 | No elasticity in the nodule (entirely hard) | Highly suspicious |

- Asteria et al. (2008): Score 3-4 had **94.1% sensitivity and 81% specificity**
- 4-point system is simpler and more reproducible than 5-point system

### 2. Shear Wave Elastography (SWE)

**Principle:** An acoustic radiation force impulse (ARFI) is generated by the US probe, creating shear waves that propagate laterally through tissue. Stiffer tissue transmits shear waves faster. The shear wave velocity (m/s) or Young's modulus (kPa) is measured quantitatively.

| Characteristic | Details |
|---|---|
| Acoustic impulse | Generated by the US probe (no manual compression) |
| Output | Quantitative — shear wave velocity (m/s) or Young's modulus (kPa) |
| Display | Colour-coded elasticity map + numerical values |
| Malignancy cutoff (kPa) | Generally >65 kPa (mean stiffness); some studies use 40-85 kPa depending on parameter |
| Malignancy cutoff (m/s) | Generally >2.5-3.5 m/s |
| Advantage | **Quantitative, operator-independent, reproducible** |
| Limitation | More expensive, not universally available, affected by calcification and cystic change |

### SWE Parameters

| Parameter | Normal Thyroid | Benign Nodule | Malignant Nodule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mean stiffness (kPa) | 10-30 | 15-50 | >65 |
| Maximum stiffness (kPa) | 20-50 | 30-80 | >85-115 |
| Shear wave velocity (m/s) | 1.5-2.5 | 2.0-3.5 | >3.5 |
| Stiffness ratio | — | <1.5 | >1.5-2.0 |

### Diagnostic Performance of Elastography

| Technique | Sensitivity | Specificity | PPV | NPV |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strain elastography (Rago score 4-5) | 85-97% | 80-100% | 55-100% | 96-98% |
| Strain elastography (Asteria score 3-4) | 82-94% | 70-81% | 40-65% | 95-97% |
| Shear wave (mean >65 kPa) | 80-90% | 75-92% | 50-80% | 92-97% |
| Strain ratio (>2.0) | 78-92% | 70-88% | 45-72% | 93-96% |

### Limitations and Pitfalls of Elastography

| Limitation | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Macrocalcifications | Cause artefactually high stiffness — false positive |
| Cystic components | Liquid component invalidates strain measurement |
| Deep nodules | Reduced compression accuracy for strain elastography |
| Follicular neoplasms | Follicular adenomas and carcinomas have similar stiffness — elastography cannot distinguish them |
| Small nodules (<5 mm) | Insufficient tissue for reliable measurement |
| Operator dependence (SE) | Compression force and angle affect strain elastography |
| Background thyroiditis | Hashimoto's thyroiditis increases background tissue stiffness — alters strain ratio |
| Isthmus nodules | Thin tissue layer may give unreliable results |

### Integration of Elastography with TIRADS

Elastography is NOT a standalone diagnostic tool but an adjunctive technique:

| Scenario | Role of Elastography |
|---|---|
| TIRADS low/intermediate risk | Soft on elastography: supports observation; Hard: consider FNAC |
| Bethesda III/IV cytology | Stiff nodule favours repeat FNAC or surgery; soft favours observation |
| Multiple nodules | Prioritise stiffest nodule for FNAC |
| Follow-up of indeterminate nodules | Increasing stiffness over time may prompt intervention |
| Cannot replace FNAC | Elastography does not diagnose malignancy — cytology/histology remains gold standard |

## US-Guided FNAC Technique

### Indications Based on TIRADS

| TIRADS Category | Size Threshold for FNAC |
|---|---|
| Highly suspicious (TR5 / ATA high) | >=1 cm |
| Moderately suspicious (TR4 / ATA intermediate) | >=1-1.5 cm |
| Mildly suspicious (TR3 / ATA low) | >=1.5-2.5 cm |
| Not suspicious (TR2 / ATA very low) | >=2 cm or observe |
| Benign (TR1 / ATA benign) | No FNAC |

### Technique

1. Patient supine, neck extended (rolled towel under shoulders)
2. High-frequency linear probe (7-15 MHz)
3. Identify target nodule; assess real-time under US
4. Prepare skin with antiseptic; local anaesthesia optional (25G needle for LA)
5. 25-27 gauge needle, 10-20 mL syringe with or without aspiration
6. **Capillary (non-aspiration) technique** often preferred — less bloody samples
7. Advance needle into nodule under continuous US guidance
8. Multiple passes (2-3 passes per nodule) — aim for different areas within nodule
9. Express material onto glass slides; air-dry (Diff-Quik) and alcohol-fix (Papanicolaou)
10. On-site cytopathologist (rapid on-site evaluation — ROSE) improves adequacy rates

### Adequacy Criteria (Bethesda System)

- Minimum 6 groups of well-preserved follicular cells
- Each group containing at least 10 cells
- Exception: cystic fluid (may be adequate even without cellular groups)
- Samples with abundant colloid may be adequate even with fewer cells

## US for Lymph Node Assessment

### Suspicious Lymph Node Features

| Feature | Significance |
|---|---|
| Microcalcifications | Most specific for papillary thyroid cancer metastasis |
| Cystic change | Highly specific for thyroid cancer metastasis (especially PTC) |
| Peripheral/capsular vascularity | Abnormal flow pattern — nodal hilum displaced |
| Loss of fatty hilum | Loss of normal echogenic hilum |
| Round shape (S/L ratio >0.5) | Loss of normal oval/reniform shape |
| Heterogeneous echotexture | Replacement by tumour tissue |
| Hyperechoic nodal tissue | Thyroid tissue echogenicity within node — suggests metastasis |
| Irregular margins | Extranodal extension |

### Doppler Patterns in Thyroid Nodules

| Pattern | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Type I — no flow | Benign or avascular |
| Type II — perinodular flow | More likely benign (capsular vessels) |
| Type III — intranodular flow | Intermediate — may be benign or malignant |
| Type IV — both peri- and intranodular flow | Indeterminate |
| Chaotic intranodular flow | More concerning for malignancy |
| Resistive index (RI) >0.8 | Suggestive of malignancy (not highly specific) |

### Doppler Limitations

- Significant overlap between benign and malignant patterns
- Hyperplastic nodules can have prominent intranodular vascularity
- Doppler alone should NOT be used to determine FNAC need
- ACR TI-RADS deliberately excludes Doppler from the scoring system

## Exam-Relevant Key Points

> [!tip] High-Yield Points
> - ACR TI-RADS is points-based across 5 categories; ATA and EU-TIRADS are pattern-based — know the differences
> - Microcalcifications and taller-than-wide shape are the strongest predictors of malignancy on US
> - Spongiform nodule = very low malignancy risk (<3%) — no FNAC needed if <2 cm (ATA)
> - Purely cystic nodule = essentially 0% malignancy risk — no FNAC indicated
> - Strain elastography (Rago/Asteria) is qualitative and operator-dependent; SWE is quantitative and operator-independent
> - Rago score 4-5 has 97% sensitivity and 100% specificity in the original study — but real-world performance is lower
> - SWE cutoff for malignancy: mean stiffness >65 kPa or velocity >3.5 m/s
> - Elastography CANNOT distinguish follicular adenoma from follicular carcinoma — both may have similar stiffness
> - Suspicious lymph node features: microcalcifications, cystic change, peripheral vascularity, loss of hilum
> - Comet-tail artefacts (colloid) are a BENIGN feature — do not confuse with microcalcifications
> - ACR TI-RADS assigns 0 points to ill-defined margins (same as smooth) — this is a common exam discussion point
