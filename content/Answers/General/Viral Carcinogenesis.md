## Introduction

Oncogenic viruses play a major role in the aetiology of specific head and neck cancers. The two most important viruses are **Human Papillomavirus (HPV)**, primarily type 16, which is the established cause of a rising subset of oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma, and **Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV)**, the aetiological agent for nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC). Additionally, HIV increases the risk of HNSCC 2-3 fold. Understanding viral carcinogenesis is essential because HPV-positive and HPV-negative HNSCC are now recognized as fundamentally different diseases with distinct molecular biology, treatment responses, and prognosis.

---

## Part A: Human Papillomavirus (HPV)

### Epidemiology

- HPV is the primary cause of the recent increase in oropharyngeal SCC, particularly in younger, non-smoking patients
- HPV16 accounts for >90% of HPV-positive HNSCC
- HPV prevalence in oropharyngeal SCC: 60-80% in Western countries
- Sexually transmitted — linked to oral sexual behaviour and number of sexual partners
- Rising incidence: HPV-positive OPC has been called an "epidemic" in Western nations

### Molecular Mechanism of HPV Carcinogenesis

HPV is a small, double-stranded DNA virus. In cancer, the viral genome integrates into host DNA, disrupting the E2 regulatory gene and causing overexpression of two critical viral oncoproteins:

**E6 Oncoprotein:**
- Binds p53 via the E6-associated protein (E6AP)
- Promotes **ubiquitin-mediated degradation of p53**
- Loss of p53 function → loss of G1 checkpoint, loss of DNA damage-induced apoptosis
- Also activates telomerase (hTERT) → cellular immortalization

**E7 Oncoprotein:**
- Binds and **inactivates retinoblastoma protein (Rb)**
- Releases E2F transcription factor → forces S-phase entry
- Causes compensatory **p16 overexpression** (loss of Rb negative feedback)
- p16 accumulation → serves as a clinical surrogate marker for HPV infection

### HPV+ vs HPV− HNSCC

| Feature | HPV-Positive | HPV-Negative |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Site | Oropharynx (tonsil, BOT) | Oral cavity, larynx, hypopharynx |
| Risk factors | Sexual behaviour, young age | Tobacco, alcohol |
| p53 | Wild-type (degraded by E6) | Mutated (60-80%) |
| p16 | Overexpressed (surrogate marker) | Lost/inactivated |
| Total mutations | Fewer | More |
| Prognosis | Significantly better | Worse |
| 3-year OS (RTOG 0129) | 82.4% | 57.1% |
| Response to CRT | Excellent | Less responsive |
| Staging | Separate AJCC 8th edition staging | Standard staging |

### Detection of HPV

| Method | Target | Clinical Use |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| p16 IHC | p16 protein overexpression | First-line screening; surrogate marker |
| HPV ISH | HPV DNA in tissue | Confirmatory; distinguishes HPV types |
| HPV PCR | HPV DNA (E6/E7) | Most sensitive; cannot distinguish active vs. latent |
| E6/E7 mRNA | Active viral transcription | Gold standard for oncogenic activity; not widely available |

**p16 IHC** is the most widely used clinical test. Diffuse, strong nuclear and cytoplasmic staining in >70% of tumour cells is considered positive.

## Part B: Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV)

### Epidemiology

- EBV is the primary aetiological agent for **nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC)**
- Endemic in Southern China, Southeast Asia, North Africa
- EBV infects >90% of world population — but NPC develops only in genetically/environmentally susceptible individuals
- Also associated with **oral hairy leukoplakia** and certain lymphomas

### Molecular Mechanism of EBV Carcinogenesis

EBV is a herpesvirus that establishes latent infection in B lymphocytes and epithelial cells. In NPC, EBV expresses a restricted set of latent genes:

**Key Viral Oncoproteins:**

- **LMP1 (Latent Membrane Protein 1):** Functions as a constitutively active TNF receptor — activates NF-κB, JAK/STAT, PI3K/AKT pathways → promotes cell survival, proliferation, and invasion. Considered the primary EBV oncoprotein.
- **LMP2:** Mimics B-cell receptor signaling; provides survival signals; promotes epithelial-mesenchymal transition
- **EBNA1:** Required for viral genome maintenance; promotes genomic instability
- **EBERs (EBV-encoded small RNAs):** Abundantly expressed; activate innate immune pathways; used for diagnostic ISH

### EBV Detection

- **EBV-encoded RNA in situ hybridization (EBER ISH):** Gold standard for confirming EBV in tissue
- **Plasma EBV DNA:** Used for NPC screening (endemic areas), monitoring treatment response, and detecting recurrence
- **EBV serology (VCA-IgA, EA-IgA):** Used for screening in endemic populations

## Part C: HIV and Head and Neck Cancer

- HIV-infected patients have an **increased relative risk of 2-3** for developing HNSCC
- Immunosuppression may impair surveillance of HPV and EBV
- HIV+ patients with HNSCC tend to present at younger age
- Treatment outcomes are comparable with appropriate antiretroviral therapy
- Higher rates of treatment toxicity due to immunocompromised state

---

## Part D: Other Viruses

- **HHV-8 (Human Herpesvirus 8):** Associated with Kaposi sarcoma of the oral cavity and oropharynx in HIV/AIDS patients
- **HTLV-1:** Associated with adult T-cell lymphoma, which can present in the head and neck

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. HPV16 is the established cause of a distinct subset of oropharyngeal SCC — 60-80% prevalence in Western countries
> 2. HPV E6 degrades p53 (via ubiquitin pathway); E7 inactivates Rb → together they bypass both major cell cycle checkpoints
> 3. p16 overexpression is a surrogate marker of HPV — results from loss of Rb negative feedback on p16
> 4. HPV+ OPC has dramatically better prognosis: 82% vs 57% 3-year OS (Ang et al. RTOG 0129)
> 5. HPV+ and HPV− HNSCC are now recognized as different diseases with separate AJCC 8th edition staging
> 6. EBV causes nasopharyngeal carcinoma through LMP1 (NF-κB activation), LMP2, and EBNA1
> 7. EBER ISH is the gold standard for EBV detection in tissue; plasma EBV DNA for screening and monitoring
> 8. HIV increases HNSCC risk 2-3 fold — likely through impaired immune surveillance of HPV and EBV
> 9. zur Hausen's Nobel Prize-winning work established papillomavirus-cancer link and the basis for HPV vaccination
> 10. Gillison et al. (2000) provided the first molecular proof that HPV causes a subset of HNSCC — a paradigm-changing study

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - Always test for p16/HPV in oropharyngeal SCC — it determines staging (AJCC 8th ed.) and prognosis
> - p16 IHC is sufficient for clinical decision-making in oropharyngeal cancer; E6/E7 mRNA is the gold standard but rarely needed
> - HPV+ OPC patients are typically younger, non-smokers, with cystic neck nodes and small primaries — don't dismiss as benign
> - Plasma EBV DNA is increasingly used for NPC screening in endemic populations and for real-time treatment monitoring
> - De-escalation trials for HPV+ OPC are ongoing — do NOT de-escalate treatment outside of clinical trials
> - HPV vaccination (Gardasil 9) targets HPV16/18 and may reduce future HPV-related HNSCC incidence — recommend for eligible patients
