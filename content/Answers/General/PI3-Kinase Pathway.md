## Introduction

The phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K)/AKT/mTOR pathway is one of the most important signalling cascades in cancer biology and is the **most frequently dysregulated mitogenic pathway in HNSCC**. Stell and Maran's Chapter 3 states: the PI3-kinase pathway is an **important downstream effector of the EGFR** and many other membrane-based receptors and is a **central player in cancer pathogenesis**.

Harrison's Chapter 7 confirms: the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway has been implicated in many cancers, and activation of this pathway contributes to **formation and progression of SCCHN as well as resistance to chemotherapy and radiation**.

Constitutive activation of the PI3K cascade is common in HNSCC, occurring in up to **70-90% of cases** (Stell and Maran's).

---

## Part A: Normal Pathway Signalling

### Components of the PI3K/AKT/mTOR Pathway

Scott-Brown's Chapter 6 provides a detailed description of the pathway:

**1. PIK3CA (p110α):** The catalytic subunit of PI3K enzyme. PIK3CA encodes phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate 3-kinase catalytic subunit alpha, which catalyses the conversion of **PIP2 to PIP3** by adding phosphate from ATP to phosphoinositol lipids.

**2. AKT (Protein Kinase B/PKB):** PIP3 activates PDK1, which phosphorylates AKT. AKT is the **active effector component** of the pathway, promoting cellular survival by phosphorylating multiple downstream targets.

**3. mTOR:** Activated by AKT; promotes protein synthesis and cell growth through the mTOR pathway.

**4. PTEN (Phosphatase and Tensin Homologue):** Stell and Maran's states: PTEN is an important **negative regulator** of the PI3K-AKT pathway, regulating PIP3 dephosphorylation, which decreases the phosphorylated AKT fraction and promotes G1 arrest.

### Pathway Cascade

| Step | Component | Function |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **1. Receptor activation** | EGFR/ErbB family + ligand (EGF, TGF-α) | Autophosphorylation and dimerization |
| **2. PI3K recruitment** | PIK3CA (p110α) recruited to membrane | Converts PIP2 → PIP3 |
| **3. AKT activation** | PIP3 → PDK1 → phospho-AKT | Activated AKT phosphorylates targets |
| **4. mTOR activation** | AKT → mTOR | Promotes protein synthesis, cell growth |
| **5. Negative regulation** | PTEN dephosphorylates PIP3 → PIP2 | Opposes PI3K; promotes G1 arrest |

### Downstream Effects of AKT Activation

Scott-Brown's explains that AKT activation promotes:
- **Cell survival** — inhibits pro-[[Answers/General/Apoptosis|apoptosis]] proteins (BAD, caspase-9)
- **Cell proliferation** — activates cell cycle progression
- **Cell metabolism** — regulates glucose uptake
- **Cell movement** — promotes invasion and metastasis
- **[[Answers/General/Tumour Angiogenesis|angiogenesis]]** — activates mTOR → HIF-1α → VEGF
- **Protein synthesis** — mTOR pathway activation

## Part B: Dysregulation in HNSCC

### Mechanisms of Pathway Activation

Stell and Maran's Chapter 3 catalogues the mechanisms:

| Mechanism | Frequency | Detail |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **PIK3CA amplification** | 30-40% | Chromosomal amplification of 3q26.3 locus |
| **PIK3CA activating mutations** | ~21% overall | Helical domain (HPV+); helical + kinase domain (HPV-) |
| **AKT amplification** | 20-30% | Increased AKT protein levels |
| **PTEN loss** | ~30% | Somatic mutation, homozygous deletion, or promoter methylation |
| **Overall pathway dysregulation** | 70-90% | At least one component altered |

Harrison's Chapter 7 confirms: constitutive AKT activation has been associated with amplification of PIK3CA or AKT2, activating mutations in PIK3CA or AKT1, and PTEN loss. In SCCHN tumour specimens, PIK3CA, AKT2, or PTEN alterations were found in **30-50%**.

### [[Answers/General/HPV in Oral and Oropharyngeal Cancer|HPV-positive]] vs HPV-Negative

Scott-Brown's Chapter 6 provides the critical distinction:

| Feature | HPV-Negative | HPV-Positive |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **PIK3CA mutation type** | Throughout gene; helical + kinase domain | Helical domain mutations (E542K, E545K) predominantly |
| **PIK3CA frequency** | >30% (mutations + amplification) | >60% |
| **Concurrent mutations** | TP53 + PIK3CA common | PIK3CA may be the ONLY mutated oncogene |
| **PTEN** | Frequent inactivation | Less common |

Scott-Brown's notes: mutations in PIK3CA are common in HPV- cancers (>30% of cases), and also common in HPV+ cancers (>60% of cases). Typically mutations in PIK3CA for HPV- cancers result in constitutive activation of the protein, whereas for PTEN, which functions as a TSG to oppose the activity of PIK3CA, mutations lead to inactivation.

Lui et al. (2013) found that in a subset of HPV+ tumours, **PIK3CA or PIK3R1 was the only mutated cancer gene** — highlighting the central importance of this pathway in HPV-associated HNSCC.

## Part C: Therapeutic Targeting

### PI3K Inhibitors

| Agent | Target | Class | Status in HNSCC |
|-------|--------|-------|----------------|
| **Alpelisib (BYL719)** | PI3Kα-selective | PI3K inhibitor | Phase II trials; approved in PIK3CA-mutant breast cancer |
| **Copanlisib** | Pan-PI3K (p110α/δ) | PI3K inhibitor | Phase I/II with [[Answers/General/Monoclonal Antibodies in Head and Neck Cancer|cetuximab]] in PIK3CA-mutant HNSCC |
| **Buparlisib (BKM120)** | Pan-PI3K | PI3K inhibitor | Phase II in R/M HNSCC |

### mTOR Inhibitors

Harrison's Chapter 7 provides detailed information on mTOR inhibitors:

| Agent | Mechanism | Evidence in HNSCC |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Temsirolimus** | Rapamycin analogue; mTOR inhibitor | Inhibited 70% growth in SCCHN cell lines at lowest dose; 80% by 21 days |
| **Everolimus** | Oral mTOR inhibitor | Phase I with cisplatin: 1 partial response in 9 SCCHN patients |
| **Ridaforolimus** | mTOR inhibitor | Phase I: 1 patient with SCCHN had SD >4 months |

Harrison's notes: the dysregulation of the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway occurs frequently in SCCHN and has been linked with **poor outcome**. AKT activation, PI3K accumulation, and PTEN down-regulation could reflect the early biochemical effects of **tobacco components such as nicotine**.

### Dual PI3K/mTOR Inhibitors

| Agent | Target | Evidence |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **BEZ235 (dactolisib)** | Dual PI3K/mTOR | PIK3CA-mutant tumorgrafts sensitive; wild-type resistant (Lui et al., 2013) |
| **Gedatolisib (PF-05212384)** | Dual PI3K/mTOR | Preclinical efficacy; combination with MEK inhibitors |

### AKT Inhibitors

| Agent | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| **Enzastaurin** | Combined with rapamycin: efficacy in cell lines and tumour shrinkage in vivo (Harrison) |
| **MK-2206** | Phase I/II trials in HNSCC |

### Combination Strategies

Harrison's emphasizes: resistance to EGFR therapy is associated with upregulation of downstream pathways. Key combinations under investigation:
- **PI3K inhibitor + cetuximab** — overcomes EGFR resistance
- **PI3K inhibitor + MEK inhibitor** — targets both downstream pathways
- **mTOR inhibitor + cisplatin** — synergistic cytotoxicity

## Part D: Clinical Significance and Biomarkers

### PI3K Mutations as Predictive Biomarkers

Lui et al. (2013) established that PI3K pathway mutations serve as **predictive biomarkers** for treatment selection:
- Patient-derived tumorgrafts with **PIK3CA mutations were sensitive** to mTOR/PI3K inhibitor BEZ-235
- **PIK3CA wild-type tumours were resistant**
- All tumours with **concurrent mutation of multiple PI3K pathway genes were advanced (stage IV)** — implicating concerted pathway aberrations in progression

### PI3K in the Context of HNSCC Signalling

Scott-Brown's Chapter 6 places PI3K in the broader signalling context, noting that the most commonly mutated genes in HNSCC are: **TP53, PIK3CA, CDKN2A, NOTCH, FADD, FAT1, CCND1, PTEN, TP63** — and that PIK3CA is the most commonly mutated oncogene.

The TCGA study (2015) confirmed that alterations in **EGFR, FGFR, PIK3CA, and [[Answers/General/Cell Cycle and Cancer|cyclin-dependent kinases]]** represent candidate targets for therapeutic intervention in most HNSCCs.

---

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. The PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is the MOST FREQUENTLY dysregulated mitogenic pathway in HNSCC — activated in 70-90% of cases (Stell and Maran's)
> 2. Key components: PIK3CA (catalytic subunit) → converts PIP2 to PIP3 → activates AKT → activates mTOR; PTEN is the negative regulator
> 3. Mechanisms of activation: PIK3CA amplification (30-40%), PIK3CA mutations (21%), AKT amplification (20-30%), PTEN loss (~30%)
> 4. HPV+ tumours: helical domain PIK3CA mutations (E542K, E545K); HPV- tumours: mutations throughout gene including kinase domain
> 5. In HPV+ HNSCC, PIK3CA may be the ONLY mutated oncogene — central driver in HPV-associated carcinogenesis
> 6. PI3K pathway mutations are PREDICTIVE BIOMARKERS — PIK3CA-mutant tumours respond to PI3K/mTOR inhibitors; wild-type do not (Lui et al., 2013)
> 7. PTEN loss opposes PI3K → decreased PIP3 dephosphorylation → constitutive AKT activation → promotes survival and G1 arrest escape
> 8. Therapeutic targets: PI3K inhibitors (alpelisib, copanlisib), mTOR inhibitors (temsirolimus, everolimus), dual inhibitors (BEZ235), AKT inhibitors
> 9. Tobacco exposure activates the pathway — nicotine promotes AKT activation and PTEN down-regulation (Harrison)
> 10. The TCGA (2015) confirmed PIK3CA as one of the most commonly mutated oncogenes in HNSCC alongside TP53 and CDKN2A

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - For the exam, draw the pathway: EGFR → PI3K (PIK3CA) → PIP2→PIP3 → PDK1 → AKT → mTOR; PTEN opposes by dephosphorylating PIP3
> - Know the THREE main mechanisms of activation: PIK3CA amplification/mutation, AKT amplification, and PTEN loss
> - The HPV+/HPV- distinction in PIK3CA mutations is high-yield: HPV+ = helical domain; HPV- = throughout gene
> - PI3K pathway is downstream of EGFR — this explains why some HNSCC tumours are resistant to cetuximab (activating PIK3CA mutations bypass EGFR blockade)
> - mTOR inhibitors (temsirolimus, everolimus) are the most clinically advanced PI3K pathway drugs in HNSCC
> - PTEN is the key tumour suppressor — its loss removes the "brake" on PI3K signalling and constitutively activates AKT
