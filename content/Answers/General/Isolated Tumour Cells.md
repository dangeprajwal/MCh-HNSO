## Introduction

The ability to detect increasingly small tumour deposits in lymph nodes has been enhanced by serial sectioning, immunohistochemistry (IHC), and molecular techniques such as RT-[[Answers/General/PCR and Microarray Analysis|PCR]]. This has led to the identification of two categories of sub-clinical nodal disease: **micrometastases** and **isolated tumour cells (ITCs)**. Stell and Maran's provides a dedicated section stating that "one of the many goals of translational research in cancer has been to refine disease prediction by detecting tumour-specific molecular alterations in histologically normal tissues either at resection margins or identifying 'sub-pathological' metastases in regional lymph nodes." However, the clinical significance of these findings, particularly ITCs, remains a subject of ongoing investigation.

---

## Part A: Definitions and Classification

### UICC/AJCC Classification (Hermanek 1999)

| Category | Size | Cell Count | Metastatic Activity | Staging Impact (HNSCC) |
|----------|------|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Macrometastasis** | >2 mm | — | Present (proliferation, stromal reaction, [[Answers/General/Extranodal Extension|extranodal extension]]) | pN1-pN3 (stage-appropriate) |
| **Micrometastasis** | >0.2 mm to <=2 mm | — | Present | **pN1(mi)** — upstages the neck |
| **Isolated Tumour Cells (ITC)** | <=0.2 mm | <200 cells | **Absent** — no evidence of proliferation or stromal reaction | **pN0(i+)** — does NOT upstage |

**Key Distinction (Stell and Maran's):**
> "Isolated tumour cells are defined as malignant deposits within lymph nodes that measure <=0.2 mm in greatest extent or appear as single cells or small clusters of <200 cells, detectable on standard processing or immunohistochemistry, that show **no evidence of metastatic activity**. Unlike micrometastases, the presence of ITCs **does not upstage** the pathological stage, with their presence being denoted as **pN0(i+)**."

### Notation System

| Finding | H&E | IHC/Molecular | Notation |
|---------|-----|--------------|----------|
| No tumour cells | Negative | Negative | pN0 |
| ITC detected by IHC | Negative | Positive | **pN0(i+)** |
| ITC detected by molecular method (RT-PCR) | Negative | Positive | **pN0(mol+)** |
| Micrometastasis | Positive or negative | Positive | **pN1(mi)** |

### Exception: Melanoma and Merkel Cell Carcinoma

Scott-Brown's notes that "isolated tumour cells are classified as **N1** in cutaneous malignant melanoma and Merkel cell carcinoma" — unlike HNSCC where ITCs do not upstage. This is a critical distinction for exam purposes.

## Part B: Detection Methods

### 1. Serial Sectioning

- Standard H&E examines only one or two sections per node — a single 5-micron section examines <1% of the node volume
- Serial sectioning at 150-250 micron intervals increases detection of occult metastases
- Jatin Shah Ch11: "Some investigators prefer not to send the lymph node for [[Answers/General/Frozen Section in Surgical Margins|frozen section]] and wait for 'rush paraffin sections' within 24 hours" to allow special processing

### 2. Immunohistochemistry (IHC)

- **Pan-cytokeratin antibodies (AE1/AE3):** Stain epithelial cells within lymph nodes — identify tumour cells that are invisible on standard H&E
- Stell and Maran's: Using IHC, micrometastases can be detected in **5-25%** of [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Node Negative Neck in Oral Cancer|clinically N0 neck]] neck dissection specimens, with **upstaging occurring in up to 12%** of patients
- Dhawan et al. (2016): Pan-CK + serial sectioning detects occult metastases missed by routine H&E

### 3. Molecular Techniques

- **Quantitative RT-PCR (QRT-PCR):** Results obtainable within 2 hours of tissue harvesting (Stell and Maran's)
- Jatin Shah Ch11: RT-PCR for tyrosinase mRNA used for melanoma sentinel nodes; cytokeratin markers for SCC
- **Limitation:** The heterogeneity of HNSCC "precludes the use of a single tumour-specific marker" (Stell and Maran's)

### 4. Sentinel Node Biopsy (SNB)

The primary clinical context for ITC detection:

- **Technique (Jatin Shah Ch11):** Radioisotope (Tc-99m nanocolloid) lymphoscintigraphy + isosulfan blue dye injection; handheld gamma probe identifies sentinel node intraoperatively
- **SENT Trial (Schilling 2015):** 415 T1-T2 N0 OSCC patients across 14 European centres — SNB identified in **99.5%** of cases; established as reliable and safe
- **Garrel RCT (2020):** First randomized equivalence trial — SNB oncologically **equivalent** to neck dissection with **lower morbidity** in the first 6 months
- **Montgomery:** Tschopp et al. found 16 micrometastases in 82 sentinel nodes from 31 patients, upstaging 14 patients from cN0 to pN1. Sensitivity 93%, NPV 94%

## Part C: Clinical Significance

### Current Evidence

Stell and Maran's provides a balanced assessment:

> "The clinical significance of micrometastases is **undetermined**. Prospective studies have used different markers and techniques to assess micrometastases and have arrived at **diametrically opposite conclusions**. In the absence of a universally acceptable marker or a battery of markers, it will be difficult to design clinical trials."

> "Because of the unknown prognostic significance of micrometastases or indeed implications for additional postoperative treatment, **the extra work involved in discovering it on a routine basis is not currently justified**."

### What Is Known

| Finding | Significance |
|---------|-------------|
| Macrometastasis (>2 mm) | Clearly upstages; mandates adjuvant treatment |
| Micrometastasis (0.2-2 mm) | Upstages to pN1(mi); may warrant [[Answers/Oral Cavity/Adjuvant Radiation Therapy in Oral Cancer|adjuvant RT]] |
| ITC (<=0.2 mm) | pN0(i+); does NOT upstage; treatment implications uncertain |

### Arguments For and Against Clinical Relevance of ITCs

| For Relevance | Against Relevance |
|--------------|-------------------|
| ITCs may represent early metastatic seeding | No evidence of metastatic activity (proliferation, stromal reaction) |
| Some studies show worse DFS with ITC+ nodes | Many studies show no prognostic impact |
| May guide adjuvant treatment decisions | Different markers give contradictory results |
| Molecular staging could refine prognosis | Added cost and processing time not justified routinely |

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. ITCs are defined as <=0.2 mm or <200 cells with NO evidence of metastatic activity — classified as pN0(i+), they do NOT upstage the neck (unlike micrometastases which upstage to pN1(mi))
> 2. Detection methods: serial sectioning + IHC (pan-CK AE1/AE3) detects micrometastases in 5-25% and upstages up to 12% of clinically N0 neck dissections
> 3. The SENT trial (415 patients) and Garrel RCT established sentinel node biopsy as reliable, safe, and oncologically equivalent to neck dissection for T1-T2 N0 oral cancer
> 4. The clinical significance of ITCs remains undetermined — studies have reached diametrically opposite conclusions (Stell and Maran's)
> 5. Exception: In melanoma and Merkel cell carcinoma, ITCs ARE classified as N1 — unlike HNSCC where they remain N0

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - **ITC vs Micrometastasis — The 0.2 mm Cutoff:** <=0.2 mm or <200 cells = ITC (pN0); >0.2 to 2 mm = micrometastasis (pN1mi) — this size cutoff is the most commonly tested distinction
> - **The "(i+)" and "(mol+)" Suffixes:** pN0(i+) = ITC detected by IHC; pN0(mol+) = ITC detected by molecular method — both remain N0 but the finding should be documented
> - **Melanoma Exception:** ITCs in melanoma sentinel nodes ARE classified as N1 — this is opposite to the HNSCC convention and a frequent exam question
> - **Single Section Problem:** A standard 5-micron H&E section examines <1% of the lymph node volume — this is why serial sectioning and IHC dramatically increase detection rates
> - **QRT-PCR Speed:** Results available within 2 hours of tissue harvesting — potentially enabling intraoperative decision-making for sentinel node assessment
