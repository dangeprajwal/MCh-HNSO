## Introduction

Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumours (RECIST) is the internationally accepted standard for objectively measuring tumour response to treatment in clinical trials. It provides a common language for reporting whether a tumour has responded (complete or partial response), remained stable, or progressed. The criteria evolved from the original **WHO bidimensional criteria (1981)** through **RECIST 1.0 (2000)** and **RECIST 1.1 (2009)** to the immunotherapy-adapted **iRECIST (2017)**. Montgomery Chapter 5 notes that RECIST is "widely accepted as a method of measuring tumor response" but has significant limitations in head and neck cancer due to anatomic complexity, non-spherical tumour shapes, and the emergence of targeted agents that cause tumour necrosis without size change.

---

## Part A: Evolution of Response Criteria

### WHO Criteria (1981) — The Foundation

Miller et al. (1981) established the first standardized response criteria:

- **Measurement:** Bidimensional — product of the longest diameter (LD) x greatest perpendicular diameter (PD)
- **Response Categories:**
  - **Complete Response (CR):** Complete disappearance of all known disease
  - **Partial Response (PR):** >=50% decrease in sum of products of all measurable lesions
  - **Progressive Disease (PD):** >=25% increase in sum of products or appearance of new lesions
  - **Stable Disease (SD):** Neither PR nor PD criteria met
- **Limitations:** Variability in interpretation, no standardized imaging, no limit on number of measured lesions, inter-observer measurement inconsistency

### RECIST 1.0 (2000) — Simplification to Unidimensional

Therasse et al. (2000) introduced a major simplification:

- **Measurement:** Unidimensional — longest diameter only (sum of longest diameters of all target lesions)
- **Target Lesions:** Maximum **10 lesions** (up to 5 per organ); minimum size **20 mm** by conventional imaging or **10 mm** by spiral CT
- **Non-Target Lesions:** All other lesions assessed qualitatively (present, absent, or unequivocal progression)
- **Response Categories:**
  - **CR:** Disappearance of all target lesions
  - **PR:** >=30% decrease in sum of longest diameters (baseline reference)
  - **PD:** >=20% increase in sum of longest diameters (nadir reference) or new lesions
  - **SD:** Neither PR nor PD

### RECIST 1.1 (2009) — Current Standard

Eisenhauer et al. (2009) refined the criteria with key updates:

- **Target Lesions:** Reduced to maximum **5 total** (up to **2 per organ**); minimum size **10 mm** by CT
- **Lymph Node Criteria (NEW):**
  - >=15 mm short axis = **target lesion** (measurable)
  - 10-15 mm short axis = **non-target lesion**
  - <10 mm short axis = **non-pathological** (normal)
- **CR for Lymph Nodes:** Must regress to **<10 mm short axis** (not complete disappearance)
- **PD Refinement:** >=20% increase in sum of longest diameters **AND** an absolute increase of >=**5 mm**
- **New Lesions:** Confirmed by FDG-PET if equivocal on CT

### Comparison Table: WHO vs RECIST 1.0 vs RECIST 1.1

| Feature | WHO (1981) | RECIST 1.0 (2000) | RECIST 1.1 (2009) |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Measurement | Bidimensional (LD x PD) | Unidimensional (LD) | Unidimensional (LD) |
| PR Threshold | >=50% decrease in product | >=30% decrease in sum LD | >=30% decrease in sum LD |
| PD Threshold | >=25% increase in product | >=20% increase in sum LD | >=20% increase + 5 mm absolute |
| Target Lesions | Not specified | Max 10 (5/organ), >=20 mm | Max 5 (2/organ), >=10 mm |
| Lymph Nodes | Not specified | Not specified | Short axis >=15 mm = target |
| CR for Nodes | Not specified | Complete disappearance | Regression to <10 mm short axis |

## Part B: RECIST 1.1 — Practical Application

### Step-by-Step Assessment Protocol

1. **Baseline Imaging:** CT or MRI within 4 weeks before treatment; identify all measurable lesions
2. **Select Target Lesions:** Up to 5 total (2 per organ); choose the largest, most reproducibly measurable lesions; lymph nodes must have short axis >=15 mm
3. **Measure:** Record the longest diameter of each target lesion (short axis for lymph nodes)
4. **Sum of Diameters (SoD):** Calculate the sum of all target lesion diameters — this is the **baseline SoD**
5. **Follow-Up Imaging:** Repeat at protocol-specified intervals (typically every 6-8 weeks)
6. **Compare:** Calculate % change from baseline (for PR) and from nadir (for PD)
7. **Classify Response:**

| Response | Criteria |
|----------|---------|
| **Complete Response (CR)** | Disappearance of all target lesions; all pathological lymph nodes <10 mm short axis |
| **Partial Response (PR)** | >=30% decrease in SoD from baseline |
| **Progressive Disease (PD)** | >=20% increase in SoD from nadir + >=5 mm absolute increase; or new lesions |
| **Stable Disease (SD)** | Neither PR nor PD criteria met |

### Non-Target Lesion Assessment

| Non-Target Response | Criteria |
|--------------------|---------|
| CR | Disappearance of all non-target lesions; normalization of tumour markers |
| Non-CR/Non-PD | Persistence of one or more non-target lesion(s) |
| PD | Unequivocal progression of existing non-target lesions |

### Overall Response Determination

| Target Lesions | Non-Target Lesions | New Lesions | Overall Response |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| CR | CR | No | **CR** |
| CR | Non-CR/Non-PD | No | **PR** |
| PR | Non-PD | No | **PR** |
| SD | Non-PD | No | **SD** |
| PD | Any | Yes/No | **PD** |
| Any | PD | Yes/No | **PD** |
| Any | Any | Yes | **PD** |

## Part C: iRECIST — Adaptation for Immunotherapy

Seymour et al. (2017) developed iRECIST to address the unique response patterns seen with immune checkpoint inhibitors (e.g., pembrolizumab, nivolumab):

### The Problem: Pseudoprogression

- Immune checkpoint inhibitors can cause initial **tumour enlargement or new lesions** due to immune cell infiltration before true tumour regression
- This "pseudoprogression" would be classified as PD by conventional RECIST, potentially leading to premature treatment discontinuation
- Scott-Brown's notes that in KEYNOTE-012, 20% of pembrolizumab-treated HNSCC patients satisfied RECIST v1.1 criteria for response

### iRECIST Categories

| Category | Definition |
|----------|-----------|
| **iCR** | Complete response (same as RECIST 1.1) |
| **iPR** | Partial response (same as RECIST 1.1) |
| **iSD** | Stable disease (same as RECIST 1.1) |
| **iUPD** | Unconfirmed progressive disease — initial progression that requires **confirmation** at next assessment (4-8 weeks) |
| **iCPD** | Confirmed progressive disease — progression confirmed on repeat imaging |

### Key Principle

- When initial PD is detected, it is designated **iUPD** (unconfirmed)
- Treatment may continue while awaiting confirmation
- If next assessment at 4-8 weeks shows further progression → **iCPD** (confirmed) → treatment stopped
- If next assessment shows stability or improvement → iUPD is "reset" and conventional RECIST categories apply

## Part D: Limitations of RECIST in Head and Neck Cancer

Montgomery Chapter 5 provides critical insight into RECIST limitations specific to HNC:

### Anatomic Limitations

1. **Non-Spherical Tumours:** RECIST relies on single-diameter measurement that works best for spherical masses. HNC tumours are often **irregular, flat, or mucosal-based** — a lesion may clinically shrink or flatten, but the longest diameter remains unchanged
2. **Anatomic Complexity:** The complex anatomy of the head and neck (multiple tissue planes, air-tissue interfaces, bone) makes reproducible measurement challenging
3. **Treatment-Related Confounders:** Post-treatment **oedema, ulceration, and bacterial superinfection** confound CT measurements

### Biological Limitations

4. **Targeted Agents:** Montgomery notes that "traditional response rates determined by measurement of size do not apply to targeted agents, which may cause central **tumour necrosis** or reduction in metabolic activity **without change in size**"
5. **Pathological Correlation:** Patil et al. (2013, TMH) demonstrated **no linear correlation** between RECIST-based radiological size change and pathological response after induction chemotherapy in HNSCC (Spearman r = -0.039, P = 0.857)
6. **Pseudoprogression:** Immunotherapy with checkpoint inhibitors can cause initial enlargement before regression

### Alternative Approaches

Montgomery notes that **FDG-[[Answers/General/PET-CT in Head and Neck Cancer|PET/CT]]** has gained interest as an alternative response assessment tool:
- Overall accuracy: **85.5%** (PET/CT) vs **67.9%** (CT alone)
- PET-based criteria (PERCIST) assess metabolic response rather than size
- Particularly useful for targeted agents where size change may not reflect biological response
- Montgomery: "time to progression or progression-free survival may be more valid 'measurements' of efficacy than traditional criteria of size reduction"

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. RECIST evolved from WHO bidimensional criteria (1981) → RECIST 1.0 unidimensional (2000) → RECIST 1.1 (2009, current standard) → iRECIST for immunotherapy (2017)
> 2. RECIST 1.1 key features: max 5 target lesions (2/organ), >=10 mm minimum size, lymph node short axis >=15 mm for target lesions, PR >=30% decrease, PD >=20% increase + 5 mm absolute
> 3. Lymph node CR requires regression to <10 mm short axis (not complete disappearance) — a critical distinction from non-nodal CR
> 4. iRECIST introduces iUPD (unconfirmed PD) requiring confirmation at 4-8 weeks to address pseudoprogression with immunotherapy
> 5. RECIST has significant limitations in HNC: non-spherical tumours, anatomic complexity, treatment-related oedema, and no correlation with pathological response after induction chemotherapy (Patil 2013: r = -0.039)

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - **The 30/20 Rule:** PR requires >=30% decrease from baseline; PD requires >=20% increase from nadir — these thresholds are NOT symmetric because they apply to different reference points
> - **Lymph Nodes Are Special:** Short axis measurement (not longest diameter), >=15 mm threshold for target, CR = <10 mm (not disappearance) — the most commonly tested RECIST distinction
> - **RECIST Was Designed for Metastatic Disease:** It was never intended for locally advanced curable HNC assessment, which is why pathological correlation is poor in the induction chemotherapy setting
> - **PET Superiority:** FDG-PET/CT accuracy (85.5%) significantly exceeds CT-based RECIST (67.9%) for response assessment in HNC — consider PERCIST criteria for metabolic response
> - **Immunotherapy Caveat:** Never stop checkpoint inhibitor therapy based on a single scan showing progression — always confirm with repeat imaging at 4-8 weeks (iRECIST iUPD → iCPD protocol)
