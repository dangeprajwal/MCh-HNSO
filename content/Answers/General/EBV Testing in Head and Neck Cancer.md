## Introduction

Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) is a ubiquitous double-stranded DNA herpesvirus (HHV-4) that infects >90% of the world's adult population. In head and neck oncology, EBV has a central pathogenic role in nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC) — virtually 100% of non-keratinising and undifferentiated NPC harbour EBV. EBV testing encompasses serological, molecular, and tissue-based methods that serve diagnostic, screening, prognostic, and surveillance functions.

## EBV Biology Relevant to NPC

### Viral Structure and Infection

- **Double-stranded DNA virus**, ~172 kb genome
- Primary receptor: **CD21 (CR2)** on B lymphocytes; also infects nasopharyngeal epithelium via integrin receptors
- Two infection patterns:
  - **Latent infection**: viral genome maintained as episome; limited gene expression to evade immune surveillance
  - **Lytic infection**: full viral replication with production of mature virions

### Latent Gene Products

| Gene Product | Function | Detection Method |
|---|---|---|
| EBNA-1 | Episome maintenance, immune evasion | IHC, WB |
| EBNA-2, 3A, 3B, 3C | B-cell transformation (Type III latency) | IHC |
| LMP-1 | Oncogene — mimics CD40, activates NF-κB | IHC (65% of NPC) |
| LMP-2A/2B | Mimics BCR signalling, promotes survival | RT-PCR |
| EBERs 1 and 2 | Non-coding RNAs, abundant in all latency | ISH (gold standard) |
| BARTs (miRNAs) | >20 [[Answers/General/MicroRNAs in Oral Cancer|microRNAs]], tumour maintenance | RT-PCR |

- NPC exhibits **Latency Type II**: EBNA-1, LMP-1, LMP-2, EBERs, BARTs
- LMP-1 is the principal oncogene — activates NF-κB, MAPK, [[Answers/General/PI3-Kinase Pathway|PI3K/AKT pathway]] pathways → proliferation, anti-[[Answers/General/Apoptosis|apoptosis]], [[Answers/General/Tumour Angiogenesis|angiogenesis]], EMT

### EBV and NPC: Pathogenesis

- Multifactorial aetiology: EBV + [[Answers/General/Genetic Susceptibility to Oral Cancer|genetic susceptibility]] (HLA haplotypes, chromosome 6p21) + environmental factors (nitrosamines, salt-preserved fish)
- EBV infection is an **early event** — monoclonal viral genome in tumour cells indicates infection precedes clonal expansion
- WHO classification:
  - **Type I (keratinising SCC)**: variable EBV association (0-20%)
  - **Type II (non-keratinising)**: >95% EBV-positive
  - **Type III (undifferentiated/UCNT)**: ~100% EBV-positive

## EBV Testing Methods

### A. Serological Tests

| Test | Target | Clinical Use | Sensitivity/Specificity |
|---|---|---|---|
| VCA IgA | Viral capsid antigen | Screening, diagnosis | Sens 85-90%, Spec 85-90% |
| EA IgA | Early antigen | Diagnosis, monitoring | Sens 70-80%, lower specificity |
| EBNA-1 IgA | Nuclear antigen | Screening (newer) | Sens 90%, Spec 90% |
| VCA IgG | Viral capsid antigen | Past infection (not useful for NPC) | Non-specific |

- **Limitations**: elevated titres may be found in endemic populations without NPC; poor PPV in low-prevalence areas
- VCA IgA and EA IgA historically used for mass screening in southern China
- Serological titres correlate with tumour burden and may rise before clinical relapse

### B. Plasma/Serum EBV DNA (Quantitative PCR)

**Most important modern test for NPC management**

- **Method**: real-time quantitative PCR targeting BamHI-W repeats of EBV genome in cell-free plasma DNA
- **Source**: tumour-derived — NPC cells release EBV DNA into circulation (cell-free DNA)

#### Diagnostic Value
- Sensitivity 96%, specificity 93% for NPC detection
- Superior to serological tests (Lo et al. 1999)
- **Plasma** > serum (less background from lysed lymphocytes)

#### Prognostic Value
- **Pretreatment plasma EBV DNA**:
  - Independent prognostic factor — Lin et al. (2004): 376 NPC patients; high EBV DNA (≥1500 copies/mL) predicted worse DFS and OS independent of TNM stage
  - Correlates with tumour volume, stage, and nodal burden
  - Incorporated into **NPC risk stratification** algorithms
- **Post-treatment EBV DNA** (Chan et al. 2002):
  - Detectable EBV DNA at 6-8 weeks post-RT = high risk of relapse
  - Undetectable = excellent prognosis
  - Used for **response assessment** and **early detection of recurrence**

#### Surveillance
- Serial plasma EBV DNA monitoring every 3-6 months
- Rising EBV DNA levels precede clinical/radiological relapse by **median 6 months**
- Enables early salvage therapy

### C. Population Screening

- **Chan et al. (2017)** — NEJM landmark: screened 20,174 asymptomatic men aged 40-62 in Hong Kong using plasma EBV DNA
  - Positive result → repeat in 4 weeks → if persistently positive → nasal endoscopy + MRI
  - **34 NPC cases detected**: 71% Stage I/II (vs 20% in historical controls)
  - 3-year PFS 97% in screened group
  - NPV 99.995% for single negative test
  - Established feasibility of population-based NPC screening in endemic regions

### D. Tissue-Based Tests

#### EBER In Situ Hybridisation (ISH)
- **Gold standard** for detecting EBV in tissue sections
- Targets EBERs 1 and 2 — abundantly expressed (~10⁷ copies/cell) in all forms of EBV latency
- **Nuclear staining** pattern in tumour cells
- Sensitivity ~100% for non-keratinising/undifferentiated NPC
- Gulley et al. (1996): established EBER ISH as the definitive method, superior to PCR (which may detect EBV in bystander lymphocytes)

#### Applications of EBER ISH
- Confirm NPC diagnosis in metastatic neck node from unknown primary
- Distinguish EBV-associated undifferentiated carcinoma from HPV-associated OPSCC (EBER+/p16- vs EBER-/p16+)
- Diagnose EBV-associated lymphomas (NK/T-cell lymphoma, PTLD)

#### LMP-1 Immunohistochemistry
- Detects LMP-1 protein — present in ~65% of NPC
- Lower sensitivity than EBER ISH; mainly research use
- Membranous/cytoplasmic staining pattern

#### EBV DNA PCR on Tissue
- Highly sensitive but **less specific** — may detect EBV in infiltrating lymphocytes
- Quantitative PCR on FNA of cervical lymph nodes: useful for CUP workup

## Clinical Algorithm for EBV Testing in NPC

1. **Screening** (endemic areas): Plasma EBV DNA → if positive, repeat in 4 weeks → if persistently positive → nasal endoscopy + MRI
2. **Diagnosis**: EBER ISH on biopsy tissue (gold standard) + plasma EBV DNA (tumour marker)
3. **Staging/Prognosis**: Pretreatment plasma EBV DNA level for risk stratification
4. **Response assessment**: Post-treatment plasma EBV DNA at 6-8 weeks
5. **Surveillance**: Serial plasma EBV DNA every 3-6 months for 5 years — rising levels trigger imaging

## EBV Testing in Non-NPC Head and Neck Tumours

| Tumour | EBV Association | Test |
|---|---|---|
| Nasopharyngeal carcinoma (Type II/III) | ~100% | EBER ISH, plasma DNA |
| NK/T-cell lymphoma (nasal type) | ~100% | EBER ISH |
| Lymphoepithelioma-like carcinoma (salivary) | Variable (30-100%) | EBER ISH |
| Oropharyngeal SCC | Rare (p16+/EBER- usually HPV) | EBER ISH to exclude |
| Sinonasal undifferentiated carcinoma | Negative | EBER ISH to exclude NPC |
| Post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder | >90% | EBER ISH, plasma DNA |

> [!abstract] Key Points
> - EBV is present in virtually 100% of non-keratinising/undifferentiated NPC; the association with keratinising NPC (WHO Type I) is variable
> - **EBER ISH** is the gold standard tissue test — nuclear staining with near 100% sensitivity
> - **Plasma EBV DNA** (quantitative PCR) serves as tumour marker for diagnosis (sens 96%), prognosis, response assessment, and surveillance
> - Post-treatment undetectable plasma EBV DNA = excellent prognosis; persistent positivity predicts relapse
> - Population screening using plasma EBV DNA is feasible — Chan 2017 detected 71% early-stage NPC in asymptomatic individuals

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> - Use **plasma** not serum for EBV DNA quantification — serum contains background EBV from lysed lymphocytes
> - In CUP presenting as cervical node metastasis, **EBER ISH positivity** strongly suggests nasopharyngeal primary — even if nasopharynx appears normal on endoscopy, targeted biopsies and MRI are warranted
> - Rising plasma EBV DNA during surveillance precedes clinical relapse by ~6 months — acts as a "liquid biopsy" enabling early salvage
> - EBER ISH distinguishes EBV-associated undifferentiated carcinoma (EBER+/p16-) from HPV-associated OPSCC (EBER-/p16+) — critical for correct classification and treatment
> - Pretreatment plasma EBV DNA ≥1500 copies/mL identifies high-risk patients who may benefit from intensified treatment (induction chemotherapy + CCRT)
