## Definition

MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are **small non-coding RNA molecules** (18–25 nucleotides) that regulate gene expression post-transcriptionally by binding to the 3' untranslated region (3'UTR) of target messenger RNAs (mRNAs), leading to mRNA degradation or translational repression. A single miRNA can regulate hundreds of target genes, and a single gene can be regulated by multiple miRNAs, creating complex regulatory networks.

## Biogenesis

| Step | Process | Location |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Transcription | RNA Pol II → primary miRNA (pri-miRNA) | Nucleus |
| 2. Processing | Drosha/DGCR8 → precursor miRNA (pre-miRNA, ~70 nt hairpin) | Nucleus |
| 3. Export | Exportin-5 transports pre-miRNA to cytoplasm | Nuclear pore |
| 4. Maturation | Dicer cleaves → miRNA duplex (~22 nt) | Cytoplasm |
| 5. RISC loading | Guide strand loaded into RISC complex | Cytoplasm |
| 6. Target regulation | miRNA-RISC binds 3'UTR → mRNA degradation or translational block | Cytoplasm |

## MiRNAs as Oncogenes and Tumour Suppressors

### OncomiRs (Upregulated in Oral Cancer)

| miRNA | Targets | Function | Clinical Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| miR-21 | PTEN, PDCD4, TPM1 | Anti-apoptosis, invasion | Most consistently upregulated; poor prognosis |
| miR-155 | SOCS1, TP53INP1 | Immune evasion, proliferation | Elevated in saliva of OSCC patients |
| miR-31 | FIH-1, ARID1A | Proliferation, hypoxia | Early marker; elevated in oral leukoplakia |
| miR-211 | TCF12, BIN1 | EMT, migration | Promotes tongue SCC invasion |
| miR-196a | HOX genes, Annexin A1 | Migration, metastasis | Associated with lymph node metastasis |

### Tumour Suppressor miRNAs (Downregulated in Oral Cancer)

| miRNA | Targets | Function Lost | Clinical Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| miR-34a | CDK6, SIRT1, Notch1 | Cell cycle arrest, apoptosis | Downstream effector of p53 |
| miR-200 family | ZEB1, ZEB2, BMI1 | EMT inhibition | Loss → EMT and metastasis |
| miR-375 | PDK1, IGF1R | Growth suppression | Consistently downregulated in OSCC |
| miR-125b | STAT3, ErbB2, Bcl-2 | Proliferation control | Low levels → cisplatin resistance |
| miR-99a | mTOR, FGFR3 | Growth suppression | Prognostic marker in tongue SCC |
| let-7 family | RAS, HMGA2, c-MYC | Differentiation | Broadly tumour-suppressive |

## Role in Pathogenesis of Oral Tongue SCC

### 1. Tumour Initiation and Progression

- **miR-21 overexpression** is the earliest and most consistent change — detectable in oral leukoplakia before malignant transformation
- **miR-31** is upregulated in the progression from dysplasia to carcinoma
- Loss of **miR-34a** (p53 target) removes cell cycle checkpoint control
- Aberrant miRNA profiles distinguish normal mucosa → dysplasia → carcinoma

### 2. Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition (EMT) and Invasion

- Loss of **miR-200 family** leads to upregulation of ZEB1/ZEB2 → E-cadherin repression → EMT
- EMT enables detachment, invasion, and metastasis
- **miR-21** promotes invasion through PTEN suppression → PI3K/Akt activation

### 3. Lymph Node Metastasis

- **miR-196a** and **miR-211** are associated with nodal metastasis in tongue SCC
- Loss of **miR-375** correlates with advanced N-stage
- miRNA signatures can predict occult nodal metastasis in cN0 patients — potential to guide elective neck dissection decisions

### 4. Drug Resistance

- **miR-125b** downregulation → cisplatin resistance through Bcl-2 upregulation
- **miR-21** upregulation → resistance to 5-FU and cisplatin
- Restoring tumour-suppressive miRNAs may sensitise tumours to chemotherapy

## Clinical Applications

### Diagnostic Biomarkers

| Sample Type | miRNA | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Saliva | miR-21, miR-31, miR-125b | Non-invasive screening for OSCC |
| Plasma | miR-21, miR-184 | Circulating biomarker for detection |
| Tissue | miR-375, miR-200c | Diagnostic confirmation |

Salivary miRNAs are particularly attractive for oral cancer screening — they are **stable, detectable by qRT-PCR**, and can be collected non-invasively.

### Prognostic Biomarkers

| miRNA | Prognostic Value |
|---|---|
| High miR-21 | Poor OS and DFS in tongue SCC |
| Low miR-375 | Advanced stage, poor prognosis |
| Low miR-200c | EMT phenotype, metastasis |
| High miR-196a | Nodal metastasis, poor prognosis |

### Therapeutic Targets

| Strategy | Mechanism | Status |
|---|---|---|
| miRNA mimics | Restore tumour-suppressive miRNAs (miR-34a, miR-200) | Preclinical; miR-34 mimic reached Phase I |
| AntagomiRs (anti-miRs) | Inhibit oncogenic miRNAs (anti-miR-21) | Preclinical |
| miRNA sponges | Sequester oncogenic miRNAs | Experimental |
| Exosome-based delivery | Deliver miRNA mimics via exosomes | Early research |

**MRX34** (miR-34a mimic, Mirna Therapeutics) was the first miRNA-based therapy to enter clinical trials in cancer but was halted due to immune-related adverse events. The concept remains viable with improved delivery systems.

### Prediction of Treatment Response

- miR-21 levels predict response to cisplatin-based chemoradiation
- miR-125b restoration sensitises OSCC cells to cisplatin in vitro
- miRNA profiling may enable **precision medicine** — selecting patients likely to respond to specific agents

## Challenges and Future Directions

| Challenge | Current Status |
|---|---|
| Delivery of miRNA therapeutics | Nanoparticle, exosome, and viral vector approaches |
| Off-target effects | Single miRNA affects hundreds of genes |
| Standardisation of detection | qRT-PCR, microarray, NGS — no consensus |
| Validation in large cohorts | Most studies are small, single-centre |
| Clinical trials | Very few miRNA-based interventions in HNC |

> [!abstract] Key Points
> 1. MicroRNAs are small non-coding RNAs (18–25 nt) that regulate gene expression post-transcriptionally by targeting mRNAs for degradation or translational repression
> 2. miR-21 is the most consistently upregulated oncomiR in oral cancer — promotes proliferation, invasion, and drug resistance by targeting PTEN and PDCD4
> 3. miR-34a (p53 target) and miR-200 family (EMT regulators) are key tumour suppressor miRNAs lost in oral tongue SCC
> 4. Loss of miR-200 family → ZEB1/ZEB2 upregulation → EMT → invasion and metastasis
> 5. miRNA signatures can predict occult nodal metastasis in cN0 oral tongue SCC — potential to guide neck management
> 6. Salivary miRNAs (miR-21, miR-31) are promising non-invasive biomarkers for oral cancer screening
> 7. miR-125b downregulation contributes to cisplatin resistance — restoration may sensitise tumours
> 8. MRX34 (miR-34a mimic) was the first miRNA therapy in clinical trials but was halted due to adverse events
> 9. miRNA profiling may enable precision medicine approaches — selecting patients for specific therapies
> 10. Major challenges include delivery systems, off-target effects, and validation in large prospective cohorts

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. **miR-21 is the "universal oncomiR"** — if you remember only one miRNA for the exam, remember miR-21 (upregulated, targets PTEN, promotes invasion and chemo-resistance)
> 2. **miR-34a links p53 to miRNAs** — when p53 is mutated (common in oral SCC), miR-34a is lost → loss of cell cycle arrest
> 3. The **miR-200/ZEB1 axis** is the key molecular switch for EMT in oral cancer — loss of miR-200 = gain of mesenchymal phenotype
> 4. **Salivary miRNA testing** may be the future of oral cancer screening — non-invasive, stable, quantifiable by qRT-PCR
> 5. For the exam, frame miRNAs as both **diagnostic biomarkers** (saliva, plasma) and **therapeutic targets** (mimics, antagomiRs)
