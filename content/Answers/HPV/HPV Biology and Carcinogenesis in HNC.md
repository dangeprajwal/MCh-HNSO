## HPV Overview

Human papillomavirus (HPV) is a **non-enveloped, double-stranded circular DNA virus** (approximately 8 kb genome) belonging to the family *Papillomaviridae*. Over **200 genotypes** have been identified, stratified by their oncogenic potential:

- **High-risk (HR-HPV)**: types 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 — associated with malignant transformation
- **Low-risk (LR-HPV)**: types 6, 11 — associated with benign papillomas (oral, laryngeal, sinonasal)
- **HPV-16** is responsible for **>90% of HPV-associated oropharyngeal SCC (OPC)**; HPV-18 accounts for a further 3–5%

### Genomic Organisation
The HPV genome encodes:
- **Early genes (E1–E7)**: replication, transcription, immune evasion, oncogenesis
- **Late genes (L1, L2)**: capsid proteins (L1 = major capsid — basis for VLP vaccines)
- **Long control region (LCR / URR)**: regulatory sequences; E2 binds here to repress E6/E7

## HPV Life Cycle and Mechanisms of Carcinogenesis

### Step 1 — Infection of Basal Epithelial Cells
HPV infects **basal keratinocytes** via microabrasions or ulcerations in the epithelium. In the oropharynx, the **crypt epithelium of the palatine and lingual tonsils** is particularly susceptible due to its reticulated architecture allowing direct basal cell access. Viral entry is mediated via **heparan sulfate proteoglycans** and L1 capsid binding to integrin receptors (α6-integrin).

### Step 2 — Episomal Maintenance (Productive Infection)
In non-transformed cells, HPV DNA is maintained as **circular episomes** (extrachromosomal elements). E2 protein binds the LCR and acts as a **transcriptional repressor of E6 and E7**. The virus replicates in concert with host cell division; productive viral replication and virion release occur in differentiating superficial layers.

### Step 3 — Integration → E6/E7 Overexpression (Transforming Infection)
In a fraction of infected cells, HPV DNA **integrates into the host genome**. Integration typically disrupts the **E2 open reading frame**, eliminating E2 repressor function. The result is unconstrained, constitutive overexpression of **E6 and E7 oncoproteins**, driving malignant transformation.

> Integration is not absolutely required for transformation — some HPV+ cancers retain episomal HPV — but E6/E7 overexpression is the critical event regardless.

## Key Oncoproteins

### E6 Oncoprotein — p53 Degradation and Immortalisation

| Function | Mechanism | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| p53 degradation | E6 recruits **E6AP** (E6-associated protein; UBE3A ubiquitin ligase) → forms E6–E6AP complex → ubiquitinates p53 → proteasomal degradation | Loss of p53-mediated apoptosis and G1 arrest; cells cannot respond to DNA damage |
| Telomerase activation | E6 activates **hTERT** (telomerase reverse transcriptase) transcription | Cellular immortalisation |
| EGFR signalling | E6 upregulates EGFR expression and signalling | Increased proliferation |
| PDZ domain proteins | E6 binds and degrades PDZ-containing proteins (DLG1, SCRIB, MAGI) | Loss of cell polarity, epithelial–mesenchymal transition |

> Clinically critical: because p53 is **degraded** (not mutated) in HPV+ tumours, the TP53 gene is wild-type. This is exploited therapeutically — APR-246 (reactivates p53) is being studied but is less relevant in HPV+ disease.

### E7 Oncoprotein — pRb Degradation and p16 Upregulation

| Function | Mechanism | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| pRb degradation | E7 binds the **LXCXE motif** of pRb (retinoblastoma protein) → recruits ubiquitin ligase → proteasomal degradation of pRb | Releases **E2F transcription factors** from pRb repression → uncontrolled S-phase entry |
| p16 upregulation (compensatory) | Loss of pRb disrupts the **CDK4/6–cyclin D1→pRb→E2F** feedback loop. Normally, CDK4/6 phosphorylates pRb to release E2F; when pRb is absent, cells compensate by overexpressing **p16^INK4a** (CDKN2A), the CDK4/6 inhibitor, in a futile feedback attempt | **Massive p16 overexpression** — the basis for p16 IHC as a surrogate HPV marker |
| Degradation of pocket proteins | Also degrades p107 and p130 | Further disruption of cell cycle checkpoints |

### E5 Oncoprotein (Minor Role)
E5 enhances **EGFR signalling** and prevents lysosomal degradation of EGFR; less important in established malignancy (often lost after integration disrupts early region).

## p16 as a Surrogate HPV Marker

### Mechanism of p16 Overexpression
The paradox: **p16 (CDKN2A) is a tumour suppressor**, yet it is overexpressed in HPV+ cancer. The explanation is that p16 normally inhibits CDK4/6, which phosphorylates pRb. When **E7 degrades pRb**, the upstream feedback signal is lost, and cells — in a futile attempt to restore pRb function — massively upregulate p16. Thus **p16 overexpression = surrogate marker of pRb degradation by E7 = surrogate for HPV infection**.

### p16 IHC Protocol (CAP 2018 Guidelines)
- **Positive**: strong, diffuse **nuclear AND cytoplasmic** staining in **≥70% of tumour cells**
- Antibody: clone E6H4 (CINtec; Ventana/Roche) is standard
- **Sensitivity** for HPV in OPC: **94%** \| **Specificity**: **83%**
- Positive predictive value in OPC: ~95% (given high HPV prevalence in OPC)

### Critical Limitation — Site Specificity
| Site | p16 as HPV Surrogate? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Oropharynx** | Yes — validated | HPV prevalence 60–80%; p16+ PPV ~95% |
| Oral cavity | No | HPV prevalence low; p16 overexpressed for other reasons (PIK3CA activation, Rb loss by methylation) |
| Larynx/hypopharynx | No | HPV prevalence very low; high false-positive rate |
| Sinonasal | No | See [[Answers/HPV/HPV and Sinonasal Malignancies\|HPV and Sinonasal Malignancies]] |
| Nasopharynx | No | EBV-driven; see [[Answers/Nasopharynx/EBV and NPC Carcinogenesis\|EBV and NPC Carcinogenesis]] |

## HPV-Positive vs HPV-Negative HNSCC — Comparative Profile

| Feature | HPV-Positive | HPV-Negative |
|---|---|---|
| Primary carcinogen | HPV-16 (sexual transmission) | Tobacco + alcohol (synergistic) |
| Typical age | 5th decade (younger) | 6th–7th decade |
| Sex ratio (M:F) | Higher male predominance (oral sex exposure) | Male predominance (tobacco) |
| Primary site | **Oropharynx** (tonsil > BOT) | Oral cavity, larynx, hypopharynx |
| Histology | Often **non-keratinising** SCC, basaloid features | Keratinising SCC |
| TP53 status | **Wild-type** (protein degraded by E6, gene intact) | **Mutated** (direct carcinogen adducts) |
| p16 | **Overexpressed** (E7 → pRb degradation) | **Lost** (CDKN2A methylation or deletion) |
| Rb | Degraded by E7 | Often mutated/methylated |
| PIK3CA | Mutated in 20–30% | Mutated in 20–40% |
| CCND1 (cyclin D1) amplification | Rare | Common (up to 30%) |
| FGFR3 | Amplified in some HPV+ | Less common |
| 3p/9p deletions | Uncommon | Common (field cancerisation) |
| Copy number variation | Low (genomically stable) | High (chromosomally unstable) |
| TIL density | High (immunologically hot) | Low–moderate |
| PD-L1 expression | High | Variable |
| 5-year OS | **70–85%** (all stages) | **35–45%** |
| Pattern of failure | Distant > locoregional | Locoregional > distant |
| Response to CRT | Excellent | Moderate |

## AJCC 8th Edition Staging — HPV-Positive OPC (p16+)

The AJCC 8th edition (2017) introduced a **completely separate staging system** for p16-positive oropharyngeal SCC, reflecting its fundamentally different natural history. This is one of the highest-yield exam topics.

### Why a Separate System?
HPV+ OPC frequently presents with **large nodal metastases** and **small primary tumours** (often T0 or T1 primary with N2–3 neck disease). The old AJCC 7th edition classified such patients as Stage IVA (portending poor prognosis), yet their actual 5-year OS was 80%+. The new staging corrects this — most HPV+ OPC patients fall into Stage I or II under the 8th edition.

### T Classification (same as HPV-negative OPC)
- T1: ≤2 cm
- T2: >2 cm, ≤4 cm
- T3: >4 cm OR extension to epiglottis (lingual surface)
- T4: moderately or very advanced local disease (invades larynx, extrinsic tongue muscles, medial pterygoid, hard palate, mandible, lateral pterygoid, pterygoid plates, lateral nasopharynx, skull base, or encases carotid)

### N Classification (HPV-positive — COMPLETELY DIFFERENT)
| N Stage | HPV-Positive OPC | HPV-Negative/Other HNSCC |
|---|---|---|
| N0 | No regional nodes | No regional nodes |
| N1 | **Ipsilateral nodes, ≤4 cm** | Single ipsilateral node, ≤3 cm |
| N2 | **Ipsilateral >4 cm \| bilateral \| contralateral, ≤6 cm** | Multiple ipsilateral; bilateral; contralateral |
| N3 | **Any node >6 cm OR ENE+** | >6 cm OR ENE+ |

> Key difference: HPV+ OPC has **NO N2a/b/c** subcategories. Contralateral nodes are classified as N2, not N3. ENE is less prognostically important (hence N3 threshold is higher). Extranodal extension in HPV+ OPC does NOT automatically confer Stage IV.

### Stage Grouping (Clinical Staging)
| Stage | T | N |
|---|---|---|
| **I** | T0–T2 | N0–N2 |
| **II** | T3 | N0–N2; OR T0–T2 N3 |
| **III** | T4 any N; OR T0–T4 N3 |

> Stage III in HPV+ OPC corresponds roughly to old Stage IVA in HPV- classification. A patient with T2N2M0 HPV+ OPC is **Stage I** (excellent prognosis), whereas the same node staging in HPV- disease is Stage IVA.

### Pathological Staging (pTNM for post-surgical specimens)
Pathological N staging for HPV+ OPC:
- pN0: no nodes
- pN1: 1–4 positive nodes, no ENE
- pN2: ≥5 positive nodes OR any ENE+
- pN3: not used in current AJCC 8th Ed HPV+ OPC pathological scheme (ENE+ = pN2)

## Prognosis

- **5-year OS**: HPV+ OPC 70–85% vs HPV- OPC 35–45%
- Superior disease-free survival, locoregional control, and overall survival across all T and N categories
- HPV status is the **single strongest prognostic factor** in OPC (stronger than T-stage, N-stage, or treatment modality)
- Mechanism of better prognosis: wild-type p53 (capable of apoptosis when p53 is reactivated post-treatment); high TIL density (immune-mediated tumour killing); lower genomic instability; better response to radiotherapy and cisplatin
- Despite better prognosis, HPV+ OPC can develop **late distant metastases** (lung, bone, liver) — more so than HPV-; median time to distant failure 18–24 months; late failure (>3 years) has better prognosis than early

---

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> HPV-16 drives oropharyngeal SCC carcinogenesis through E6-mediated p53 degradation and E7-mediated pRb degradation; E7-induced pRb loss causes compensatory p16 overexpression, making p16 IHC a validated surrogate HPV marker in the oropharynx only. AJCC 8th edition recognises a completely separate staging system for p16+ OPC with simplified N categories (N1: ipsilateral ≤4 cm; N2: >4 cm or contralateral/bilateral; N3: >6 cm), resulting in most HPV+ OPC patients staging as Stage I–II with correspondingly excellent 5-year OS of 70–85%.

---

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. HPV-16 accounts for >90% of all HPV-associated oropharyngeal SCC; HPV-18 contributes a further 3–5%.
> 2. E6 degrades p53 via the E6AP ubiquitin ligase pathway — hence TP53 is WILD-TYPE in HPV+ tumours (degraded, not mutated).
> 3. E7 degrades pRb, releasing E2F. Compensatory upregulation of p16 (the CDK4/6 inhibitor that normally keeps pRb dephosphorylated) produces the paradoxical p16 overexpression used as the IHC surrogate.
> 4. p16 IHC is a valid HPV surrogate ONLY in the oropharynx — false-positive rate is unacceptably high in oral cavity, larynx, and sinonasal sites.
> 5. AJCC 8th edition HPV+ OPC N-staging eliminates N2a/b/c: ipsilateral ≤4 cm = N1; >4 cm or contralateral = N2; >6 cm = N3. Stage I encompasses T0–T2, N0–N2.
> 6. HPV+ HNSCC is genomically stable (low copy number variation) with high TIL density — "immunologically hot" — explaining superior response to both CRT and immunotherapy.
> 7. Tobacco smoking significantly WORSENS prognosis in HPV+ OPC; smokers with HPV+ OPC have intermediate prognosis between non-smokers HPV+ and HPV- patients.
> 8. Integration of HPV DNA disrupts the E2 ORF, removing E2-mediated repression of the E6/E7 promoter — this is the key event driving E6/E7 overexpression.
