## Rationale for De-escalation

### Why De-escalate?
Standard treatment for locally advanced oropharyngeal SCC is **concurrent cisplatin-based CRT**:
- External beam RT: **70 Gy** in 35 fractions over 7 weeks
- Concurrent cisplatin: **100 mg/m²** every 3 weeks × 3 cycles (or weekly 40 mg/m² × 7 cycles)

This regimen achieves excellent tumour control (3-year OS 80–90% in HPV+ OPC) but causes significant **late toxicity**:

| Late Toxicity | Incidence in Standard CRT |
|---|---|
| Severe dysphagia (PEG dependency at 1 year) | 20–40% |
| Xerostomia (Grade 3) | 15–30% |
| Osteoradionecrosis | 3–8% |
| Hypothyroidism | 30–50% |
| Hearing loss (cisplatin ototoxicity) | 15–30% |
| Peripheral neuropathy (cisplatin) | 10–25% |
| Nephrotoxicity | 10–20% |

HPV+ OPC patients are:
- **Younger** (5th decade) — decades of life remaining with these toxicities
- **Excellent prognosis** — many are cured with standard treatment; the incremental benefit of full-intensity treatment must be weighed against lifelong toxicity
- Potentially **over-treated** by standard CRT designed for tobacco-driven (HPV-) HNC

### De-escalation Hypothesis
*"If oncological outcomes are maintained, a less toxic regimen would be preferred for HPV+ OPC."*

Three main de-escalation strategies have been tested:
1. **Substitution**: replace cisplatin with a less toxic agent (cetuximab)
2. **Radiation dose reduction**: decrease RT from 70 Gy to 50–60 Gy
3. **Surgery-first (TORS) to enable adjuvant RT alone** (avoid concurrent chemotherapy)

---

## Strategy 1 — Substitution: Replace Cisplatin with Cetuximab

### Background
Cetuximab (anti-EGFR monoclonal antibody) + RT (Bonner regimen) was approved for locally advanced HNSCC in 2006 based on the Bonner trial (OS benefit vs RT alone). Hypothesis: cetuximab-RT less toxic than cisplatin-RT while maintaining efficacy in HPV+ OPC.

### De-ESCALaTE HPV Trial (Mehanna 2019, Lancet)
- **Design**: Phase III, open-label, randomised trial
- **Population**: HPV+ T3–T4 or N2–N3 OPC (excluding T1–T2N0–N1 early disease)
- **Arms**: Cetuximab-RT (70 Gy) vs Cisplatin-RT (70 Gy + cisplatin 100 mg/m² ×3)
- **Result**: **Cetuximab-RT had WORSE overall survival** (2-year OS: 86.5% cetuximab vs 97.5% cisplatin; HR for death = 5.0)
- **Toxicity**: cetuximab-RT had similar serious adverse event rates; NOT less toxic in absolute terms (different toxicity profile — acneiform rash, infusion reactions vs ototoxicity, nephrotoxicity)
- **Conclusion**: **Cetuximab is INFERIOR to cisplatin** in HPV+ OPC; cetuximab is NOT an acceptable de-escalation agent

### RTOG 1016 Trial (Gillison 2019, Lancet)
- **Design**: Phase III, open-label, non-inferiority randomised trial (n=849)
- **Arms**: Cetuximab-RT (70 Gy) vs Cisplatin-RT (70 Gy + cisplatin 100 mg/m² ×3)
- **Result**: **Non-inferiority NOT demonstrated** — cetuximab-RT inferior
  - 5-year OS: 77.9% cetuximab-RT vs 84.6% cisplatin-RT (statistically significant difference)
  - 5-year PFS: 67.7% vs 78.4%
- **Toxicity**: cetuximab arm had lower rates of feeding tube use at 1 year, but similar severe toxicity overall
- **Conclusion**: Confirms De-ESCALaTE — **cisplatin remains the standard concurrent agent** for HPV+ OPC; cetuximab is not acceptable in this population

> **Bottom line on substitution: Cetuximab ABANDONED as de-escalation agent in HPV+ OPC. Cisplatin is the gold standard concurrent agent.**

## Strategy 2 — Radiation Dose Reduction

### Rationale
If cisplatin is retained, can the radiation dose be safely reduced from 70 Gy to 50–60 Gy?

### ECOG-ACRIN E3311 (Price 2020, J Clin Oncol)
- **Design**: Phase II randomised trial of TORS followed by **risk-stratified adjuvant RT**
- **Pathological risk groups and adjuvant therapy**:
  | Risk Group | Criteria | Adjuvant Therapy |
  |---|---|---|
  | Low | Clear margins (>3 mm), pN0–N1 without ENE | **50 Gy RT** |
  | Intermediate | Close margins (1–3 mm), pN1–N2 without ENE | **60 Gy RT** |
  | High | Positive margins, ENE+ | **66 Gy + cisplatin** |
- **Result**: 2-year PFS 96% (low risk), 94% (intermediate), 91% (high) — **acceptable in all groups**
- Low-risk and intermediate-risk patients treated with 50–60 Gy maintained excellent oncological control
- **Conclusion**: TORS-based pathological risk stratification allows safe de-escalation of adjuvant RT dose to 50 Gy in low-risk and 60 Gy in intermediate-risk HPV+ OPC

### NRG HN002 (Yom 2021, J Clin Oncol)
- **Design**: Phase II randomised trial
- **Arms**: 60 Gy + weekly cisplatin (40 mg/m² ×6) vs 60 Gy alone (no chemotherapy)
- **Population**: T1–T2, N1–N2b OPC, HPV+, non-smokers or light smokers (≤10 pack-years)
- **Result**: 60 Gy + weekly cisplatin arm met pre-specified 2-year PFS threshold (90.5%); 60 Gy alone arm did NOT (87.6% — below pre-specified threshold of 88%)
- **Conclusion**: Weekly cisplatin with 60 Gy RT is a promising de-escalated regimen for selected low-risk HPV+ OPC; further study needed

### PATHOS Trial (Ongoing)
- **Design**: Phase III RCT for HPV+ OPC treated with primary TORS + neck dissection
- **Stratification**: standard risk (no adjuvant), low risk (50 Gy RT), high risk (60 Gy ± cisplatin)
- Primary endpoint: MD Anderson Dysphagia Inventory (MDADI) at 12 months
- Results awaited — key trial for definitive answer on TORS-based de-escalation

### MC1273 (Quon 2021)
- Phase II trial: 60 Gy vs 54 Gy in HPV+ OPC (non-surgical)
- 54 Gy with reduced cisplatin dose maintained acceptable locoregional control
- Data maturing; not yet practice-changing

## Strategy 3 — TORS-Based De-escalation (Surgery First)

### Rationale
Primary TORS followed by adjuvant RT only (without concurrent cisplatin) for low-risk patients:
- Avoids cisplatin toxicity in 50–60% of HPV+ OPC patients with low-risk pathological features
- Adjuvant RT (50–60 Gy without cisplatin) substantially less toxic than concurrent cisplatin-CRT

### ORATOR Trial (Palma 2020, Lancet)
- **Design**: Phase II RCT — TORS (+ neck dissection, + risk-stratified adjuvant RT) vs primary RT (70 Gy ± concurrent therapy)
- **Population**: T1–T2, N0–N2 OPC (mixed HPV status)
- **Result**:
  - Similar 2-year OS (RT 91% vs TORS 92%)
  - Similar locoregional control
  - **RT group better MDADI score at 1 year** (swallowing)
  - **TORS group better physical function QoL at 2 years**
- **Conclusion**: Both approaches are valid for T1–T2 OPC; TORS allows de-escalation of adjuvant therapy (cisplatin avoidance); RT modality choice depends on institutional expertise and patient preference

### The TORS De-escalation Pathway
```
Primary TORS + Bilateral/ipsilateral neck dissection
                    |
          Pathological risk assessment
         /           |              \
Low risk         Intermediate      High risk
Clear margins    Close margins     +ve margins
pN0–N1 no ENE    pN2 no ENE        ENE+
      |               |                 |
   50 Gy RT      60 Gy RT         66 Gy + cisplatin
(No cisplatin!)  (No cisplatin!)   (Full CRT)
```

In this pathway, ~50–60% of HPV+ OPC patients qualify as low or intermediate risk → avoid cisplatin entirely.

## Strategy 4 — Induction Chemotherapy Bioselection

### Concept
Use induction chemotherapy (IC) response as a **bioselection tool** to identify patients likely to achieve complete response with de-escalated definitive therapy:
- Complete responders after IC → de-escalated RT (60 Gy without cisplatin)
- Non-responders → standard RT-CRT (70 Gy + cisplatin)

### OPTIMA Trial (Seiwert 2019)
- **Design**: Phase II single-arm bioselection study
- **Induction**: Carboplatin AUC6 + nab-paclitaxel 100 mg/m² days 1, 8, 15 × 2 cycles
- **Complete responders** (>50% response): 60 Gy RT with cetuximab
- **Non/partial responders**: 60 Gy RT + cisplatin; poor responders → 75 Gy + cisplatin
- **Result**: 2-year PFS 95% for complete responders on de-escalated arm; 80% for non-responders on standard arm
- **Limitation**: lack of predictive biomarkers to select responders without IC trial; IC itself causes toxicity

### OPTIMA2 (Ongoing)
- Refining bioselection approach with improved IC regimen and better response criteria

## Current Standard of Care (2025)

| Clinical Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Non-surgical HPV+ OPC (T1–T4 N0–N3) | **Cisplatin-RT 70 Gy** (standard); cetuximab is INFERIOR |
| TORS-eligible HPV+ OPC (T1–T2, accessible) | TORS + neck dissection → risk-stratified adjuvant RT (50–66 Gy ± cisplatin) |
| Low-risk post-TORS | 50 Gy RT alone (ECOG-ACRIN E3311 — acceptable) |
| RT dose reduction (non-surgical) | 60 Gy + weekly cisplatin investigational; NOT yet standard |
| Cetuximab substitution | CONTRAINDICATED — inferior OS (De-ESCALaTE, RTOG 1016) |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> De-escalation in HPV+ OPC aims to reduce late toxicity (dysphagia, xerostomia, ototoxicity) while maintaining excellent oncological outcomes; two landmark phase III trials (De-ESCALaTE HPV and RTOG 1016, both Lancet 2019) definitively showed that cetuximab-RT is INFERIOR to cisplatin-RT (OS 77.9% vs 84.6% at 5 years in RTOG 1016), abandoning cetuximab as a de-escalation agent. The most evidence-supported current de-escalation strategy is TORS-based pathological risk stratification (ECOG-ACRIN E3311): low-risk post-TORS patients receive 50 Gy RT without cisplatin, avoiding cisplatin in ~50–60% of HPV+ OPC patients while maintaining 2-year PFS of 96%.

---

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. De-ESCALaTE HPV (Lancet 2019): cetuximab-RT had WORSE OS than cisplatin-RT (HR for death = 5.0) — cetuximab is not a de-escalation agent in HPV+ OPC.
> 2. RTOG 1016 (Lancet 2019): same conclusion — 5-year OS 77.9% cetuximab vs 84.6% cisplatin; non-inferiority NOT demonstrated; cisplatin is the standard.
> 3. ECOG-ACRIN E3311 (JCO 2020): TORS-based pathological risk stratification → low-risk patients receive 50 Gy RT (no cisplatin) with 2-year PFS of 96%.
> 4. ORATOR trial (Lancet 2020): TORS and RT have similar OS and locoregional control; RT better swallowing at 1 year; TORS better physical function QoL at 2 years — both are valid approaches.
> 5. PATHOS and ORATOR2 are the key ongoing phase III trials that will definitively establish TORS-based de-escalation as standard of care.
> 6. NRG HN002 (JCO 2021): 60 Gy + weekly cisplatin achieved 90.5% 2-year PFS in selected low-risk HPV+ OPC — a promising but not yet standard de-escalated chemoradiation regimen.
> 7. Induction chemotherapy bioselection (OPTIMA): complete IC responders may receive de-escalated RT (54–60 Gy) — concept validated but requires further prospective evidence.
> 8. NRG HN005 tests nivolumab + RT vs cisplatin + RT — if successful, immunotherapy-based de-escalation will be the next paradigm shift in HPV+ OPC management.
