## The Critical Negative: ACC and HPV

> **Adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC) of the sinonasal tract is NOT HPV-associated.**

This is a high-yield negative for the MCh examination. The question specifically tests whether candidates know the actual driver of ACC carcinogenesis versus erroneously attributing it to HPV.

### Adenoid Cystic Carcinoma — Molecular Driver
- **MYB-NFIB fusion gene**: t(6;9)(q22–24;p23–24) chromosomal translocation creates a MYB-NFIB fusion transcript
- MYB is a transcription factor; NFIB (nuclear factor I B) brings enhancer elements → constitutive MYB activation → uncontrolled transcription
- Present in **~50–86% of ACC** across all head and neck sites (salivary glands, sinonasal, lacrimal, trachea)
- **NOTCH1 mutations**: second major driver (~14% of ACC); associated with poor prognosis and solid histological pattern
- **MYBL1-NFIB fusion**: alternative fusion in ACC without MYB-NFIB (~8%)
- HPV DNA: **absent or rare incidental finding** in ACC; no evidence of causal role; HPV does not interact with MYB-NFIB signalling
- p16 IHC: may show focal staining in ACC (due to proliferative activity) — not indicative of HPV

### Clinical Implications
- ACC management is NOT affected by HPV testing
- Prognosis determinants in sinonasal ACC: histological grade (cribriform vs tubular vs solid), perineural invasion (nearly universal), distant metastasis (lung — characteristic late metastasis), surgical margin status
- See [[Answers/General/Surgical Margins in Head and Neck Cancer|Surgical Margins in HNC]] for margin principles in ACC

## HPV in Sinonasal Malignancies — Actual Associations

### 1. Sinonasal Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| HPV prevalence in sinonasal SCC | **20–25%** (vs 60–80% in OPC) |
| Predominant types | HPV-16 (majority); HPV-18; HPV-6 and 11 (low-risk) |
| Detection method | HPV DNA ISH or E6/E7 mRNA (p16 IHC NOT reliable as surrogate in sinonasal SCC) |
| Prognosis | HPV+ sinonasal SCC appears to have better prognosis — similar mechanism to OPC |
| Staging | No separate AJCC staging for HPV+ sinonasal SCC (unlike OPC) |
| Significance | HPV role in sinonasal SCC less clearly defined; some represent spread from oropharyngeal primary |

> In Alos et al. (Cancer 2009), HPV-positive sinonasal SCC had significantly better 5-year OS (86% vs 41% HPV-negative). This finding may eventually lead to modified treatment protocols analogous to OPC.

### 2. Sinonasal Inverted Papilloma (Schneiderian Papilloma)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| HPV types detected | **HPV-6 and HPV-11** (low-risk; 10–50% of inverted papillomas) |
| Role of HPV | HPV detected but causal role in IP is **NOT proven** |
| Association with malignant transformation | **Debated** — HPV-16/18 detected more frequently in SCC arising in IP (5–15% of IP undergo malignant transformation); causal link not established |
| Clinical relevance | HPV status does not yet guide IP management |

### 3. Sinonasal Undifferentiated Carcinoma (SNUC)

- **HPV detected in minority** (<20%) of SNUC
- No clear causal role established
- Other drivers: EBV (rare), IDH2 mutations, SMARCB1/INI1 loss (related to SMARCB1-deficient carcinoma)
- WHO 2022 recognises SMARCB1-deficient sinonasal carcinoma as separate entity

### 4. WHO 2022 — New HPV-Related Sinonasal Entities

The 2022 WHO Classification of Head and Neck Tumours introduced several new entities relevant to HPV in the sinonasal tract:

#### HPV-Related Multiphenotypic Sinonasal Carcinoma (HMSC)
- Previously called "HPV-related carcinoma with adenoid cystic-like features"
- **HPV-positive** (predominantly HPV-33, occasionally HPV-16)
- Morphologically resembles ACC (cribriform pattern) but lacks MYB-NFIB fusion
- **Transcriptionally active HPV** (E6/E7 mRNA positive)
- Distinct from ACC: **MYB-NFIB negative**, p16+, HPV+
- Prognosis: intermediate; does not follow ACC natural history (less perineural spread, different metastatic pattern)
- High-yield distinction: if a sinonasal tumour morphologically resembles ACC but is p16+ and HPV+, suspect HMSC NOT ACC

#### DEK-AFF2 Carcinoma
- HPV-related; fused DEK-AFF2 gene by chromosomal translocation t(6;12)
- Newly recognised WHO 2022 entity
- HPV-16 or HPV-33; transcriptionally active
- Sinonasal location; keratinising or non-keratinising morphology

## p16 IHC in Sinonasal Tumours — Critical Limitation

> **p16 IHC is NOT a reliable HPV surrogate in sinonasal tumours.**

Reasons for high false-positive rate in sinonasal tissue:
- PIK3CA activation (common in sinonasal malignancies) upregulates p16 via non-HPV mechanisms
- CDKN2A (p16) amplification or promoter demethylation
- Intrinsic p16 overexpression in certain histological types

### Recommended Testing Algorithm for Sinonasal SCC
1. **Do NOT use p16 IHC alone** to determine HPV status
2. Use **HPV DNA ISH** (broadspectrum HR-HPV probe) as primary test
3. Confirm with **E6/E7 mRNA** (RNAscope ISH) if HPV+ to prove oncogenic activity
4. If HMSC suspected (ACC-like morphology + p16+): test for MYB-NFIB fusion (negative in HMSC, positive in ACC) + confirm HPV with ISH

## Summary Table — HPV in Sinonasal Pathology

| Tumour Type | HPV Association | Key Driver | p16 as Surrogate? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adenoid Cystic Carcinoma** | **NO** | MYB-NFIB fusion; NOTCH1 | No |
| Sinonasal SCC | Yes (20–25%) | HPV-16/18 | No — use ISH |
| Inverted Papilloma | Possible (LR types 6/11) | HPV-6/11 (debated) | No |
| SNUC | Minority | IDH2, SMARCB1/INI1 | No |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Sinonasal adenoid cystic carcinoma is NOT HPV-associated — it is driven by the MYB-NFIB fusion gene (t(6;9)) and NOTCH1 mutations; this is a critical negative that frequently appears in examinations. HPV is detected in ~20–25% of sinonasal SCC (predominantly HPV-16), with possible better prognosis, and in a proportion of inverted papillomas (LR types 6/11); the 2022 WHO classification recognises new HPV-driven entities including HPV-related multiphenotypic sinonasal carcinoma (HMSC) and DEK-AFF2 carcinoma, which must be distinguished from ACC by MYB-NFIB fusion testing.

---

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. ACC of ANY head and neck site (sinonasal, parotid, submandibular, lacrimal) is driven by MYB-NFIB fusion — HPV has NO role in ACC carcinogenesis.
> 2. HPV-related multiphenotypic sinonasal carcinoma (HMSC) mimics ACC morphologically but is MYB-NFIB NEGATIVE and HPV-33/16 POSITIVE — the WHO 2022 distinction is critical.
> 3. p16 IHC is NOT a valid HPV surrogate in sinonasal tumours — always confirm with HPV DNA ISH or E6/E7 mRNA.
> 4. HPV prevalence in sinonasal SCC (~20–25%) is much lower than in OPC (~70%); significance debated but HPV+ sinonasal SCC may have better prognosis.
> 5. Sinonasal inverted papilloma harbours LR-HPV (types 6/11) in 10–50% of cases; causal role unproven; HPV-16/18 detected in malignant transformation in IP.
> 6. SNUC is not HPV-driven; IDH2 mutations and SMARCB1/INI1 loss are the relevant molecular drivers; WHO 2022 recognises SMARCB1-deficient sinonasal carcinoma as a separate entity.
> 7. MYB-NFIB fusion testing (FISH or RT-PCR) is the definitive molecular marker for ACC — positive in ~50–86% of ACC, absent in HMSC.
> 8. In the exam context: if asked about "HPV and sinonasal ACC" — state clearly and early that the answer is NO, then demonstrate knowledge of the actual ACC driver (MYB-NFIB) and pivot to what HPV IS associated with in the sinonasal tract.
