> [!question] Questions Covered
> - **Chemo Q27:** Evidence for pembrolizumab in advanced HNSCC (TMH 2017)

## Source
**Shah JP. Head and Neck Surgery and Oncology, 5th Ed. Elsevier, 2019**; De Vita VT et al. Cancer: Principles and Practice of Oncology. 11th ed. Wolters Kluwer, 2019; Colevas AD et al. NCCN Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology — Head and Neck Cancers v2.2024; Harrison LB et al. 4th ed. 2014.

> [!cite] Landmark Articles
>
> Burtness et al. (2019) published the KEYNOTE-048 phase III trial in The Lancet, which fundamentally redefined first-line treatment for recurrent or metastatic HNSCC. The trial enrolled 882 patients with untreated R/M HNSCC in three arms: pembrolizumab monotherapy versus EXTREME (cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU) and pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU versus EXTREME. Pembrolizumab monotherapy demonstrated superior overall survival in the PD-L1 CPS ≥1 population (median OS 12.3 vs 10.3 months; HR 0.78; p = 0.0086) and the CPS ≥20 population (14.9 vs 10.7 months; HR 0.61; p = 0.0004), with markedly fewer grade 3–4 adverse events (17% vs 69%). Pembrolizumab + chemotherapy improved OS in all-comers (HR 0.73; p = 0.0034). These results established pembrolizumab-based therapy as the new standard of care in first-line R/M HNSCC, replacing the decade-old EXTREME regimen.
>
> Cohen et al. (2019) reported the KEYNOTE-040 phase III trial in The Lancet, randomising 495 patients with platinum-treated R/M HNSCC to pembrolizumab versus investigator's choice (methotrexate, docetaxel, or cetuximab). Pembrolizumab significantly improved OS (median 8.4 vs 6.9 months; HR 0.80; p = 0.016), with grade 3–4 AEs 13% versus 36%. This established pembrolizumab as a second-line option, with substantially greater benefit in high CPS subgroups (CPS ≥20: median OS 11.6 vs 7.9 months; HR 0.53).

## Pembrolizumab in HNSCC

### Drug Profile

**Pembrolizumab (Keytruda; Merck/MSD)**

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Humanised IgG4κ monoclonal antibody |
| Target | PD-1 (Programmed Death-1; CD279) |
| Mechanism | Blocks PD-1 / PD-L1 interaction; restores cytotoxic T-cell function |
| Route | Intravenous infusion over 30 minutes |
| Standard dosing | 200 mg IV every 3 weeks OR 400 mg IV every 6 weeks |
| Dosing basis | Fixed-dose (weight-independent) |
| FDA approval in HNSCC (1st line) | June 2019 — pembrolizumab monotherapy (CPS ≥1) + pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU (all-comers) |
| FDA approval in HNSCC (2nd line) | August 2019 — platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC |

### Mechanism of Action

Pembrolizumab is a **humanised IgG4** antibody targeting PD-1. The IgG4 isotype (with S228P stabilising mutation) prevents Fc-mediated effector functions (ADCC, CDC) that could deplete PD-1+ T cells.

**PD-1/PD-L1 pathway in HNSCC:**
- PD-L1 is overexpressed on HNSCC tumour cells, tumour-associated macrophages, and dendritic cells
- PD-L1 upregulation is driven by IFN-γ released by infiltrating T cells ("adaptive immune resistance")
- PD-1 engagement on T cells → SHP-2 phosphatase activation → ZAP-70 dephosphorylation → T-cell exhaustion and anergy
- Pembrolizumab blocks this interaction → reinvigorated CD8+ cytotoxic T lymphocytes kill PD-L1+ tumour cells

**CPS (Combined Positive Score):**
The key biomarker for pembrolizumab in HNSCC:
$$\text{CPS} = \frac{\text{PD-L1+ tumour cells + PD-L1+ lymphocytes + PD-L1+ macrophages}}{\text{total viable tumour cells}} \times 100$$

CPS cutoffs:
- **CPS ≥1** → pembrolizumab monotherapy 1st-line approved (superior OS vs EXTREME)
- **CPS ≥20** → greatest OS benefit from monotherapy (HR 0.61; median OS 14.9 months)
- **CPS 0** → pembrolizumab monotherapy not approved in 1st line; combination with chemotherapy still applicable

### KEYNOTE-048 — The Pivotal 1st-Line Trial

**Study design:** Phase III, open-label, RCT; 3 arms

- **Arm A:** Pembrolizumab 200 mg q3w (up to 24 months)
- **Arm B:** Pembrolizumab 200 mg q3w + carboplatin/cisplatin + 5-FU (6 cycles → pembrolizumab maintenance)
- **Arm C (control):** EXTREME — cetuximab 400 mg/m² loading then 250 mg/m² weekly + carboplatin/cisplatin + 5-FU (6 cycles → cetuximab maintenance)

**Population:** 882 patients with untreated R/M HNSCC (oral cavity, oropharynx, hypopharynx, larynx); ECOG PS 0–1; no prior systemic therapy for R/M disease

#### Pembrolizumab Monotherapy vs EXTREME

| Endpoint | CPS ≥1 | CPS ≥20 | All-comers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Median OS — Pembro | 12.3 months | 14.9 months | 11.5 months |
| Median OS — EXTREME | 10.3 months | 10.7 months | 10.7 months |
| Hazard Ratio | **0.78** | **0.61** | 0.83 |
| p-value | 0.0086 | 0.0004 | 0.0281 |
| Grade 3–4 AEs | **17%** | **17%** | — |
| Grade 3–4 AEs (EXTREME) | **69%** | **69%** | — |

→ **Monotherapy approved for CPS ≥1** (not all-comers — narrowly missed statistical threshold in all-comers analysis)

#### Pembrolizumab + Chemotherapy vs EXTREME

| Endpoint | All-comers | CPS ≥1 |
|---|---|---|
| Median OS — Pembro+chemo | 13.0 months | 13.6 months |
| Median OS — EXTREME | 10.7 months | 10.3 months |
| Hazard Ratio | **0.73** | **0.65** |
| p-value | 0.0034 | 0.0004 |
| Grade 3–4 AEs | 85% | — |

→ **Combination approved regardless of PD-L1 CPS** (all-comers)

#### Clinical Decision-Making from KEYNOTE-048

| PD-L1 CPS | Preferred Regimen |
|---|---|
| CPS ≥20 | Pembrolizumab monotherapy (lowest toxicity; best OS) |
| CPS ≥1 to <20 | Pembrolizumab monotherapy or pembrolizumab + chemo |
| CPS 0 | Pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU (monotherapy not approved) |

### KEYNOTE-040 — The 2nd-Line Trial

**Study design:** Phase III, 495 patients with prior platinum-treated R/M HNSCC

| Endpoint | Pembrolizumab | Investigator's Choice |
|---|---|---|
| Median OS | 8.4 months | 6.9 months |
| HR | **0.80** (95% CI 0.65–0.98) | — |
| p-value | 0.016 | — |
| CPS ≥20 median OS | 11.6 months | 7.9 months (HR 0.53) |
| Grade 3–4 TRAEs | 13% | 36% |

→ Pembrolizumab approved for 2nd-line R/M HNSCC (any PD-L1 expression, but greater benefit in higher CPS)

### Pembrolizumab in the Curative Setting — KEYNOTE-412

**KEYNOTE-412 (Phase III):** Pembrolizumab + concurrent CRT vs CRT alone in locally advanced HNSCC
- Primary endpoint: **event-free survival (EFS)** — **not met** at pre-specified interim analysis (2023)
- HR for EFS: 0.83 (95% CI 0.68–1.03); p = 0.0429 (required p = 0.0092 for significance)
- **Pembrolizumab is NOT standard of care in the curative (definitive CRT) setting** as of 2024

**KEYNOTE-689 (ongoing):** Perioperative pembrolizumab in resectable HNSCC (neoadjuvant + adjuvant); results awaited

### Pembrolizumab vs Nivolumab — Key Differences

| Feature | Pembrolizumab | Nivolumab |
|---|---|---|
| 1st-line approval | ✅ CPS ≥1 monotherapy; all-comers + chemo | ❌ (CheckMate 651 negative) |
| 2nd-line approval | ✅ (KEYNOTE-040) | ✅ (CheckMate 141) |
| Biomarker requirement (1st line mono) | CPS ≥1 mandatory | Not required |
| Key biomarker | PD-L1 **CPS** | PD-L1 TPS (less discriminatory) |
| Dosing | 200 mg q3w / 400 mg q6w | 240 mg q2w / 480 mg q4w |
| Approved backbone (1st line combo) | Pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU | N/A |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Pembrolizumab (Keytruda) is a humanised IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody now established as the standard first-line therapy for recurrent or metastatic HNSCC. Based on KEYNOTE-048: pembrolizumab monotherapy is superior to EXTREME (cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU) in CPS ≥1 patients (OS 12.3 vs 10.3 months; HR 0.78); pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU is superior to EXTREME in all-comers (HR 0.73). CPS ≥20 confers the greatest OS benefit (14.9 months; HR 0.61) and allows monotherapy to avoid chemotherapy toxicity entirely. In 2nd-line platinum-refractory disease, pembrolizumab improved OS over cytotoxic therapy in KEYNOTE-040 (8.4 vs 6.9 months; HR 0.80). Integration into curative CRT (KEYNOTE-412) did not meet its primary EFS endpoint. PD-L1 CPS (Combined Positive Score) — counting tumour cells plus immune cells — is the key biomarker that distinguishes pembrolizumab's approval from older PD-L1 TPS scoring.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. CPS ≥1: pembrolizumab monotherapy 1st-line is **superior** to EXTREME — this replaced the EXTREME regimen in most patients worldwide.
> 2. CPS ≥20: greatest OS benefit (14.9 months); monotherapy preferred to spare chemotherapy toxicity.
> 3. CPS 0: pembrolizumab monotherapy is NOT approved 1st-line — use pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU.
> 4. CPS (counts immune cells) is more predictive than TPS (counts only tumour cells) in HNSCC — this is why CPS was validated as the regulatory biomarker.
> 5. Grade 3–4 AEs: pembrolizumab monotherapy 17% vs EXTREME 69% — a 4-fold reduction in severe toxicity.
> 6. KEYNOTE-048 proved pembrolizumab + chemotherapy is better than **cetuximab** + same chemotherapy (EXTREME) — confirming anti-PD-1 superiority over anti-EGFR as the immunological add-on.
> 7. KEYNOTE-412 was negative — pembrolizumab added to definitive CRT did NOT improve EFS in LA-HNSCC; do NOT use pembrolizumab routinely in the curative setting.
> 8. KEYNOTE-040 (2nd-line): the greater benefit in CPS ≥20 (HR 0.53) vs overall population (HR 0.80) demonstrates the dose-response relationship between PD-L1 CPS and immunotherapy benefit.
> 9. Pembrolizumab's IgG4 isotype (vs IgG1) is crucial — avoids Fc-receptor-mediated depletion of activated PD-1+ T cells that would paradoxically worsen antitumour immunity.
> 10. Duration of therapy: pembrolizumab is given for up to **24 months** in responding patients, then stopped — unlike cytotoxic chemotherapy which is continued to progression.
