## Source
**Shah JP. Head and Neck Surgery and Oncology, 5th Ed. Elsevier, 2019**; De Vita VT et al. Cancer: Principles and Practice of Oncology. 11th ed. Wolters Kluwer, 2019; Colevas AD et al. NCCN Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology — Head and Neck Cancers v2.2024; Harrison LB et al. 4th ed. 2014.

> [!cite] Landmark Articles
>
> Ferris et al. (2016) published the CheckMate 141 phase III randomised controlled trial in the New England Journal of Medicine, establishing nivolumab as the first immune checkpoint inhibitor approved for head and neck cancer. The trial randomised 361 patients with platinum-refractory recurrent or metastatic HNSCC to nivolumab 3 mg/kg IV q2w versus investigator's choice (methotrexate, docetaxel, or cetuximab). The primary endpoint of overall survival was significantly improved with nivolumab (median OS 7.5 vs 5.1 months; HR 0.70; 95% CI 0.51–0.96; p = 0.01), with 1-year OS rates of 36% versus 17%. Critically, grade 3–4 treatment-related adverse events were dramatically lower with nivolumab (13% vs 35%), establishing a superior therapeutic index over cytotoxic therapy. The survival benefit was consistent across subgroups including HPV status, smoking history, and primary site.
>
> Ferris et al. (2018) published the two-year follow-up of CheckMate 141, demonstrating durable benefit: the 2-year OS rate with nivolumab was 16.9% versus 6.0% with investigator's choice, confirming long-term survival benefit in a subset of patients — a hallmark of checkpoint inhibitor therapy distinct from cytotoxic agents. These patients represent the "tail of the curve," which has become the primary goal of immunotherapy.

## Nivolumab in HNSCC

### Drug Profile

**Nivolumab (Opdivo; Bristol-Myers Squibb)**

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Fully human IgG4 monoclonal antibody |
| Target | PD-1 (Programmed Death-1; CD279) |
| Mechanism | Blocks PD-1 binding to PD-L1 (CD274) and PD-L2 (CD273); restores T-cell activation |
| Route | Intravenous infusion over 30 minutes |
| Standard dosing | 240 mg IV every 2 weeks OR 480 mg IV every 4 weeks |
| Dosing basis | Fixed-dose (weight-independent) |
| Storage | Refrigerated; single-use vial |
| FDA approval in HNSCC | November 2016 — platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC (2nd line) |

### Mechanism of Action

PD-1 is an inhibitory receptor expressed on activated T cells, NK cells, and B cells. Under normal physiological conditions, PD-1 engagement by its ligands (PD-L1 and PD-L2) limits T-cell activity, preventing autoimmunity — the **peripheral tolerance** mechanism.

In HNSCC, PD-L1 is expressed on tumour cells and tumour-associated macrophages in 50–80% of cases. Binding of tumour-PD-L1 to T-cell PD-1:
1. Activates **SHP-2** (tyrosine phosphatase) → dephosphorylates ZAP-70 and CD3ζ → inhibits TCR downstream signalling
2. Suppresses PI3K/AKT survival pathway in T cells
3. Drives T-cell **exhaustion** — upregulation of TIM-3 and LAG-3 co-inhibitory receptors
4. Reduces T-cell cytokine production (IFN-γ, IL-2, TNF-α) and cytotoxic granule release

**Nivolumab** blocks PD-1 with high affinity (K_D ~3 nM), preventing PD-L1/PD-L2 binding and **releasing the peripheral T-cell brake** → reinvigorated CD8+ cytotoxic T lymphocytes kill tumour cells.

**IgG4 backbone** is chosen deliberately:
- IgG4 does not activate complement (CDC) or ADCC efficiently → reduces risk of T-cell depletion
- Stabilised with S228P hinge mutation to prevent half-molecule exchange

### CheckMate 141 — Key Trial Data

**Study design:** Phase III, open-label, randomised 2:1 (nivolumab:investigator's choice)

**Population:** Platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC (oral cavity, oropharynx, hypopharynx, larynx); prior platinum within 6 months of last dose

| Parameter | Nivolumab (n=240) | Investigator's Choice (n=121) |
|---|---|---|
| Median OS | **7.5 months** | 5.1 months |
| HR for death | **0.70** (95% CI 0.51–0.96) | — |
| p-value | **0.01** | — |
| 1-year OS | **36%** | 17% |
| 2-year OS | **17%** | 6% |
| ORR | 13.3% | 5.8% |
| Median PFS | 2.0 months | 2.3 months |
| Grade 3–4 TRAEs | **13%** | 35% |
| Treatment discontinuation due to AEs | 3% | 10% |

**Subgroup analysis:**
- **HPV/p16+ HNSCC:** Greater OS benefit (HR 0.55 in p16+ oropharynx)
- **HPV/p16– HNSCC:** Still significant benefit (HR 0.73)
- **PD-L1 expression:** Benefit regardless of PD-L1 status, though higher expressors showed greater effect
- **Smoking history:** Consistent benefit in smokers and never-smokers
- **Primary site:** Oral cavity, oropharynx, hypopharynx, larynx — all showed benefit

### Nivolumab: Predictive Biomarkers

| Biomarker | Significance in CheckMate 141 |
|---|---|
| PD-L1 TPS ≥1% | Improved OS vs TPS <1%, but benefit present regardless of expression |
| HPV/p16+ | HR 0.55 (oropharynx, p16+) — greater OS benefit |
| TMB (tumour mutational burden) | Higher TMB → improved response; FDA pan-tumour approval for TMB-H ≥10 mut/Mb (pembrolizumab, not nivolumab specifically) |
| CD8+ TIL density | High TIL density correlates with response; investigational |

**Key point:** Unlike pembrolizumab (which requires PD-L1 CPS ≥1 for 1st-line monotherapy), **nivolumab's approval is not restricted by PD-L1 expression** — benefit is seen regardless.

### Immune-Related Adverse Events (irAEs) with Nivolumab

Grade 3–4 irAEs occur in ~13% of patients — significantly lower than conventional cytotoxic therapy.

| irAE | Frequency | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Fatigue | 14% (any grade) | Supportive; steroids if severe |
| Rash / pruritus | 9% | Topical steroids; hold for Grade 3 |
| Hypothyroidism | 10–15% | Levothyroxine replacement; continue ICI |
| Hyperthyroidism | 5% | Beta-blockers; usually self-limiting |
| Diarrhoea / colitis | 8% | Grade ≥2: hold ICI; prednisone 1–2 mg/kg |
| Pneumonitis | 2–4% | Hold ICI; high-dose IV steroids; pulmonary input |
| Hepatitis | 2–3% | Hold ICI; steroids; mycophenolate if refractory |
| Myocarditis | <0.5% | **Permanently discontinue**; urgent cardiology |
| Nephritis | 1% | Hold ICI; prednisone |
| Hypophysitis | 1% | Steroids; pituitary axis replacement |

**irAE management principles:**
- Grade 1: continue with close monitoring
- Grade 2: hold ICI; oral prednisone 0.5–1 mg/kg; resume when ≤ Grade 1
- Grade 3: hold ICI; IV methylprednisolone 1–2 mg/kg; permanent discontinuation considered
- Grade 4: **permanently discontinue nivolumab**; high-dose IV steroids; organ-specific specialist input

### Nivolumab vs Pembrolizumab in R/M HNSCC

| Feature | Nivolumab | Pembrolizumab |
|---|---|---|
| Approved line (2nd line) | ✅ After platinum failure | ✅ After platinum failure |
| Approved 1st line | ❌ (CheckMate 651 negative) | ✅ (KEYNOTE-048) |
| PD-L1 restriction | None (any expression) | CPS ≥1 for monotherapy 1st line |
| Key trial | CheckMate 141 | KEYNOTE-048 / KEYNOTE-040 |
| Dosing | 240 mg q2w or 480 mg q4w | 200 mg q3w or 400 mg q6w |
| Backbone for 1st line | Not standard | Pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU |

**CheckMate 651** (Phase III, 1st-line R/M HNSCC): Nivolumab + ipilimumab vs EXTREME (cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU). Primary endpoint (OS in CPS ≥20 population) was **not met** — dual checkpoint blockade did not improve 1st-line survival over EXTREME, unlike the pembrolizumab story. Therefore, **nivolumab remains a 2nd-line agent** in current guidelines.

### Current Place in HNSCC Treatment Algorithm

```
1st-line R/M HNSCC
  ├─ CPS ≥1: Pembrolizumab monotherapy (KEYNOTE-048)
  └─ All-comers: Pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU (KEYNOTE-048)

2nd-line / Platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC
  ├─ Nivolumab ← CheckMate 141 ✅
  └─ Pembrolizumab ← KEYNOTE-040 ✅
```

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Nivolumab (Opdivo) is a fully human IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody that restores cytotoxic T-lymphocyte activity by blocking the PD-1 / PD-L1 peripheral tolerance checkpoint. In HNSCC, the pivotal CheckMate 141 phase III trial demonstrated a significant OS benefit over investigator's choice in platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC (7.5 vs 5.1 months; HR 0.70; p = 0.01) with a markedly superior toxicity profile (grade 3–4 AEs 13% vs 35%). The 2-year OS "tail of the curve" (17% vs 6%) illustrates the durable benefit that distinguishes immunotherapy. Nivolumab is approved for 2nd-line R/M HNSCC without PD-L1 restriction. Unlike pembrolizumab, it did not improve 1st-line OS in CheckMate 651. IgG4 isotype was chosen to avoid T-cell depletion via complement/ADCC.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. Nivolumab is an **IgG4** antibody (not IgG1) — IgG4 avoids complement-mediated depletion of PD-1+ T cells that would paradoxically worsen immunosuppression.
> 2. CheckMate 141 primary endpoint: OS — significant (HR 0.70; p = 0.01); PFS was NOT significantly improved (both ~2 months) — a pattern typical of checkpoint inhibitors where tumour regression is delayed.
> 3. The "tail of the curve" (2-year OS 17% vs 6%) is the clinically meaningful endpoint for immunotherapy — a small subset achieves durable long-term responses not seen with cytotoxic chemotherapy.
> 4. Nivolumab benefit was seen regardless of PD-L1 expression — unlike pembrolizumab 1st-line monotherapy which requires CPS ≥1.
> 5. HPV/p16+ oropharyngeal HNSCC had greater benefit (HR 0.55) — likely due to higher immune infiltration and PD-L1 expression in HPV+ tumours.
> 6. Grade 3–4 TRAE rate 13% with nivolumab vs 35% with cytotoxic therapy — the tolerability advantage is as important as the survival benefit.
> 7. CheckMate 651 (nivolumab + ipilimumab 1st line) was negative for its primary endpoint (OS in CPS ≥20) — nivolumab does NOT have a 1st-line approval in HNSCC.
> 8. Fixed-dose nivolumab (240 mg or 480 mg) replaced weight-based dosing (3 mg/kg) following pharmacokinetic studies showing equivalent exposure across body weights.
> 9. Myocarditis (<0.5%) is rare but potentially fatal — requires permanent discontinuation and urgent cardiac management.
> 10. The IFN-γ paradox: TIL-released IFN-γ upregulates PD-L1 on tumour cells (adaptive immune resistance); nivolumab breaks this cycle by blocking PD-1 on the TIL.
