> [!cite] Landmark Articles
>
> The clinical transformation of HNSCC management by immunotherapy was driven by two pivotal trials. Ferris et al. (2016) established nivolumab as the first checkpoint inhibitor approved in HNSCC, demonstrating a survival benefit in platinum-refractory R/M disease (CheckMate 141: OS 7.5 vs 5.1 months; HR 0.70). Burtness et al. (2019) subsequently proved that pembrolizumab was superior to the established EXTREME regimen in first-line R/M HNSCC (KEYNOTE-048), with the greatest benefit in CPS ≥20 tumours (OS 14.9 vs 10.7 months; HR 0.61) and markedly reduced toxicity (grade 3–4 AEs 17% vs 69%), fundamentally altering first-line treatment.
>
> Cohen et al. (2019) extended pembrolizumab approval to second-line platinum-refractory disease through KEYNOTE-040, confirming durable OS improvement with a superior safety profile. These three trials collectively replaced the era of cytotoxic chemotherapy as the sole systemic option in R/M HNSCC with immunotherapy-based strategies guided by PD-L1 CPS biomarker testing.

## Immunotherapy in Head and Neck Cancer

### Why Is HNSCC Amenable to Immunotherapy?

HNSCC has several features that make it a promising immunotherapy target:

1. **High PD-L1 expression:** 50–80% of HNSCC express PD-L1 on tumour cells and/or infiltrating immune cells
2. **Tumour-infiltrating lymphocytes (TILs):** HNSCC — especially HPV+ oropharyngeal cancer — has an "inflamed" (hot) tumour microenvironment with high CD8+ TIL density
3. **Tumour mutational burden (TMB):** Tobacco-related HNSCC has high TMB (from tobacco carcinogen-induced DNA mutations) → more neoantigens → greater immunogenicity
4. **Viral antigens:** HPV+ HNSCC expresses viral E6/E7 oncoproteins → tumour-specific antigens → strong potential for T-cell recognition
5. **IFN-γ-driven PD-L1 upregulation:** Active immune infiltration drives PD-L1 — adaptive immune resistance that is reversed by anti-PD-1 therapy

### Immunological Hallmarks of HNSCC Tumour Microenvironment

The HNSCC tumour microenvironment (TME) contains:

| Cell Type | Role in Immune Evasion |
|---|---|
| Regulatory T cells (Tregs, CD4+CD25+FOXP3+) | Suppress CD8+ cytotoxic T cells; produce IL-10 and TGF-β |
| Myeloid-derived suppressor cells (MDSCs) | Inhibit T-cell proliferation via ROS, NO, arginase-1; impair MHC class I expression |
| M2 tumour-associated macrophages (TAMs) | PD-L1+; secrete IL-10, TGF-β, VEGF; promote angiogenesis and immunosuppression |
| Exhausted CD8+ TILs (PD-1+TIM-3+LAG-3+) | Dysfunctional; reduced IFN-γ, granzyme B; fail to kill tumour cells |
| Tumour cells | Downregulate MHC class I; express PD-L1, CD47 ("don't eat me") |

### Current Role of Immunotherapy in HNSCC

#### A. Recurrent/Metastatic HNSCC (R/M HNSCC)

**First-line (untreated R/M HNSCC):**

| PD-L1 CPS | Recommended Regimen | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| CPS ≥20 | **Pembrolizumab monotherapy** | KEYNOTE-048: OS 14.9 vs 10.7 months; HR 0.61 |
| CPS ≥1 to <20 | **Pembrolizumab monotherapy** OR **pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU** | KEYNOTE-048: mono HR 0.78; combo HR 0.65 |
| CPS 0 | **Pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU** | KEYNOTE-048: combo HR 0.73 (all-comers) |

**Second-line / platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC:**
- **Nivolumab** (CheckMate 141): OS 7.5 vs 5.1 months; HR 0.70 — **approved, no PD-L1 restriction**
- **Pembrolizumab** (KEYNOTE-040): OS 8.4 vs 6.9 months; HR 0.80 — **approved**
- **Cetuximab or methotrexate or docetaxel** remain alternatives when ICIs are unavailable or contraindicated

**The "tail of the curve" concept:**
A defining feature of checkpoint inhibitor therapy is **durable long-term responses** in a minority of patients — the OS curves separate and a "tail" of sustained survival appears that does not occur with cytotoxic therapy. In CheckMate 141, 2-year OS was 17% with nivolumab vs 6% with cytotoxic therapy.

#### B. Locally Advanced HNSCC (Curative Intent) — Investigational

Integrating ICIs with definitive CRT in LA-HNSCC has been extensively studied but has **not yet been successful**:

| Trial | Agent | Result |
|---|---|---|
| KEYNOTE-412 | Pembrolizumab + CRT vs CRT | Primary endpoint (EFS) **not met** (HR 0.83; p = 0.0429 vs required 0.0092) |
| JAVELIN H&N 100 | Avelumab + CRT vs CRT | **Negative** — no EFS benefit |
| IMvoke010 | Atezolizumab maintenance post-CRT | **Negative** — no DFS benefit |

**KEYNOTE-689** (perioperative pembrolizumab in resectable HNSCC) — ongoing; results awaited

→ As of 2024, **immunotherapy is NOT standard of care in the definitive/curative setting for LA-HNSCC**.

#### C. Evidence for Immunotherapy in Primary (Non-Metastatic) HNSCC

The use of immunotherapy in patients with primary (non-metastatic) HNSCC remains investigational:
- **Neoadjuvant ICI:** Single-arm studies show pathological complete response (pCR) rates of 10–15% with pembrolizumab or nivolumab before surgery — exceeds cytotoxic neoadjuvant chemotherapy pCR rates; however, no randomised OS data yet
- **Adjuvant ICI post-CRT:** IMvoke010 (atezolizumab) and KEYNOTE-689 (pembrolizumab) trials ongoing
- **ICI in HPV+ OPC de-escalation trials:** KEYNOTE-B10 exploring pembrolizumab as a radiation de-escalation partner; results awaited

### Predictive Biomarkers — Who Benefits from Immunotherapy?

| Biomarker | Clinical Significance |
|---|---|
| **PD-L1 CPS ≥1** | Pembrolizumab 1st-line monotherapy approval (KEYNOTE-048) |
| **PD-L1 CPS ≥20** | Greatest OS benefit from pembrolizumab monotherapy (HR 0.61; median OS 14.9 months) |
| **HPV/p16 positivity** | Higher TIL density; greater nivolumab benefit in p16+ OPC (HR 0.55, CheckMate 141) |
| **TMB-H (≥10 mut/Mb)** | FDA pan-tumour pembrolizumab approval (KEYNOTE-158); tobacco-related HNSCC tends to have higher TMB |
| **MSI-H / dMMR** | FDA pan-tumour pembrolizumab; rare in HNSCC but worth testing in unusual histologies |
| **Inflamed phenotype (IFN-γ signature)** | High CD8+ TIL + IFN-γ gene expression = "hot" tumour = best ICI response |
| **Excluded or desert phenotype** | Low/absent TILs = poor ICI response; barriers include fibrosis (TGF-β), MDSCs |

**The three tumour immune phenotypes:**
1. **Inflamed ("hot"):** High TIL density in tumour parenchyma; PD-L1+ → best ICI responders
2. **Excluded:** TILs present at tumour margins but excluded from parenchyma (TGF-β barrier) → intermediate response
3. **Desert ("cold"):** No TILs; no PD-L1 → poor ICI response; need to combine with tumour-priming strategies

### Recent Advances in Immunotherapy in HNSCC

#### 1. Next-generation Checkpoint Combinations
- **LAG-3 + PD-1:** Relatlimab + nivolumab approved in melanoma (RELATIVITY-047); phase II trials in HNSCC
- **TIM-3 + PD-1:** Sabatolimab + spartalizumab; early-phase HNSCC trials
- **TIGIT + PD-1/PD-L1:** Tiragolumab + atezolizumab (CITYSCAPE trial); vibostolimab + pembrolizumab (HNSCC trials)

#### 2. Tumour-Infiltrating Lymphocyte (TIL) Therapy
- Harvest TILs from surgical specimen; ex vivo expansion; high-dose lymphodepletion + TIL infusion
- Early-phase trials in HNSCC post-ICI failure; HNSCC-specific TIL products in development

#### 3. HPV-Specific Therapeutic Vaccines + ICI
- ISA101 (HPV16 SLP vaccine) + pembrolizumab: phase II trial in HPV+ R/M HNSCC; ORR ~33% (Massarelli et al., 2019) — significantly higher than monotherapy
- VGX-3100 DNA vaccine against HPV16/18 E6/E7 + pembrolizumab: ongoing trials
- **Rationale:** Vaccine primes HPV-specific T cells; ICI blocks their exhaustion in the TME

#### 4. Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs)
- **Tisotumab vedotin** (anti-tissue factor): active in R/M HNSCC post-ICI; innovaTV 301 trial
- **Trastuzumab deruxtecan** (HER2+): salivary gland cancers and selected HNSCC with HER2 overexpression
- **Patritumab deruxtecan** (HER3+): active in EGFR/HER3+ HNSCC; phase II data promising

#### 5. Bispecific Antibodies
- Simultaneous blockade of two checkpoints or checkpoint + tumour antigen with one molecule
- PD-1×CTLA-4, PD-L1×LAG-3 bispecifics in early HNSCC trials

### Immunotherapy in Primary vs Recurrent HNSCC — Summary

| Setting | Role | Evidence Level |
|---|---|---|
| R/M HNSCC (1st line) | **Standard of care** (pembrolizumab ± chemo) | Level I (KEYNOTE-048) |
| R/M HNSCC (2nd line) | **Standard of care** (nivolumab or pembrolizumab) | Level I (CheckMate 141, KEYNOTE-040) |
| LA-HNSCC definitive CRT | **Investigational** — NOT standard | Phase III negative (KEYNOTE-412) |
| LA-HNSCC adjuvant post-CRT | **Investigational** | IMvoke010 negative; KEYNOTE-689 pending |
| LA-HNSCC neoadjuvant | **Investigational** | Phase II data only |
| Primary resectable (neoadjuvant) | **Investigational** | KEYNOTE-689, etc. — ongoing |

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Immunotherapy with anti-PD-1 checkpoint inhibitors has become the standard of care for recurrent/metastatic HNSCC. In first-line R/M HNSCC, pembrolizumab monotherapy (CPS ≥1) and pembrolizumab + platinum/5-FU (all-comers) are superior to the EXTREME regimen (KEYNOTE-048). In second-line platinum-refractory disease, both nivolumab (CheckMate 141) and pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-040) are approved. PD-L1 CPS is the key predictive biomarker — CPS ≥20 confers greatest OS benefit (14.9 months with pembrolizumab vs 10.7 with EXTREME). HNSCC is immunotherapy-responsive due to high PD-L1 expression, inflamed TME (especially HPV+), and high TMB in tobacco-driven cancers. Integration into curative CRT (KEYNOTE-412) has failed to meet primary endpoints. Recent advances include next-gen checkpoint combinations (LAG-3+PD-1), HPV vaccines + pembrolizumab, ADCs, and TIL therapy.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. The EXTREME regimen (cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU) was first-line standard for 10 years (2008–2019) until KEYNOTE-048 replaced it with pembrolizumab-based therapy.
> 2. CPS ≥20 patients should receive pembrolizumab monotherapy 1st-line — avoiding chemotherapy entirely; this is the highest-benefit, lowest-toxicity strategy.
> 3. CPS 0 patients still benefit from immunotherapy — pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU (combination is approved for all-comers regardless of CPS).
> 4. The "tail of the curve" = long-term survivors on immunotherapy (~17% 2-year OS with nivolumab vs ~6% with cytotoxic); this justifies tolerance of treatment even after initial apparent progression (pseudoprogression).
> 5. KEYNOTE-412 was negative — this is the most important thing to know about immunotherapy in the curative setting as of 2024.
> 6. ISA101 vaccine + pembrolizumab in HPV+ HNSCC achieved ~33% ORR (Massarelli 2019) — higher than pembrolizumab alone, supporting the strategy of priming T cells with vaccines then releasing the brake with ICI.
> 7. "Hot" (inflamed) vs "cold" (desert) tumours predict ICI response — TGF-β exclusion of TILs is the main barrier in excluded/cold tumours; TGF-β pathway inhibitors are being tested as partners to anti-PD-1.
> 8. TMB-H is more relevant in tobacco-driven HNSCC (high mutation load) than HPV+ HNSCC (which has lower TMB but high TIL density — two different immunogenic mechanisms).
> 9. HER2+ salivary gland cancers respond to trastuzumab deruxtecan — HNSCC immunotherapy is now extending beyond squamous cell carcinoma to include specific non-SCC subtypes.
> 10. HNSCC is one of the tumour types that led to FDA pan-tumour approvals of pembrolizumab for TMB-H and MSI-H/dMMR — any HNSCC with these features is eligible for pembrolizumab regardless of site.
