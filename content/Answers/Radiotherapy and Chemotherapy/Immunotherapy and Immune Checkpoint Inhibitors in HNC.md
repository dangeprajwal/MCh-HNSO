> [!cite] Landmark Articles
>
> The approval of immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) has fundamentally altered the treatment landscape for recurrent and metastatic head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC). Ferris et al. (2016) published the CheckMate 141 phase III trial in the New England Journal of Medicine, randomising 361 patients with platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC to nivolumab versus investigator's choice (methotrexate, docetaxel, or cetuximab). Nivolumab demonstrated a significantly longer median overall survival (7.5 months versus 5.1 months; HR 0.70; p = 0.01) and a 1-year OS rate of 36% versus 17%, with a markedly superior toxicity profile (grade 3–4 adverse events 13% versus 35%). This established nivolumab as the first FDA-approved immunotherapy for platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC.
>
> Burtness et al. (2019) reported the KEYNOTE-048 phase III trial in The Lancet, comparing pembrolizumab monotherapy and pembrolizumab plus platinum/5-FU against the EXTREME regimen (cetuximab plus platinum/5-FU) as first-line treatment in 882 patients with R/M HNSCC. Pembrolizumab monotherapy demonstrated superior OS versus EXTREME in PD-L1 CPS ≥1 populations (median OS 12.3 vs 10.3 months; HR 0.78) and CPS ≥20 populations (median OS 14.9 vs 10.7 months; HR 0.61). Pembrolizumab plus chemotherapy was superior to EXTREME in CPS ≥1 (HR 0.65) and all-comers (HR 0.73). These results established pembrolizumab-based regimens as the new first-line standard for R/M HNSCC, supplanting the EXTREME regimen.
>
> Cohen et al. (2019) reported the KEYNOTE-040 phase III trial in The Lancet, demonstrating that pembrolizumab significantly improved OS versus investigator's choice in previously platinum-treated R/M HNSCC (median OS 8.4 vs 6.9 months; HR 0.80; p = 0.016), with markedly lower rates of grade 3–4 adverse events (13% vs 36%). The benefit was amplified in patients with higher PD-L1 CPS, reinforcing PD-L1 CPS as the primary predictive biomarker for pembrolizumab in HNSCC.

## Immunotherapy and Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors in HNSCC

### Tumour Immune Evasion in HNSCC

Head and neck squamous cell carcinoma employs multiple mechanisms to evade immune destruction. The tumour microenvironment (TME) in HNSCC is characterised by:

- **Upregulation of immune checkpoint molecules:** PD-L1, PD-L2, CTLA-4, TIM-3, LAG-3
- **Recruitment of immunosuppressive cells:** regulatory T cells (Tregs), myeloid-derived suppressor cells (MDSCs), tumour-associated macrophages (TAMs, M2 polarised)
- **T-cell exhaustion:** chronic antigen exposure leads to functional exhaustion of tumour-infiltrating lymphocytes (TILs), marked by co-expression of PD-1, TIM-3, LAG-3, and TIGIT
- **Downregulation of MHC class I:** reduces antigen presentation to CD8+ cytotoxic T lymphocytes
- **Secretion of immunosuppressive cytokines:** TGF-β, IL-10, VEGF promote an immunosuppressive milieu

### Concept of Immune Gatekeepers

The term **"immune gatekeepers"** refers to checkpoint molecules that regulate and limit immune activation to prevent autoimmunity under normal physiological conditions. In cancer, tumour cells exploit these checkpoints to evade immune destruction. The key concept is that blocking these molecules — using monoclonal antibodies — **"releases the brake"** on T-cell activation and restores anti-tumour immunity.

The principal immune gatekeepers relevant to HNSCC are:

**1. PD-1 / PD-L1 Axis**
- **PD-1** (Programmed Death-1; CD279) is expressed on activated CD8+ and CD4+ T cells, NK cells, and B cells
- **PD-L1** (CD274, B7-H1) is expressed on tumour cells, tumour-associated macrophages, and dendritic cells; upregulated by IFN-γ (adaptive resistance mechanism)
- **PD-L2** (CD273, B7-DC) is expressed primarily on dendritic cells and macrophages
- PD-1 binding to PD-L1/PD-L2 → inhibition of TCR signalling (SHP-2 phosphatase activation) → T-cell exhaustion, anergy, and apoptosis
- IFN-γ released by tumour-infiltrating T cells paradoxically upregulates PD-L1 on tumour cells, constituting "adaptive immune resistance"
- In HNSCC: PD-L1 is expressed in 50–80% of tumours; HPV+ tumours tend to have higher immune infiltration

**2. CTLA-4 (Cytotoxic T-Lymphocyte Antigen-4; CD152)**
- Expressed on activated T cells and Tregs; upregulated after T-cell activation
- Competes with co-stimulatory CD28 for binding to B7 ligands (CD80/CD86) on antigen-presenting cells (APCs)
- CTLA-4 has higher affinity for B7 than CD28 → outcompetes CD28 → inhibits early T-cell activation in the lymph node
- CTLA-4 on Tregs enhances Treg-mediated immunosuppression
- Ipilimumab (anti-CTLA-4) blocks this axis; combined with PD-1 blockade produces synergistic but more toxic immunoactivation

**3. Other Checkpoints (Emerging Targets)**
- **TIM-3** (T-cell Immunoglobulin and Mucin domain-3): expressed on exhausted CD8+ T cells and Tregs; ligands include galectin-9, HMGB1; marks the most exhausted T-cell phenotype; co-blockade with PD-1 under investigation
- **LAG-3** (Lymphocyte Activation Gene-3; CD223): expressed on activated and exhausted T cells; binds MHC class II; relatlimab (anti-LAG-3) + nivolumab approved for melanoma (RELATIVITY-047); trials in HNSCC ongoing
- **TIGIT** (T-cell immunoreceptor with Ig and ITIM domains): binds CD155/CD112; competes with co-stimulatory CD226; associated with T-cell exhaustion in HNSCC
- **CD47** ("don't-eat-me signal"): expressed on tumour cells; engages SIRPα on macrophages to prevent phagocytosis; anti-CD47 antibodies in early trials

### FDA-Approved Immunotherapy Agents in HNSCC

#### Nivolumab (Opdivo) — anti-PD-1 IgG4 monoclonal antibody
- **CheckMate 141 (2016):** Phase III, platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC
  - Nivolumab vs investigator's choice (methotrexate / docetaxel / cetuximab)
  - Median OS: **7.5 vs 5.1 months** (HR 0.70; p = 0.01)
  - 1-year OS: **36% vs 17%**
  - Grade 3–4 AEs: 13% vs 35%
  - Response rate: 13.3% vs 5.8%
  - Benefit seen regardless of PD-L1 expression, although higher expressors benefited more
- **FDA approval:** November 2016 — 2nd-line R/M HNSCC after platinum failure
- **Dosing:** 240 mg IV q2w or 480 mg IV q4w

#### Pembrolizumab (Keytruda) — anti-PD-1 IgG4 humanised monoclonal antibody
- **KEYNOTE-040 (2019):** Phase III, platinum-refractory R/M HNSCC
  - Pembrolizumab vs investigator's choice
  - Median OS: 8.4 vs 6.9 months (HR 0.80; p = 0.016)
  - CPS ≥1: HR 0.74; CPS ≥20: HR 0.53
  - Grade 3–4 AEs: 13% vs 36%
  - **FDA approval (2nd line):** August 2019

- **KEYNOTE-048 (2019):** Phase III, 1st-line R/M HNSCC (3 arms)
  - Arm A: Pembrolizumab monotherapy
  - Arm B: Pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU
  - Arm C (control): EXTREME (cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU)

  | Endpoint | Pembrolizumab mono (CPS ≥1) | Pembrolizumab mono (CPS ≥20) | Pembrolizumab + chemo (all-comers) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Median OS vs EXTREME | 12.3 vs 10.3 months | 14.9 vs 10.7 months | 13.0 vs 10.7 months |
  | Hazard Ratio | 0.78 | 0.61 | 0.73 |
  | PFS | Similar | Similar | Improved |

  - **FDA approval (1st line):** June 2019
    - Pembrolizumab monotherapy for CPS ≥1 R/M HNSCC
    - Pembrolizumab + platinum + 5-FU for all-comers R/M HNSCC
  - **Dosing:** 200 mg IV q3w or 400 mg IV q6w

### Predictive Biomarkers for Immunotherapy

| Biomarker | Assay | Clinical Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **PD-L1 CPS** (Combined Positive Score) | IHC (22C3 PharmDx) | CPS ≥1: pembrolizumab 1st-line mono eligible; CPS ≥20: greatest OS benefit |
| **PD-L1 TPS** (Tumour Proportion Score) | IHC | Less discriminatory than CPS in HNSCC |
| **TMB-H** (≥10 mut/Mb) | NGS | FDA pan-tumour approval for pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-158); HNSCC has moderate-high TMB |
| **MSI-H / dMMR** | IHC / PCR | FDA pan-tumour pembrolizumab approval; rare in HNSCC |
| **HPV/p16 status** | p16 IHC / HPV ISH | HPV+ HNSCC: higher TIL density; PD-L1 response less clear; immunotherapy active |
| **IFN-γ gene signature** | RNA expression | Investigational; correlates with response to anti-PD-1 |
| **LAG-3 expression** | IHC | Investigational; co-expression with PD-1 = exhausted phenotype |

**CPS calculation:** (PD-L1+ tumour cells + PD-L1+ lymphocytes + PD-L1+ macrophages) / total viable tumour cells × 100

### Combination Strategies

**Pembrolizumab + Platinum + 5-FU (KEYNOTE-048):** Current standard 1st-line for most patients with R/M HNSCC regardless of CPS. Superior OS and PFS versus EXTREME in CPS ≥1 and all-comer populations. Grade 3–4 AE rate 85% (higher than monotherapy due to chemotherapy toxicity).

**Nivolumab + Ipilimumab (CheckMate 651):** Phase III, 1st-line R/M HNSCC; primary endpoint (OS in CPS ≥20) was not met; combination not approved in HNSCC for 1st-line use.

**Durvalumab ± Tremelimumab:** CONDOR trial (3rd line) — durvalumab + tremelimumab vs monotherapy; modest ORR improvements without clear OS benefit. EAGLE trial (2nd line or later) — negative for OS; no approval.

### Immunotherapy in the Curative Setting (LA-HNSCC)

Integration of ICIs with concurrent chemoradiation (CRT) in locally advanced HNSCC remains investigational:

- **KEYNOTE-412:** Pembrolizumab + CRT vs CRT alone for LA-HNSCC; primary endpoint (EFS) not met at interim analysis (2023); integration of pembrolizumab into curative CRT not currently standard
- **JAVELIN Head and Neck 100** (avelumab + CRT): Negative trial
- **INDUCE-3** (pembrolizumab + CRT), **KEYNOTE-689** (perioperative pembrolizumab in resectable HNSCC): ongoing; results awaited

### Immune-Related Adverse Events (irAEs)

ICIs can cause inflammatory toxicity in any organ system. Mechanism: loss of self-tolerance; immune activation beyond the tumour.

**Incidence in HNSCC trials:**
- Pembrolizumab: any-grade irAEs ~60–70%; grade 3–4 irAEs ~15%
- Nivolumab: any-grade ~55%; grade 3–4 ~13%

**Common irAEs by organ:**

| System | irAE | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Skin | Rash, pruritus, vitiligo | Topical/oral steroids; rarely discontinue |
| Endocrine | Hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, adrenal insufficiency, hypophysitis | Hormone replacement; steroids for hypophysitis |
| GI | Diarrhoea/colitis | Hold ICI; prednisone 1–2 mg/kg; infliximab for steroid-refractory |
| Pulmonary | Pneumonitis | Hold ICI; methylprednisolone 1–2 mg/kg IV; consider bronchoscopy |
| Hepatic | Hepatitis (ALT/AST elevation) | Hold ICI; prednisone; mycophenolate for steroid-refractory |
| Cardiac | Myocarditis (rare, ~0.1%, but fatal) | Permanent discontinuation; high-dose steroids; cardiac monitoring |
| Renal | Nephritis | Hold ICI; prednisone |
| Musculoskeletal | Arthritis, myositis | NSAIDs; steroids |

**Grading and action:**
- Grade 1: continue ICI with monitoring
- Grade 2: hold ICI; oral prednisone 0.5–1 mg/kg; resume when ≤Grade 1
- Grade 3: hold ICI; prednisone 1–2 mg/kg; consider permanent discontinuation
- Grade 4: **permanently discontinue** ICI; high-dose IV steroids

**Key principle:** Do not use immunosuppressants prophylactically; irAEs are not dose-related but can occur at any time, including months after stopping ICI.

### Future Directions

- **LAG-3 blockade:** Relatlimab + nivolumab (RELATIVITY-047) approved in melanoma; phase II/III trials in HNSCC
- **TIM-3 inhibitors:** Sabatolimab (MBG453), cobolimab — in combination with PD-1 blockade
- **Bispecific antibodies:** Simultaneous blockade of PD-1 and LAG-3 or TIM-3 with a single molecule
- **CAR-T therapy in HNSCC:** EGFRvIII-targeting, EGFR-targeting CAR-T — early phase trials; limited by solid tumour microenvironment
- **Tumour vaccines + ICI:** HPV-specific therapeutic vaccines + pembrolizumab (ISA101 + pembrolizumab — promising phase II data in HPV+ HNSCC)
- **Antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs):** Tisotumab vedotin (anti-tissue factor), trastuzumab deruxtecan (HER2+ salivary gland) — active in subsets

> [!example] Examiner's Summary
> Immune checkpoint inhibitors have established a new standard of care in recurrent/metastatic HNSCC. Pembrolizumab (anti-PD-1) is first-line therapy for R/M HNSCC — as monotherapy for CPS ≥1 and in combination with platinum/5-FU for all-comers — based on KEYNOTE-048. Nivolumab (anti-PD-1) is second-line after platinum failure, based on CheckMate 141 (OS 7.5 vs 5.1 months). PD-L1 CPS (Combined Positive Score) is the primary predictive biomarker; CPS ≥20 confers the greatest benefit from pembrolizumab monotherapy. Immune gatekeepers (PD-1/PD-L1, CTLA-4, TIM-3, LAG-3) are checkpoint molecules that physiologically prevent autoimmunity; tumours exploit them to evade immunity — blocking them "releases the T-cell brake." Integration of ICIs into curative CRT remains investigational; irAEs require organ-specific management with corticosteroids.

> [!tip] Clinical Pearls
> 1. PD-L1 CPS ≥1: pembrolizumab monotherapy 1st line is superior to EXTREME; CPS ≥20 confers the greatest OS benefit (median OS 14.9 months).
> 2. Nivolumab benefit in CheckMate 141 was seen regardless of PD-L1 expression — useful when biomarker testing is unavailable.
> 3. Cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU (EXTREME) remains an option when pembrolizumab is unavailable or for CPS 0 patients not eligible for pembrolizumab monotherapy.
> 4. KEYNOTE-048 proved that adding pembrolizumab to chemotherapy is superior to adding cetuximab to the same chemotherapy backbone (EXTREME).
> 5. Immune gatekeepers concept: CTLA-4 regulates early activation in the lymph node (central tolerance); PD-1 regulates effector function in the tumour (peripheral tolerance) — they act at different stages.
> 6. IFN-γ released by TILs paradoxically upregulates PD-L1 on tumour cells — this "adaptive immune resistance" is why immunotherapy can be self-limiting without sustained ICI therapy.
> 7. Grade 4 irAEs require permanent discontinuation; Grade 3 irAEs require high-dose prednisone (1–2 mg/kg) and ICI hold.
> 8. KEYNOTE-412 (pembrolizumab + CRT for LA-HNSCC) did not meet its primary EFS endpoint — pembrolizumab is NOT yet standard in the curative setting.
> 9. TMB-H (≥10 mut/Mb) is an FDA-approved pan-tumour biomarker for pembrolizumab; HPV+ HNSCC typically has lower TMB than HPV-negative.
> 10. Nivolumab + ipilimumab (CheckMate 651) did NOT improve 1st-line OS in R/M HNSCC — dual checkpoint blockade is not standard in HNSCC unlike melanoma.
