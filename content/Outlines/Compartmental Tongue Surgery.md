This includes the removal of 
	- The primary lesion 
	- The pathway of spread including muscular, neurovascular, and lymphatic 
	- Regions having higher chances of metastasis within the compartment which acts as a bridge between the primary lesion and the cervical neck nodes - muscular (mylohyoid), glandular space (sublingual and submandibular), and the neurovascular bundle (hypoglossal nerve, lingual nerve, and vessels) [[Calabrese-2011]]

The reason for adopting this compartment tongue resection is because of the pattern of spread of carcinoma of the tongue, which spreads inferiorly and posteriorly where the nerves and the vessels run parallel to the muscle fibers, therefore the tumor spreads easily along this pathway.

**Boundary of the compartment of the tongue**

> The lingual septum is the medial margin separating adjacent compartments in each hemi-tongue. The periosteum along the lingual surface of the mandible constitutes the lateral and anterior margins. The stylohyoid muscle and ligament form the posterior boundary. The mylohyoid muscle forms the floor of the compartment along its entire length. The compartments contain the tongue musculature (intrinsic and extrinsic) together with stromal, glandular (sublingual), and neuro-vascular structures (lingual nerve, artery and vein, terminal portion of hypoglossal nerve).
