## Landmark Articles
- Surgical margins in head and neck cancer: A contemporary review - [[Hinni-2013]] - [DOI](10.1002/hed.23110)
- Impact of Close Margins in Head and Neck Mucosal Squamous Cell Carcinoma: A Systematic Review - [[Hamman-2022]]- [DOI](10.1002/lary.29690)


>[!important]
> For overall survival at 5 years, patients with clear surgical margins had survival rates ranging from about 53% up to 91%. Those with close margins had lower survival, between roughly 38% and 70%. Patients with involved margins showed the poorest outcomes overall, with survival ranging from about 30% to 85%.
> For local recurrence at 5 years, the lowest recurrence was seen in patients with clear margins, ranging between about 5% and 27%. Recurrence was higher in patients with close margins, ranging from about 11% to 36%. The highest recurrence rates were observed in patients with involved margins, which ranged widely from about 9% up to nearly 80%. [[Hamman-2022]]
